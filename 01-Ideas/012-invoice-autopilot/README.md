# 12. Invoice Autopilot

> Smart invoicing that chases payments so freelancers and agencies don't have to

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Category** | SMB Tools / B2B SaaS |
| **Target Audience** | Freelancers, agencies, and small service businesses (1-20 people) |
| **Monetization** | Freemium ($0 for 5 invoices/mo, $19-49/mo for unlimited + automation) |
| **Recession Resistance** | High - invoicing is mandatory regardless of economy; late payments increase during downturns making the tool more valuable |
| **Build Effort** | Low - 6-8 weeks MVP |

## Problem

Freelancers spend 15+ hours/month on invoicing admin (AND CO 2024). 29% of invoices to small businesses are paid late (Fundbox data). The average freelancer is owed $6,000 in unpaid invoices at any given time. Existing tools (FreshBooks, QuickBooks) are bloated — freelancers just want to send invoices and get paid.

**Reddit signal**: r/freelance and r/Entrepreneur threads consistently complain about "chasing payments" being the #1 time drain. Top request: automated polite follow-ups that don't damage client relationships.

## Solution

One-click invoice creation from templates. AI-timed follow-up sequences (polite escalation). Client payment behavior scoring. Instant bank deposit via Stripe. Late fee auto-calculation. "Pay now" button with Apple Pay / Google Pay.

## Market Size

$4.5B invoicing software market globally; freelancer/SMB segment is $1.8B (Allied Market Research).

## Revenue Potential

10,000 users at $29/mo average = $3.5M ARR. Transaction fees on payments add 15-20% on top.

## Existing Competitors

FreshBooks, Wave, Bonsai, HoneyBook, Invoice Ninja. All either too complex or lack smart follow-up automation.

## Competitive Moat

Payment behavior ML model improves with scale. Integration simplicity (no accounting degree needed). Tone-aware follow-up sequences are hard to replicate well.
