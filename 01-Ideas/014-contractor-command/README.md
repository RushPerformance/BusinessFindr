# 14. Contractor Command

> All-in-one 1099 contractor management: onboarding, payments, compliance, and tax forms

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Category** | B2B SaaS / Compliance |
| **Target Audience** | Businesses hiring 5-100 independent contractors (agencies, construction, consulting, gig platforms) |
| **Monetization** | Per-contractor pricing ($7-12/contractor/mo) |
| **Recession Resistance** | High - contractor usage increases during recessions as companies avoid full-time hires |
| **Build Effort** | Medium - 12-16 weeks MVP |

## Problem

73% of companies plan to increase contractor usage (Staffing Industry Analysts 2024). IRS misclassification penalties are $50+ per W-2 that should have been filed, plus back taxes and benefits. The DOL and IRS have increased audits on worker classification by 40% since 2023. Small businesses using contractors have no single tool for onboarding, payments, compliance checks, and 1099 filing.

**Reddit signal**: r/Entrepreneur and r/startups threads show founders confused about contractor vs. employee rules, panicked about AB5-style laws, and frustrated that Gusto/Deel are priced for enterprise.

## Solution

Self-serve contractor onboarding portal (W-9 collection, background checks). Worker classification risk quiz with legal guidance. Automated payments with 1099-NEC filing. Contract template library. Compliance alerts for state-specific laws.

## Market Size

$5.4B contractor management platform market (Mordor Intelligence), growing 15% CAGR. SMB segment underserved.

## Revenue Potential

1,500 businesses x 15 contractors x $9/mo = $2.4M ARR. Transaction fees on payments add revenue.

## Existing Competitors

Deel, Gusto, Worksuite, Fiverr Enterprise. All priced for mid-market or enterprise; SMBs paying $500+/mo is common complaint.

## Competitive Moat

SMB-specific pricing and simplicity. State-by-state classification rules database. Integration with popular SMB accounting tools (QuickBooks, Xero).
