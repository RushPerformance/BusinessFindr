# 15. Stockroom IQ

> Simple inventory management for small retailers and e-commerce sellers that predicts reorder points

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Category** | SMB Tools / B2B SaaS |
| **Target Audience** | Small retailers, Shopify/Etsy sellers, and light manufacturers (1-50 SKUs to start) |
| **Monetization** | Tiered subscription ($19/mo for 50 SKUs, $49/mo for 500, $99/mo for unlimited) |
| **Recession Resistance** | Medium - retail is cyclical, but inventory management becomes more critical when margins tighten |
| **Build Effort** | Medium - 8-12 weeks MVP |

## Problem

43% of small businesses don't track inventory at all or use manual methods (Wasp Barcode 2024). Stockouts cost US retailers $1 trillion/year collectively. Overstocking ties up 20-30% of working capital for typical small retailers. Small sellers on Shopify/Etsy report losing 10-15% of revenue to stockouts or over-ordering.

**Reddit signal**: r/smallbusiness and r/ecommerce threads regularly ask for "something simpler than Cin7 or Fishbowl." Pain point: existing tools require QuickBooks integration to work and are overly complex for a 2-person shop.

## Solution

Barcode scanning via phone camera. Real-time stock levels across sales channels (Shopify, Etsy, Amazon, POS). AI-predicted reorder points based on sales velocity. Low-stock alerts via SMS. Dead stock identification. Simple purchase order generation.

## Market Size

$1.8B SMB inventory management market (Fortune Business Insights). Growing 10% annually as e-commerce continues expanding.

## Revenue Potential

8,000 sellers at $39/mo average = $3.7M ARR.

## Existing Competitors

Cin7, Fishbowl, SOS Inventory, Sortly. Common complaints: too complex for small operations, expensive ($100-300+/mo), require accounting integration to function.

## Competitive Moat

Simplicity and mobile-first design. AI reorder predictions improve with data. Multi-channel sync is table stakes but hard to do reliably.
