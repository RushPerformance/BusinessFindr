# 17. TinyCRM

> A CRM for businesses that hate CRMs: simple contact + deal tracking without the Salesforce bloat

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Category** | SMB Tools / B2B SaaS |
| **Target Audience** | Solopreneurs, freelancers, consultants, and small agencies (1-10 people) |
| **Monetization** | Freemium ($0 for 100 contacts, $15-29/mo for unlimited + automation) |
| **Recession Resistance** | High - sales pipeline management becomes more important when deals are harder to close |
| **Build Effort** | Low - 6-8 weeks MVP |

## Problem

65% of CRM implementations fail due to complexity (Forrester). Small business owners report spending 30 minutes/day on CRM data entry they resent. HubSpot's free tier is a funnel to $800+/mo paid plans. Salesforce starts at $25/user/mo but realistically costs $75+/user/mo to be useful. Most 1-5 person shops end up back in spreadsheets.

**Reddit signal**: r/smallbusiness and r/Entrepreneur CRM threads are a graveyard of frustration. Recurring theme: "I just want to remember who I talked to and when to follow up." Top requested features: email sync, simple deal pipeline, mobile app, and NOT being upsold.

## Solution

Add contacts via business card scan, email, or manual entry. Simple Kanban deal pipeline. Automatic follow-up reminders. Gmail/Outlook sync. One-tap call/email logging. No dashboards, no reports nobody reads, no "custom objects."

## Market Size

$58B global CRM market (Gartner), but $5.2B specifically attributed to micro-business/SMB segment (Nucleus Research).

## Revenue Potential

20,000 users at $19/mo average = $4.6M ARR. High volume, low price — the anti-Salesforce model.

## Existing Competitors

HubSpot (free tier = upsell funnel), Pipedrive ($14+/user/mo), Less Annoying CRM ($15/user/mo — closest competitor, profitable, solo-dev origin story). Folk, Attio (design-forward but still complex).

## Competitive Moat

Radical simplicity is the moat. Refusing to add features is harder than adding them. Less Annoying CRM proved this works ($3M+ ARR, bootstrapped, one founder for years).
