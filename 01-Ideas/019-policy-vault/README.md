# 19. Policy Vault

> Generate, store, and track employee acknowledgment of HR policies — handbook compliance made simple

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Category** | Compliance / B2B SaaS |
| **Target Audience** | SMBs with 10-100 employees who lack an HR department |
| **Monetization** | Subscription ($49-99/mo based on employee count) |
| **Recession Resistance** | High - employment lawsuits increase during recessions (wrongful termination claims rise 15-20%); having documented policies is the #1 defense |
| **Build Effort** | Low - 8-10 weeks MVP |

## Problem

53% of businesses with under 50 employees have no employee handbook (SHRM 2024). The average employment lawsuit settlement is $40,000 — the average defense cost is $75,000 even when the employer wins. Without documented policies and signed acknowledgments, employers have almost no legal defense. Creating an employee handbook from scratch costs $3,000-10,000 with an attorney.

**Reddit signal**: r/smallbusiness regularly surfaces panic posts: "Employee is suing me and I have no handbook." r/humanresources shows HR pros frustrated that SMB clients resist spending on policy infrastructure.

## Solution

Industry-specific handbook templates (state-adjusted). AI customization wizard. Digital employee acknowledgment with timestamps. Policy update versioning and re-acknowledgment tracking. Annual review reminders. Integration with onboarding tools.

## Market Size

$1.9B HR compliance software market for SMBs (Verified Market Research). Employee handbook segment: ~$400M.

## Revenue Potential

4,000 businesses at $69/mo average = $3.3M ARR. Attorney-reviewed custom policies as premium upsell ($500-2,000 one-time).

## Existing Competitors

Blissbook, AirMason, SixFifty (for policy generation). Most are either policy creation OR acknowledgment tracking, rarely both. No dominant SMB player.

## Competitive Moat

State-specific legal content library that stays updated. Acknowledgment audit trail becomes increasingly valuable and hard to migrate away from. Templates improve from usage patterns across industries.
