# 20. Proposal Forge

> AI-generated business proposals and SOWs that close deals faster for service businesses

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Category** | SMB Tools / B2B SaaS |
| **Target Audience** | Agencies, consultants, contractors, and professional services firms (1-25 people) |
| **Monetization** | Freemium ($0 for 3 proposals/mo, $29-59/mo for unlimited + analytics) |
| **Recession Resistance** | Medium - proposal volume drops in recessions, but win rate optimization becomes more critical |
| **Build Effort** | Low - 6-8 weeks MVP |

## Problem

Service businesses spend 2-5 hours crafting each proposal. Average close rate on proposals is 25-35% (Proposify benchmark data). The faster a proposal goes out, the higher the close rate — proposals sent within 24 hours of initial contact close at 2x the rate of those sent after 5+ days. Most small agencies copy-paste from old proposals in Word docs.

**Reddit signal**: r/Entrepreneur and r/agency threads cite proposal creation as a top bottleneck. "By the time I finish the proposal, the lead has gone cold." Tools like PandaDoc and Proposify are cited as "too expensive for what they do" ($49-65/user/mo).

## Solution

Answer 5 questions about the project scope. AI generates a branded proposal with scope, timeline, deliverables, pricing, and terms. Client-facing tracking (opened, viewed, signed). E-signature built in. Template learning from past proposals. Pricing optimization suggestions.

## Market Size

$3.2B proposal management software market (MarketsandMarkets). SMB/freelancer segment: ~$800M.

## Revenue Potential

8,000 users at $39/mo average = $3.7M ARR.

## Existing Competitors

PandaDoc ($35+/user/mo), Proposify ($49+/user/mo), Better Proposals, Qwilr. All priced for teams, not solopreneurs. None use AI to generate proposal content.

## Competitive Moat

AI content generation trained on winning proposals by industry. Proposal analytics (what sections clients spend time on) create feedback loops. Low price point undercuts incumbents.
