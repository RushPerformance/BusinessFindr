# 22. Permit Tracker

> Track and renew business permits, licenses, and certifications before they expire

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Category** | Compliance / SMB Tools |
| **Target Audience** | Contractors (HVAC, plumbing, electrical), restaurants, healthcare providers, real estate agents |
| **Monetization** | Subscription ($19-49/mo per business) |
| **Recession Resistance** | High - permits and licenses are legally required regardless of economic conditions; operating without them carries criminal penalties |
| **Build Effort** | Low - 6-8 weeks MVP |

## Problem

The average small business needs 5-12 permits and licenses across federal, state, and local jurisdictions. 1 in 4 small businesses has operated with an expired permit unknowingly (SBA data). Fines range from $500 to $10,000+ per violation. Contractors are especially vulnerable — an expired license can void insurance coverage and make completed work legally contestable.

**Reddit signal**: r/smallbusiness and r/Construction show regular posts about forgotten license renewals causing project shutdowns, lost bids, and insurance claim denials. "I almost lost a $200K contract because my state license lapsed by 2 weeks."

## Solution

Input business type and location. System identifies all required permits/licenses. Manual or photo upload of current documents. Automated renewal reminders at 90/60/30/7 days. Direct links to renewal portals. Shared dashboard for businesses with multiple employees who hold individual licenses.

## Market Size

$800M permit and license management segment within the $12.3B regulatory compliance market. No dominant SMB player.

## Revenue Potential

10,000 businesses at $29/mo = $3.5M ARR. Add-on: renewal concierge service (staff handles the renewal paperwork) at $50-200 per renewal.

## Existing Competitors

LicenseLogix (enterprise), CT Corporation (enterprise), manual spreadsheets and calendar reminders for most SMBs. This is a genuinely underserved niche.

## Competitive Moat

Permit/license database by jurisdiction is an enormous data asset. Reminder history and compliance records create switching costs. Network effects: businesses can require contractors to share permit status via the platform.
