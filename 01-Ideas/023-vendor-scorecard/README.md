# 23. Vendor Scorecard

> Rate and compare vendors based on real delivery, quality, and pricing data from your own purchase history

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Category** | B2B SaaS / SMB Tools |
| **Target Audience** | Small manufacturers, restaurants, retailers, and construction companies managing 5-50 vendor relationships |
| **Monetization** | Subscription ($39-79/mo) |
| **Recession Resistance** | Medium - vendor management matters more during tight margins, but overall procurement budgets shrink |
| **Build Effort** | Medium - 8-10 weeks MVP |

## Problem

Small businesses waste 15-20% on vendor costs due to lack of systematic comparison (Spend Matters research). The average SMB uses 25+ vendors but has no structured way to track on-time delivery rates, quality issues, or price changes over time. When a vendor raises prices 5%, most SMBs don't notice for months.

**Reddit signal**: r/smallbusiness and r/supplychain threads show owners frustrated by vendor price creep, inconsistent delivery, and having no leverage in negotiations because they lack data. "My food distributor raised prices 12% over 6 months and I only caught it when I finally compared old invoices."

## Solution

Import invoices (photo scan, CSV, or email forwarding). Auto-track price changes per item per vendor. Score vendors on price competitiveness, delivery reliability, and quality (user ratings). Alert on unusual price increases. Comparison reports for renegotiation leverage.

## Market Size

$7.2B procurement software market (Gartner). SMB segment (~$1.5B) is served by enterprise-designed tools that are way too complex.

## Revenue Potential

3,000 businesses at $49/mo average = $1.8M ARR. Vendor marketplace referral fees as expansion revenue.

## Existing Competitors

SAP Ariba, Coupa, Procurify — all enterprise ($500+/mo minimum). No SMB-native vendor management tool with real traction.

## Competitive Moat

Invoice data accumulates into a powerful negotiation asset. Over time, aggregate anonymized pricing data across the platform creates benchmarks (like Glassdoor for vendor pricing). This is extremely defensible.
