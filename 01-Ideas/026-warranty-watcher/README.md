# 26. Warranty Watcher

> Track warranties and service contracts for business equipment — never miss a claim or expiration

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Category** | SMB Tools / B2B SaaS |
| **Target Audience** | Small businesses with significant equipment (restaurants, clinics, gyms, construction, manufacturing) |
| **Monetization** | Freemium ($0 for 10 items, $19-39/mo for unlimited + alerts) |
| **Recession Resistance** | High - during recessions, businesses extend equipment life rather than replace; warranty claims become critical for avoiding unexpected repair costs |
| **Build Effort** | Low - 4-6 weeks MVP |

## Problem

SMBs lose an estimated $12,000/year in unclaimed warranties (Warranty Direct data). 68% of businesses can't locate warranty documents when needed. The average commercial kitchen has $150K+ in equipment with staggered warranty periods. When a $3,000 oven breaks and the owner can't find the warranty, they pay out of pocket for a repair that was covered.

**Reddit signal**: r/smallbusiness and r/restaurateur threads show repeated pain around equipment warranties. "My walk-in cooler compressor died. Turns out the warranty expired 2 months ago. $4,500 repair." Multiple threads asking for "an app that just tracks my warranties."

## Solution

Photo-scan receipts and warranty cards. Auto-extract warranty terms using OCR/AI. Calendar of expirations with tiered alerts (90/60/30 days). Claim filing assistant with required documentation checklists. Service history log. Shareable with maintenance staff/contractors.

## Market Size

Niche within the $2.1B asset management software market. Direct comparable: no dedicated SMB warranty tracking tool exists with significant market share.

## Revenue Potential

8,000 businesses at $24/mo average = $2.3M ARR. Extended warranty marketplace referral fees as expansion.

## Existing Competitors

No direct SMB competitors. Consumer apps (Centriq, OneHome) exist for home warranties. Enterprise asset management (SAP, IBM Maximo) is wildly overbuilt. This is a true whitespace opportunity.

## Competitive Moat

Equipment database and warranty terms library grows with each user. Simple enough concept that execution and data quality are the moats. First mover in a niche with no competition.
