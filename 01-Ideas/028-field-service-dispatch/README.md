# 28. Field Service Dispatch

> Simple job dispatch and tracking for small trade businesses — plumbers, electricians, HVAC, cleaners

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Category** | SMB Tools / B2B SaaS |
| **Target Audience** | Home service businesses with 2-20 field technicians (plumbing, HVAC, electrical, cleaning, pest control) |
| **Monetization** | Per-tech pricing ($15-25/tech/mo) |
| **Recession Resistance** | High - home repair and maintenance are essential services; demand is relatively stable across economic cycles |
| **Build Effort** | Medium - 10-12 weeks MVP |

## Problem

There are 3.7M home service businesses in the US. 70% use manual scheduling (phone/whiteboard/text messages) for dispatch. Average drive time between jobs could be reduced 20-30% with route optimization. Miscommunication between office and field costs an average of 2 hours/day per technician in wasted time. Existing tools (ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro) cost $100-300+/tech/mo.

**Reddit signal**: r/HVAC, r/Plumbing, r/smallbusiness consistently complain about software costs. "ServiceTitan is amazing but costs us $3,000/month for 8 techs." "Housecall Pro keeps adding features I don't need and raising prices." Small operators want: assign job, tech sees it on phone, customer gets ETA, job gets marked complete.

## Solution

Web dashboard for dispatchers. Mobile app for techs (job details, navigation, photos, customer signature). Customer ETA notifications via text. Basic route optimization. Invoicing from the field. No contracts, cancel anytime.

## Market Size

$5.2B field service management market (MarketsandMarkets). SMB segment with under 20 techs: ~$1.5B and underserved.

## Revenue Potential

3,000 businesses x 8 techs x $19/tech/mo = $5.5M ARR.

## Existing Competitors

ServiceTitan ($150+/tech/mo), Housecall Pro ($65+/tech/mo), Jobber ($49+/user/mo), FieldPulse. All trending upmarket and increasing prices.

## Competitive Moat

Price disruption: $19/tech vs. $150/tech is a 7x difference. Simplicity — the "just enough" feature set for a 5-person plumbing company. As incumbents chase enterprise, the bottom of the market is left open.
