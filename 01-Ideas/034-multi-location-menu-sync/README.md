# 34. Multi-Location Menu Sync

> Update menus across Google, Yelp, DoorDash, UberEats, your website, and in-store displays from one dashboard

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Category** | SMB Tools / B2B SaaS |
| **Target Audience** | Restaurants, cafes, bars, and food trucks with presence on 3+ platforms |
| **Monetization** | Subscription ($39-99/mo per location) |
| **Recession Resistance** | Medium - restaurants are cyclical, but delivery/online ordering continues growing through all economic conditions |
| **Build Effort** | Medium - 10-12 weeks MVP (API integrations are the bulk of the work) |

## Problem

The average restaurant has its menu on 5-8 different platforms (own website, Google Business, DoorDash, UberEats, Grubhub, Yelp, Facebook, in-store). Updating a single menu item (price change, 86'd item, new special) requires manual updates across all platforms. This takes 30-60 minutes per change. Inconsistent menus lead to customer complaints and order errors. 33% of negative delivery reviews cite menu inaccuracy (DoorDash internal data).

**Reddit signal**: r/restaurateur constantly mentions menu management as a top pain point. "I changed my prices 3 weeks ago and just realized DoorDash still has the old menu." Multi-location operators call it "menu hell."

## Solution

Single dashboard to manage menu items, descriptions, prices, photos, and availability. Push updates to all connected platforms simultaneously. Schedule menu changes (lunch to dinner, seasonal items). 86 tracking — mark an item unavailable and it disappears everywhere instantly. Photo management with platform-specific cropping.

## Market Size

$1.3B restaurant technology market for menu/ordering management (Technomic). Multi-platform menu sync is an emerging sub-segment.

## Revenue Potential

4,000 locations at $59/mo average = $2.8M ARR.

## Existing Competitors

Otter (by Deliverect, $100+/mo), ItsaCheckmate ($79+/mo), Checkmate — all focused on order aggregation with menu sync as a secondary feature. No tool focused purely on menu management across ALL channels (not just delivery apps).

## Competitive Moat

Platform API integrations are a meaningful technical moat (each integration takes weeks). Including Google Business and website sync (not just delivery platforms) differentiates from order aggregation tools. Network effects: as more restaurants use the tool, platforms may offer preferred integration status.
