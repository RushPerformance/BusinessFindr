# 36. Certificate & License Tracker

> Track employee certifications, training expirations, and continuing education requirements

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Category** | Compliance / B2B SaaS |
| **Target Audience** | Healthcare practices, construction companies, salons, real estate brokerages, accounting firms — any industry with licensed employees |
| **Monetization** | Per-employee pricing ($2-5/employee/mo) or flat $29-79/mo |
| **Recession Resistance** | High - professional licensing requirements are non-negotiable; operating with expired certifications carries legal liability and insurance voidance |
| **Build Effort** | Low - 6-8 weeks MVP |

## Problem

Industries requiring employee certifications (healthcare, construction, cosmetology, real estate, aviation) face constant tracking challenges. A single expired certification can shut down a job site, void malpractice insurance, or trigger regulatory action. The average healthcare practice tracks 8-15 certifications per employee (BLS, ACLS, state licenses, DEA, OSHA, etc.). 1 in 5 businesses has discovered an employee operating with an expired credential (ComplianceHR data).

**Reddit signal**: r/nursing, r/Construction, and r/salons all have threads about certification tracking nightmares. "Our clinic almost lost its accreditation because two nurses had expired BLS certs and nobody noticed." Managers use spreadsheets that inevitably go stale.

## Solution

Employee self-service portal to upload certifications with photos/PDFs. Auto-extract expiration dates via OCR. Manager dashboard showing all employees and certification status (green/yellow/red). Automated renewal reminders to both employee and manager. CE credit tracking against requirements. Compliance reports for auditors/inspectors.

## Market Size

$1.6B credential verification and compliance training market (Verified Market Research). Certification tracking subset: ~$300M.

## Revenue Potential

4,000 businesses x 20 employees x $3/mo = $2.9M ARR.

## Existing Competitors

Certemy ($5+/employee/mo, healthcare-focused), Compliance Bridge, manual spreadsheets. Most solutions are industry-specific (healthcare only, or construction only) — no horizontal play at affordable pricing.

## Competitive Moat

Cross-industry certification database is an asset no competitor has built. Employee-driven data entry reduces admin burden and improves accuracy. Compliance audit trails create switching costs. Horizontal approach means larger addressable market than vertical competitors.
