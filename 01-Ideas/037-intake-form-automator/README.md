# 37. Intake Form Automator

> Smart digital intake forms that pre-fill, validate, and route data into your existing tools

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Category** | SMB Tools / B2B SaaS |
| **Target Audience** | Law firms, medical practices, accounting firms, insurance agencies, and any professional service collecting client information |
| **Monetization** | Subscription ($19-59/mo based on form volume) |
| **Recession Resistance** | Medium - intake volume tracks with client acquisition, which dips in recessions, but operational efficiency savings remain valuable |
| **Build Effort** | Low - 4-6 weeks MVP |

## Problem

Professional service firms still use paper intake forms or basic PDFs that require manual data entry. The average law firm spends 20 minutes per client on intake data entry (Clio Legal Trends Report). Medical practices report 35% error rates in manually entered patient data. Clients find paper/PDF forms frustrating (especially filling out the same info at multiple providers). Existing form tools (Typeform, JotForm) lack industry-specific logic and integrations.

**Reddit signal**: r/LawFirm, r/Accounting, and r/dentistry threads regularly discuss intake friction. "My clients fill out a 3-page paper form, then my assistant re-types everything into our practice management software. It takes 30 minutes per client." Attorneys want forms that auto-populate their case management system.

## Solution

Industry-specific form templates with conditional logic (only show relevant fields based on answers). Client self-service via mobile-friendly link. Auto-fill from previous submissions. Data validation (SSN format, date logic, address verification). Direct push to practice management tools (Clio, Dentrix, QuickBooks). E-signature for consent forms.

## Market Size

$4.2B online form builder market (Fortune Business Insights). Industry-specific intelligent intake: ~$600M.

## Revenue Potential

6,000 practices at $39/mo average = $2.8M ARR.

## Existing Competitors

IntakeQ ($49+/mo, medical-focused), LawMatics ($200+/mo, legal-focused), Typeform/JotForm (generic, no industry integrations). Space is fragmented by industry vertical with no dominant cross-industry player.

## Competitive Moat

Practice management integrations are time-intensive to build and create lock-in. Industry-specific templates improve from usage. Conditional logic that understands industry workflows (not just generic "if X then show Y") is a genuine differentiator.
