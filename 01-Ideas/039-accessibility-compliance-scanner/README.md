# 39. Accessibility Compliance Scanner

> Automated ADA/WCAG website accessibility scanning with fix suggestions and legal protection documentation

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Category** | Compliance / B2B SaaS |
| **Target Audience** | Small e-commerce stores, local businesses with websites, web agencies managing client sites |
| **Monetization** | Subscription ($19-79/mo per website; agency plans at $149-299/mo for 10-50 sites) |
| **Recession Resistance** | High - ADA website lawsuits have increased 300% since 2018 and show no signs of slowing; this is legal protection, not a nice-to-have |
| **Build Effort** | Medium - 10-14 weeks MVP |

## Problem

ADA website accessibility lawsuits exceeded 4,600 in 2024 (UsableNet data), up from 2,300 in 2019. Average settlement: $25,000-75,000. Average defense cost even when winning: $10,000-50,000. Small e-commerce businesses are disproportionately targeted because they lack resources to fight. 96.3% of home pages have detectable WCAG accessibility failures (WebAIM Million study). Most small business owners have never heard of WCAG and don't know they're at risk.

**Reddit signal**: r/smallbusiness and r/webdev have alarming threads about businesses receiving ADA demand letters. "I got a letter demanding $15,000 because my website doesn't have alt text on images. I didn't even know this was a thing." Web agencies see this as a service offering opportunity.

## Solution

Automated website crawl identifying WCAG 2.1 AA violations. Prioritized fix list in plain English (not developer jargon). One-click fix suggestions with code snippets. Accessibility statement generator. Continuous monitoring with monthly reports. Legal protection documentation (proof of good faith compliance efforts). Agency dashboard for managing multiple client sites.

## Market Size

$800M web accessibility software market (Grand View Research), growing 20%+ CAGR driven by litigation.

## Revenue Potential

10,000 sites at $39/mo average = $4.7M ARR. Agency tier drives high ARPU.

## Existing Competitors

accessiBe ($490/yr, controversial AI overlay approach), AudioEye ($199+/mo), UserWay (free widget + $49+/mo monitoring). accessiBe is widely criticized by the accessibility community for its overlay approach. A genuine scanning + education approach is differentiated.

## Competitive Moat

Continuous monitoring creates ongoing compliance records (legal defensibility). Agency channel creates scalable distribution. As lawsuits increase, the tool's value proposition strengthens automatically. Avoiding the overlay approach (which courts have ruled insufficient) is a competitive advantage.
