## The Problem
ADHD affects 8.7 million US adults (4.4% prevalence, NIMH). Task paralysis — the inability to start tasks that feel overwhelming — is the #1 complaint on r/ADHD (2.1M members). Existing task managers (Todoist, Things, TickTick) are built for neurotypical brains: they assume you can see a task like "do taxes" and just... do it. For ADHD brains, "do taxes" is a wall. The task needs to be broken into "find W-2 in email," "open TurboTax," "enter name and address," etc. Currently, people do this decomposition manually or ask ChatGPT, but neither is integrated into a task flow with timers and momentum mechanics.

## The Solution
1. User enters an overwhelming task (e.g., "clean apartment")
2. AI decomposes it into 5-15 micro-steps with time estimates (e.g., "pick up clothes from bedroom floor — 3 min")
3. User rates current energy level (1-5); app reorders steps to match (low energy = easy wins first)
4. Built-in body-doubling timer: a lofi background presence (ambient sound + gentle check-ins)
5. "Just start" mode: shows only the very next micro-step, nothing else
6. Completion celebrations calibrated to ADHD dopamine needs (not just a checkmark)
7. Streak mechanics designed for inconsistency (ADHD-friendly: no punishment for missed days)

## Why This Wins
- Goblin Tools proved demand (viral on TikTok, millions of users) but is a one-trick tool — no workflow, no persistence, no timers
- ADHD community is extremely loyal and vocal — word-of-mouth is powerful (r/ADHD, ADHD TikTok)
- Energy-level matching is a novel, clinically-informed feature no competitor offers
- "No shame" design philosophy (no streaks that punish, no red overdue badges) differentiates from every mainstream task app
