## The Problem
100 million Americans carry medical debt (KFF, 2022). The median medical debt in collections is $690, but 1 in 5 owe more than $5,000. Here's the secret most people don't know: virtually every non-profit hospital is legally required to offer financial assistance (charity care) programs under IRS 501(r) regulations, but only 50% of eligible patients actually apply. Hospitals bury these programs in fine print. Meanwhile, medical bills are wildly inflatable — the same procedure can cost $800 or $8,000 depending on whether you negotiate. Most people don't know they can negotiate, and those who do don't know what to say.

## The Solution
1. User uploads or enters their medical bill details (provider, amount, procedure codes)
2. App identifies the hospital/provider and pulls their financial assistance policy
3. Generates a pre-filled financial assistance application based on user's income
4. Provides negotiation scripts for phone calls ("I'd like to discuss your uninsured discount...")
5. Compares bill against Medicare rates and fair-price databases to identify overcharges
6. Recommends optimal payment plan strategies to avoid collections
7. Tracks dispute status and next steps

## Existing Alternatives
- **Dollar For**: Non-profit that helps with charity care applications, but limited capacity (waitlists)
- **Resolve Medical Bills**: Human negotiators, takes 25% of savings (expensive)
- **CoPatient**: Same model, 35% of savings
- **Reddit/TikTok advice**: Free but scattered, inconsistent, no tools
- **Nothing**: Most people just pay the bill or let it go to collections

## Why This Wins
- Self-service tool at a fraction of the cost of human negotiation services
- Automated financial assistance application generation is a killer feature
- Medicare rate comparison gives users ammunition for negotiation
- Addresses a problem where the "customer" (patient) has extreme information asymmetry
- Recession-proof: demand spikes when employment-based insurance coverage drops
