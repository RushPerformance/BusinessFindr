# Elderly Care Coordinator

> Helps adult children coordinate care for aging parents: medication tracking, appointment scheduling, caregiver task sharing, and emergency contacts — all in one family dashboard.

## The Problem
53 million Americans are unpaid caregivers for an adult family member (AARP/NAC, 2020). The average caregiver spends 24 hours/week on caregiving tasks. 61% of caregivers report that coordinating among siblings/family members is a major source of stress and conflict. Currently, families use a chaotic mix of group texts, shared Google Docs, and sticky notes to track medications, appointments, and who's doing what. There is no centralized "family care dashboard." Caregiver burnout costs the US economy $522 billion/year in lost productivity.

## The Solution
1. Create a care circle for each elderly family member
2. Medication management: list meds, dosages, schedules with refill reminders
3. Appointment calendar shared across all family caregivers
4. Task board: assign and track caregiving tasks (grocery shopping, doctor drives, bill paying)
5. Health journal: log symptoms, vitals, mood for doctor visits
6. Document vault: insurance cards, advance directives, power of attorney
7. Emergency contact sheet accessible to all circle members
8. Weekly summary email to keep remote family members informed

## Existing Alternatives
- **CareZone**: Shut down in 2022
- **Lotsa Helping Hands**: Volunteer coordination only, outdated UX
- **CaringBridge**: Health updates/journal only, no task coordination
- **Medisafe**: Medication tracking only
- **Group text/Google Docs**: Universal but fragmented, no structure

## Why This Wins
- CareZone's shutdown left a massive gap — it had millions of users
- No single app combines medications + appointments + tasks + family coordination
- The "care circle" model creates natural viral growth (invite siblings)
- Emotionally resonant product with strong word-of-mouth potential
- Growing demographic: 10,000 Americans turn 65 every day
