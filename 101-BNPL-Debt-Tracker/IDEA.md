## Problem

Buy Now Pay Later usage has exploded 970% since 2019, with the average BNPL user now carrying 3-4 active installment plans simultaneously across multiple providers. A 2024 Federal Reserve study found that 43% of BNPL users have missed at least one payment, and 56% of users underestimate their total BNPL debt by more than $500. Credit Karma reports that the average American BNPL user owes $1,400 across multiple platforms, but because each platform reports independently (if at all), there is no single view of total obligations. Late fees across BNPL providers average $7-$15 per missed payment, and 28% of users report overdrawing their bank account due to forgotten BNPL autopay deductions.

---

## Solution

1. **Multi-Platform Aggregation** — Connects to Afterpay, Klarna, Affirm, PayPal Pay-in-4, Zip, and Sezzle via email receipt scanning and bank transaction matching to pull all active installment plans into one view.
2. **True Debt Dashboard** — Displays total outstanding BNPL debt, total monthly obligations, and a rolling 90-day payment calendar so users see the full picture at a glance.
3. **Payment Schedule Alerts** — Push notifications 2 days before each BNPL payment hits, with the exact amount and which bank account will be debited.
4. **Cash Flow Impact Analyzer** — Overlays BNPL payment dates against payday schedule and recurring bills to flag potential overdraft situations before they happen.
5. **Debt Payoff Planner** — Suggests optimal payment order (highest-fee-first vs. smallest-balance-first) and shows total interest/fees saved by paying off early.
6. **Spending Pattern Insights** — Categorizes BNPL purchases to show where installment spending concentrates (fashion, electronics, home goods) with month-over-month trends.
7. **Credit Score Impact Tracker** — Monitors which BNPL providers report to credit bureaus and estimates the impact of current BNPL usage on credit score.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **Mint/Credit Karma** — Shows bank transactions but does not parse BNPL installment plans as separate obligations; lumps them with regular purchases.
2. **YNAB (You Need A Budget)** — Powerful budgeting tool but requires manual entry of each BNPL payment; no automatic aggregation across BNPL providers.
3. **Cushion** — Focuses on bill negotiation and overdraft prevention but does not specifically track or aggregate BNPL obligations.
4. **Individual BNPL Apps** — Each provider (Afterpay, Klarna, etc.) shows only their own balances; no cross-platform view exists.
5. **Spreadsheets** — Many users resort to manual tracking but quickly fall behind as new purchases are added across platforms.
