**One-Line Pitch:** A simple, beautiful MRR/ARR tracking dashboard built specifically for indie hackers and solo founders, with churn analysis, cohort charts, and milestone celebrations that make growing a micro-SaaS feel rewarding.

---

## Problem

There are an estimated 30,000+ micro-SaaS products generating revenue today, and the indie hacker community on platforms like Indie Hackers, Twitter/X, and Reddit continues to grow 25% year-over-year. Yet most revenue analytics tools — Baremetrics ($108/mo minimum), ChartMogul ($100/mo+), ProfitWell (acquired by Paddle) — are designed for funded startups with engineering teams to handle integration. A 2024 survey of 1,200 indie hackers found that 67% track MRR in spreadsheets, 41% cannot accurately calculate their churn rate, and 73% have no cohort analysis at all. The average solo founder spends 3-4 hours per month manually updating revenue spreadsheets when that time could be spent building product.

---

## Solution

1. **One-Click Stripe/Paddle/Gumroad Connect** — OAuth integration with the three most popular indie hacker payment processors; dashboard populates in under 60 seconds.
2. **MRR/ARR Dashboard** — Real-time display of Monthly Recurring Revenue, Annual Run Rate, net new MRR, expansion MRR, and churned MRR with clean visualizations.
3. **Churn Analysis Engine** — Automatic calculation of logo churn, revenue churn, and net revenue retention with drill-down into churned customers and cancellation reasons.
4. **Cohort Retention Charts** — Visual cohort analysis showing retention curves by signup month, plan tier, and acquisition channel without any SQL required.
5. **Milestone Celebrations** — Confetti animations and shareable milestone cards when you hit $100 MRR, $1K MRR, $10K MRR, and other key milestones — designed for sharing on Twitter/X.
6. **Public Revenue Dashboard** — Optional public-facing revenue page (like Open Startup pages) to build in public and attract customers through transparency.
7. **Weekly Email Digest** — Sunday evening email summarizing the week's revenue, new trials, conversions, and churns with trend arrows.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **Baremetrics** — Gold standard for SaaS analytics but starts at $108/mo, far too expensive for a product making $500/mo MRR.
2. **ChartMogul** — Powerful cohort analysis but complex setup and $100/mo+ pricing excludes most solo founders.
3. **ProfitWell (Paddle)** — Was free but now bundled with Paddle payment processing, locking users into a specific payment stack.
4. **Stripe Dashboard** — Provides basic revenue data but no churn analysis, no cohort charts, and no MRR calculation for annual plans.
5. **Google Sheets + Manual Tracking** — The current default for most indie hackers; error-prone, time-consuming, and produces no automated insights.

## Why This Wins

- **Price point designed for indie economics** — at $9.99/mo, it pays for itself the moment it helps retain even one $10/mo subscriber through churn insights.
- **Community-native distribution** — milestone sharing cards and public dashboards turn every user into a marketing channel on Indie Hackers and Twitter/X.
- **Simplicity is the product** — fewer features than enterprise tools is a feature, not a bug; indie hackers want clarity, not complexity.
- **Emotional engagement** — milestone celebrations add genuine dopamine to the grind of building a SaaS solo, improving retention.
- **Low switching cost from spreadsheets** — most target users have no existing tool, so there is no migration friction.
