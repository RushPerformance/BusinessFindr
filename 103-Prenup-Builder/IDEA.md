## Problem

The average cost of a prenuptial agreement drafted by attorneys ranges from $2,500 to $10,000, with each party needing separate legal counsel. Despite this cost barrier, demand for prenups has surged — the American Academy of Matrimonial Lawyers reported a 62% increase in prenup requests from 2019 to 2024, with millennials driving 75% of new demand. A 2023 Harris Poll found that 40% of unmarried Americans believe prenups should be standard, yet only 15% of married couples actually have one. The primary barrier is cost (cited by 58% of respondents) followed by not knowing where to start (34%). Meanwhile, 2.5 million couples get married annually in the US, representing a massive underserved market for affordable legal document preparation.

---

## Solution

1. **Guided Interview Flow** — Step-by-step questionnaire covering assets, debts, income, property, business interests, inheritance expectations, and spousal support preferences in plain English.
2. **Asset Disclosure Worksheets** — Structured templates for listing all assets and liabilities for both partners, with categories for real estate, retirement accounts, investments, and personal property.
3. **State-Specific Templates** — Pre-built clause libraries for all 50 states reflecting local community property vs. equitable distribution rules and enforceability requirements.
4. **Clause Builder** — Mix-and-match selection of common prenup clauses (sunset provisions, infidelity clauses, pet custody, social media restrictions) with plain-English explanations.
5. **Side-by-Side Comparison** — Shows each partner what they gain and give up under the prenup vs. without one, reducing adversarial feelings.
6. **Attorney Review Prep** — Generates a clean, formatted document ready for each partner's attorney to review, with annotations highlighting areas that need legal verification.
7. **Notarization Coordination** — Connects couples with online notary services to complete the signing process legally.

## Existing Alternatives

2. **Rocket Lawyer** — Provides legal document templates but prenup offerings lack guided asset disclosure and comparison features.
