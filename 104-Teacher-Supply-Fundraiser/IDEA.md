# Teacher Supply Fundraiser

**One-Line Pitch:** A platform where teachers create wishlists of classroom supplies and parents, alumni, and community members can directly fund specific items — bringing transparency and efficiency to the $1.8B teacher supply gap.

| Field | Details |
|---|---|
| **Category** | Education / Social Impact |
| **Target Audience** | K-12 teachers, parents, school administrators, and community donors |
| **Monetization** | 8% commission on funded items + optional promoted listings |
| **Recession Resistance** | Medium-High |
| **Solo-Dev MVP Timeline** | 10-12 weeks |
| **Composite Score** | 39/100 |

---

## Problem

The average US teacher spends $860 of their own money on classroom supplies annually, according to the National Center for Education Statistics, with 30% of teachers in Title I schools spending over $1,000. That adds up to $1.8B in out-of-pocket teacher spending nationwide. A 2024 EdWeek survey found that 94% of teachers buy supplies with personal funds, and 62% say it causes financial stress. Meanwhile, parents and community members want to help but lack visibility into what is actually needed — PTA fundraisers often produce general funds with no transparency into spending. DonorsChoose exists but has a 15-25% platform fee and a lengthy approval process that can take weeks, discouraging teachers from smaller, time-sensitive requests.

---

## Solution

1. **Teacher Wishlist Builder** — Teachers create categorized wishlists (art supplies, books, technology, cleaning supplies, snacks) with specific items linked to Amazon, Walmart, or Staples pricing.
2. **Direct Funding** — Donors fund specific items they choose, with real-time progress bars showing how close each item is to being fully funded.
3. **Classroom Profiles** — Teachers create profiles with classroom photos, student count, school information, and a description of their teaching approach to build donor connection.
4. **Automated Fulfillment** — Once an item is fully funded, it is automatically ordered and shipped directly to the school, eliminating teacher procurement burden.
5. **Impact Reporting** — Donors receive photo updates showing funded supplies in use, with student thank-you notes (privacy-compliant) to encourage repeat giving.
6. **School Admin Dashboard** — Principals and administrators see aggregated supply needs across their school, identify patterns, and coordinate district-level purchasing.
7. **Recurring Donor Plans** — Community members can set monthly donation budgets that automatically fund the highest-priority items across selected schools.
8. **Tax Receipt Generation** — Automatic tax-deductible donation receipts for all contributions over $25.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **DonorsChoose** — The dominant platform but charges 15-25% fees, has a lengthy project approval process, and focuses on larger project-based funding rather than everyday supplies.
2. **Amazon Wishlists** — Teachers share Amazon wishlists informally but there is no school verification, no impact tracking, and no tax receipt generation.
3. **GoFundMe/Facebook Fundraisers** — General crowdfunding platforms lack education-specific features, supply tracking, and automated fulfillment.
4. **PTA/PTO Fundraisers** — Raise general funds but lack transparency into how money is spent and cannot direct funding to specific classroom needs.
5. **AdoptAClassroom.org** — Provides funding to teachers but has limited donor choice over specific items and less community engagement.

---

## Why This Wins

- **Lower fees than DonorsChoose** — 8% commission vs. 15-25% means more of every dollar reaches the classroom.
- **Speed and simplicity** — no lengthy approval process; teachers can post a wishlist and share it with parents within minutes.
- **Hyper-local distribution** — parents naturally share within school communities via text groups and Facebook, creating organic viral growth school by school.
- **Emotional resonance** — few causes are more universally supported than helping teachers, making marketing and PR significantly easier.
- **Recurring model potential** — monthly donor plans create predictable revenue while solving the "September supply rush" problem.
