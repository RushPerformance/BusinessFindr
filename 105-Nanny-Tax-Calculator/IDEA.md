## Problem

An estimated 2.2 million households in the US employ domestic workers — nannies, housekeepers, home health aides, and private chefs — creating "household employer" tax obligations that most families are completely unaware of. The IRS requires families paying a household employee more than $2,700/year (2024 threshold) to withhold Social Security and Medicare taxes, pay unemployment taxes, and file Schedule H. Yet the Treasury Inspector General estimates that 70-80% of household employers fail to comply, exposing themselves to IRS penalties averaging $4,000-$25,000. The complexity is real: household employers must navigate federal income tax withholding, FICA (7.65% employee + 7.65% employer), FUTA, and state-specific requirements that vary wildly. Current solutions like GTM Payroll or HomePay charge $1,200-$2,500/year — prohibitively expensive for families paying a part-time babysitter or housekeeper.

---

## Solution

1. **Tax Obligation Calculator** — Enter pay rate, hours, and state; instantly see total employer tax cost, employee withholding amounts, and net pay calculations.
2. **Quarterly Filing Reminders** — Push notifications and email alerts before each quarterly tax deadline (April 15, June 15, Sept 15, Jan 15) with pre-filled estimated payment amounts.
3. **Paycheck Generator** — Creates printable pay stubs showing gross pay, withholdings (FICA, federal, state), and net pay for each pay period.
4. **State Tax Compliance** — Covers state-specific requirements including state unemployment insurance (SUI), workers' comp obligations, and state disability insurance for all 50 states.
5. **W-2 and Schedule H Prep** — Generates year-end W-2 for employees and pre-fills IRS Schedule H data for the employer's personal tax return.
6. **New Hire Reporting** — Walks employers through state new-hire reporting requirements and generates the necessary forms.
7. **Payment Tracking** — Logs all payments to household employees with running totals for the year, flagging when thresholds are crossed that trigger new tax obligations.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **GTM Payroll (HomeWork Solutions)** — Full-service nanny payroll but costs $1,200-$2,000/year; overkill for families with part-time help.
2. **HomePay (by Care.com)** — Comprehensive household payroll but $2,500/year and bundled with Care.com's larger platform.
3. **SurePayroll Nanny** — Offers nanny payroll but starts at $50/mo+ and is designed for business payroll first, household second.
4. **TurboTax Schedule H** — Handles year-end filing but provides no ongoing calculation, withholding guidance, or quarterly reminders throughout the year.
5. **Manual Calculation** — Most families either ignore the obligation entirely or try to calculate taxes manually using IRS Publication 926, which is 25 pages of dense tax guidance.
