## Problem

Approximately 21 million homes in the US (roughly 1 in 5) rely on septic systems, and the EPA estimates that 10-20% of septic systems fail each year due to neglected maintenance. Septic system replacement costs $10,000-$30,000, making it one of the most expensive home repairs possible. The fundamental maintenance requirement is simple — pump the tank every 3-5 years — yet a National Onsite Wastewater Recycling Association study found that 34% of septic system owners do not know when their system was last pumped, and 45% do not maintain any records at all. When selling a home, buyers and inspectors require septic inspection documentation, and missing records can delay closings by weeks or kill deals entirely. States like Massachusetts and Washington now require septic inspections for every real estate transaction, creating a compliance documentation need.

---

## Solution

1. **System Profile** — Record septic system type (conventional, aerobic, mound), tank size, installation date, drainfield location, and service provider information in one place.
2. **Pumping Schedule Reminders** — Automated reminders based on tank size, household size, and usage patterns; adjusts frequency recommendations dynamically.
3. **Maintenance Log** — Digital record of all pumpings, inspections, repairs, and additive treatments with date, provider, cost, and uploaded receipts/reports.
4. **Inspection Documentation** — Store and organize inspection reports, photographs, and compliance certificates needed for home sales and insurance.
5. **Usage Guidelines** — Personalized recommendations for what can and cannot go down drains based on your system type, with household member access for sharing rules.
6. **Service Provider Directory** — Find licensed septic service companies in your area with reviews, pricing, and direct scheduling.
7. **Home Sale Prep Report** — Generate a comprehensive maintenance history report formatted for real estate transactions and buyer due diligence.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **Paper Records / Filing Cabinets** — The current "system" for most septic owners: receipts in a drawer, if they are kept at all.
2. **Phone Calendar Reminders** — Some homeowners set reminders but without system-specific scheduling or documentation storage.
3. **Septic Service Company Records** — Service companies keep their own records but homeowners cannot access them easily, and records are lost if the company closes.
4. **Home Maintenance Apps (HomeZada, Centriq)** — General home maintenance apps include septic as one of hundreds of categories with no specialized features.
5. **County Health Department Records** — Some counties maintain septic permit records but they are not easily searchable and contain installation data only, not maintenance history.
