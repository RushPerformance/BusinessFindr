## Problem

The US pet industry reached $136.8 billion in 2022, with veterinary care and pharmaceutical spending at $35.9 billion — a segment growing 8% annually. An estimated 68% of US households own a pet, and as pets live longer thanks to advances in veterinary medicine, chronic medication management has become routine. A 2023 Banfield Pet Hospital study found that medication non-compliance in pets is 40-50%, meaning nearly half of prescribed doses are missed or given incorrectly. This non-compliance leads to treatment failure, disease progression, and additional vet visits costing $200-$500 each. The problem intensifies in multi-pet households (35% of pet owners have 2+ pets) and with complex medication schedules (heartworm monthly, flea/tick monthly, arthritis daily, thyroid twice daily). Unlike human pharmacies, most veterinary pharmacies do not offer automatic refill reminders, and pet medications often have different schedules that do not align with human routines.

---

## Solution

1. **Multi-Pet Medication Profiles** — Create profiles for each pet with their medications, dosages, frequencies, and prescribing veterinarian — supporting unlimited pets per household.
2. **Smart Reminders** — Push notifications for each medication dose, customizable by time of day, with snooze and "given" confirmation to track compliance.
3. **Refill Alerts** — Automatic refill reminders based on remaining supply count, with direct links to online pet pharmacies (Chewy, PetMeds, 1-800-PetMeds) for reordering.
4. **Compliance Dashboard** — Visual calendar showing medication adherence over time, with percentage scores and streak tracking to motivate consistency.
5. **Vet-Shareable Reports** — Generate compliance reports that can be emailed or shown to the veterinarian, helping them assess whether treatment failures are due to the medication or non-compliance.
6. **Medication Interaction Checker** — Basic flag system for common pet medication interactions (e.g., NSAIDs + corticosteroids) when adding new prescriptions.
7. **Weight and Symptom Tracking** — Log pet weight and symptoms over time to correlate with medication changes, helping vets optimize treatment.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **Phone Alarms/Reminders** — The most common current approach, but cannot track compliance, manage refills, or generate vet reports.
2. **PetDesk** — Veterinary clinic communication platform but focused on appointment scheduling, not medication management for pet owners.
3. **Pet Health Apps (PetPace, Whistle)** — Focus on activity monitoring and GPS tracking, not medication scheduling or compliance.
4. **Chewy AutoShip** — Handles medication reordering on a schedule but does not remind owners to actually give the medication or track compliance.
5. **Veterinary Clinic Portals** — Some vet clinics offer patient portals but they are clinic-specific, lack medication reminders, and require vet-side adoption.
