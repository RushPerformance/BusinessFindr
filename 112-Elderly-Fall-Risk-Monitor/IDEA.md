## Problem

Falls are the leading cause of injury death among adults 65 and older, killing over 36,000 Americans annually and causing 3 million emergency department visits. The CDC reports that 1 in 4 older adults falls each year, and falling once doubles the chance of falling again. The direct medical costs of falls exceed $50 billion annually. Current solutions are reactive — Life Alert and medical alert devices only help after a fall has occurred. Research published in the Journal of the American Geriatrics Society demonstrates that gait speed changes, stride variability, and balance asymmetry can predict fall risk 2-4 weeks before a fall occurs with 73% accuracy. Modern smartphones contain accelerometers, gyroscopes, and motion sensors capable of detecting these changes, yet no consumer app applies this research to proactive fall prevention. Meanwhile, 77% of adults 65+ own smartphones, and 92% of their adult children express concern about their parents falling.

---

## Solution

1. **Passive Gait Monitoring** — Uses the phone's accelerometer and gyroscope during normal walking to measure gait speed, stride length, stride variability, and asymmetry without requiring the user to do anything special.
2. **Balance Assessment Tests** — Optional weekly 30-second balance tests (standing with feet together, eyes closed) that track stability scores over time.
3. **Risk Score Dashboard** — A simple 1-10 fall risk score updated weekly, visible to both the senior and their designated family members.
4. **Family Alert System** — Automatic notifications to family members when gait patterns deteriorate beyond baseline thresholds, enabling early intervention.
5. **Exercise Recommendations** — Evidence-based balance and strength exercises (from CDC's STEADI program) personalized to the user's current risk level and physical capabilities.
6. **Medication Review Prompts** — Flags when new medications are added that are known to increase fall risk (sedatives, blood pressure medications, etc.) and suggests discussing with their doctor.
7. **Environmental Safety Checklist** — Room-by-room home safety assessment identifying trip hazards, lighting issues, and grab bar needs.
8. **Fall Detection and Emergency Alert** — If a fall does occur, automatic detection with emergency contact notification and optional 911 dispatch.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **Life Alert / Medical Guardian** — Reactive fall detection devices that alert after a fall; $30-$50/mo with no predictive or preventive capabilities.
2. **Apple Watch Fall Detection** — Detects falls after they happen and calls emergency services, but does not predict or prevent falls.
3. **APDM Mobility Lab** — Clinical-grade gait analysis but costs $15,000+ and is used only in research settings and physical therapy clinics.
4. **Physical Therapy Assessments** — PT evaluations provide fall risk assessment but are point-in-time ($150-$300/visit) and not continuously monitored.
5. **CDC STEADI Toolkit** — Free fall risk screening tool for clinicians but requires clinical administration and is not accessible as a consumer self-monitoring tool.

---

## Why This Wins

- **Prevention vs. reaction** — existing products help after a fall; this app aims to prevent falls from happening, a fundamentally different and more valuable proposition.
- **No additional hardware** — works with the smartphone the senior already carries, unlike Life Alert pendants or specialized wearables.
- **Family peace of mind** — the real buyer is often the adult child, who will happily pay $7.99/mo for continuous monitoring of a parent living alone.
- **Clinical evidence base** — gait analysis for fall prediction is well-established in research literature, providing credibility with healthcare providers.
- **Recession-proof category** — aging parents need care regardless of economic conditions; adult children prioritize parent safety spending.
