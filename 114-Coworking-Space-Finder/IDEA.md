## Problem

The remote work revolution has created 35+ million Americans who work from home at least part-time, yet 67% of remote workers report feeling isolated (Buffer's State of Remote Work 2024), and 42% struggle to find productive workspaces outside their home. The coworking industry has grown to $26 billion globally with 41,000+ spaces worldwide, but discovery remains fragmented. There is no single platform where a remote worker can search for available workspace — coworking spaces list on their own websites, Google Maps shows outdated information, and aggregators like Coworker.com focus on monthly memberships rather than day passes. Libraries and cafes with good WiFi and outlets are completely absent from workspace search. A 2023 survey found that remote workers spend an average of 25 minutes researching workspace options when traveling, and 34% give up and work from hotel rooms despite wanting a more productive environment.

---

## Solution

1. **Comprehensive Workspace Directory** — Searchable database of coworking spaces, libraries with work-friendly areas, cafes with WiFi and outlets, hotel lobbies with day access, and other bookable workspaces.
2. **Amenity Filters** — Filter by WiFi speed, monitor availability, standing desks, private phone rooms, printing, coffee included, parking, and accessibility.
3. **Real-Time Availability** — Live occupancy data for partner spaces showing current crowding levels and available desks/rooms.
4. **Day Pass Booking** — Book and pay for single-day coworking passes directly through the app, with QR code entry at partner locations.
5. **Community Reviews** — User ratings focused on work-relevant criteria: WiFi reliability, noise level, outlet availability, and coffee quality.
6. **Noise Level Indicator** — Crowdsourced real-time noise level data so users can find quiet spaces when they need deep focus or louder spaces for calls.
7. **Favorite Spots and History** — Save preferred workspaces and track where you have worked, building a personal workspace portfolio across cities.
8. **Team Coordination** — Share workspace finds with teammates for co-located work days, with group booking for meeting rooms.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **Coworker.com** — Largest coworking directory but focused on monthly memberships in major cities; limited day-pass booking and no cafe/library listings.
2. **WeWork All Access** — Provides multi-location access but only within the WeWork network; $299/mo is expensive for occasional use.
3. **Deskpass** — Day-pass focused but limited to 17 US cities and does not include libraries, cafes, or non-partner spaces.
4. **Google Maps** — Shows coworking spaces but with no real-time availability, no amenity filters, and no booking capability.
5. **Workfrom** — Community-built directory of cafes and workspaces but lacks booking, has outdated listings, and limited coverage outside major metros.

---

## Why This Wins

- **Broader workspace definition** — including libraries, cafes, and hotel lobbies alongside coworking spaces means 10x more listings than competitors focused solely on coworking.
- **Day-pass focus** — optimized for the "I need a workspace today" use case rather than monthly memberships, serving the 80% of remote workers who need occasional variety.
- **Network effects** — every review, noise level report, and WiFi speed test makes the platform more valuable for all users.
- **Booking commission revenue** — in addition to subscriptions, earning 15-20% commission on day-pass bookings creates a transaction-based revenue stream.
- **Travel professional distribution** — partnerships with travel booking platforms and expense management tools create B2B acquisition channels.
