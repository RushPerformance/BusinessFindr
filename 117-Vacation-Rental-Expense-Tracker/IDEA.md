## Problem

There are 1.5 million active short-term rental hosts in the US, and the IRS requires all of them to report rental income on Schedule E (or Schedule C if providing substantial services). Yet a 2023 survey by Avalara MyLodgeTax found that 61% of hosts do not properly track expenses, 47% miss legitimate tax deductions, and 38% have never filed Schedule E correctly. The average vacation rental host has $8,000-$15,000 in deductible expenses annually (cleaning, maintenance, supplies, insurance, mortgage interest, depreciation) but claims only 60% of eligible deductions due to poor record-keeping — leaving $3,200-$6,000 in tax savings on the table. QuickBooks and traditional accounting software are designed for ongoing businesses, not the unique aspects of short-term rentals: personal use days, 14-day rule exceptions, prorated expenses, and platform-specific income reconciliation across Airbnb, VRBO, and direct bookings.

---

## Solution

1. **Platform Income Sync** — Connects to Airbnb, VRBO, and Booking.com to automatically import reservation income, cleaning fees, platform fees, and guest data.
2. **Expense Categorization** — Pre-built expense categories matching IRS Schedule E line items (advertising, cleaning, insurance, legal, management fees, mortgage interest, repairs, supplies, taxes, utilities, depreciation).
3. **Receipt Capture** — Snap photos of receipts that are automatically categorized, OCR-processed, and attached to the correct property and expense category.
4. **Occupancy Dashboard** — Track occupancy rate, average nightly rate, revenue per available night (RevPAN), and seasonal performance trends.
5. **Personal Use Day Tracker** — Automatically tracks personal use days vs. rental days, calculates the personal use ratio, and adjusts deductible expenses accordingly per IRS rules.
6. **Schedule E Generator** — Pre-fills IRS Schedule E with all income and expense data, ready to hand to a tax preparer or import into TurboTax/H&R Block.
7. **Multi-Property Support** — Manage up to 5 rental properties with separate P&L statements and consolidated portfolio-level reporting.
8. **Mileage Tracker** — Log trips to the rental property for maintenance, guest check-in, and supply runs with IRS-compliant mileage documentation.
