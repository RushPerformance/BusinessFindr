## Problem

Postpartum depression (PPD) affects 1 in 7 new mothers (approximately 600,000 women annually in the US), and postpartum anxiety affects an additional 1 in 5. The American College of Obstetricians and Gynecologists (ACOG) reports that 50% of PPD cases go undiagnosed, largely because the standard screening (Edinburgh Postnatal Depression Scale) is administered only at the 6-week postpartum visit — despite PPD onset occurring anywhere from 1 week to 12 months after delivery. A 2023 study in JAMA Psychiatry found that daily mood tracking increases early detection of PPD by 40% compared to point-in-time clinical screening. Untreated PPD costs the US $14.2 billion annually in lost productivity, healthcare utilization, and long-term child development impacts. Partners often notice changes before the mother herself recognizes symptoms, but lack a structured way to communicate concern without seeming judgmental or dismissive.

---

## Solution

1. **Daily Mood Check-In** — A 30-second daily check-in tracking mood, anxiety, sleep quality, appetite, bonding feelings, and intrusive thoughts using validated clinical scales adapted for daily use.
2. **Edinburgh Scale Integration** — Built-in Edinburgh Postnatal Depression Scale (EPDS) administered weekly with automatic scoring and trend tracking, flagging scores that warrant clinical attention.
3. **Partner Dashboard** — Optional partner view showing mood trends (not individual entries) so partners can monitor for concerning patterns and know when to offer support or suggest professional help.
4. **Provider Reports** — Generate a summary report of mood data, sleep patterns, and screening scores formatted for OB/GYN or therapist review during appointments.
5. **Symptom Pattern Recognition** — Identifies patterns between mood and contributing factors (sleep deprivation, feeding difficulties, isolation, hormonal shifts) to help mothers understand their experience.
6. **Resource Library** — Evidence-based information about PPD/PPA that normalizes the experience and explains treatment options (therapy, medication, support groups).
7. **Crisis Detection** — Automatic escalation when entries indicate severe depression or suicidal ideation, surfacing crisis resources (988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline, Postpartum Support International hotline).
8. **Peer Connection** — Moderated anonymous forums connecting mothers experiencing similar postpartum challenges, reducing the isolation that worsens PPD.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **General Mood Tracking Apps (Daylio, Moodfit)** — Track mood generically but lack postpartum-specific symptoms, Edinburgh Scale integration, and partner alerts.
2. **Pregnancy Apps (What to Expect, BabyCenter)** — Cover pregnancy and baby milestones but postpartum mental health is a small afterthought with no daily tracking.
3. **Postpartum Support International (PSI)** — Provides resources and helplines but no daily tracking tool or provider integration.
4. **Therapy/Telehealth (BetterHelp, Talkspace)** — Professional support but $60-$100/week; no between-session daily monitoring or partner coordination.
5. **PPD Screening at OB/GYN** — Standard of care but administered only 1-2 times in the postpartum year, missing the 50% of cases that develop between visits.
