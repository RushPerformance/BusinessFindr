## Problem

Noise is the #1 neighbor complaint in the US, with 57% of apartment dwellers reporting significant noise issues (National Apartment Association). The American Housing Survey found that 19 million households report bothersome street or neighborhood noise. Noise complaints to city agencies have surged 300% since the pandemic as more people work from home. The consequences are serious: noise disputes are the most common trigger for neighbor-on-neighbor violence (DOJ statistics), and 24% of people who move from apartments cite noise as a primary reason — an expensive solution at $3,000-$7,000 in moving costs. Current resolution paths are all flawed: calling the police creates antagonism and is ineffective for chronic issues (78% of noise complaints result in no citation), HOA complaints take weeks to process, and hiring a mediator costs $200-$500/session. Most people either suffer in silence or escalate confrontation because no middle-ground tool exists for structured, non-confrontational communication about noise.

---

## Solution

1. **Noise Documentation Log** — Record noise incidents with timestamps, duration, estimated decibel levels (using phone microphone), and audio recordings as evidence.
2. **Decibel Meter** — Built-in sound level meter using the phone's microphone with logging and visualization, providing objective documentation.
3. **Neighbor Letter Templates** — Pre-written, tone-tested communication templates for first contact, follow-up, and escalation — designed by conflict resolution professionals to be firm but non-confrontational.
4. **Mediation Conversation Guide** — Step-by-step facilitation guide for having an in-person conversation with a noisy neighbor, with scripts for common scenarios (parties, music, dogs, early morning noise).
5. **Escalation Pathway** — Clear roadmap of escalation options: direct conversation, written notice, landlord/HOA complaint, city noise ordinance report, mediation service, and small claims court — with templates for each step.
6. **Local Noise Ordinance Lookup** — Database of city and county noise ordinances showing specific decibel limits, quiet hours, and complaint filing procedures for the user's location.
7. **Evidence Package Generator** — Compiles all logged incidents, audio recordings, and communication history into a formatted evidence package for landlords, HOAs, or courts.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **Calling the Police** — Free but adversarial, usually ineffective for chronic noise, and can escalate the conflict dangerously.
2. **HOA/Landlord Complaints** — Slow (weeks to months for resolution), inconsistently enforced, and can damage the complainant's relationship with management.
3. **Professional Mediation Services** — Effective but expensive ($200-$500/session) and overkill for most residential noise disputes.
4. **Generic Letter Templates (online)** — Free complaint letter templates exist but are not designed for the specific emotional dynamics of neighbor relationships.
5. **Noise Recording Apps (Decibel X, NIOSH SLM)** — Measure sound levels but provide no documentation framework, communication tools, or resolution guidance.
