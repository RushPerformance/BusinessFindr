## Problem

The US food storage container market is valued at $6.3 billion, and the average American household owns 30-50 food storage containers. Yet a Harris Poll found that 79% of households report regularly losing track of containers — left at work, lent to friends after dinner parties, buried in the back of the freezer with unlabeled mystery meals. Americans spend an estimated $2.4 billion annually replacing lost or mismatched food containers. The meal prep movement has made this worse: the meal prep market has grown 350% since 2017, with 36% of Americans now batch-cooking weekly. Active meal preppers manage 15-25 containers simultaneously across refrigerators, freezers, work, gym bags, and family members' locations. Beyond container tracking, frozen meal preppers regularly throw away food because they forget what is in the freezer and when it was frozen — the average household wastes $1,500/year in food, with forgotten freezer meals being a significant contributor.

---

## Solution

1. **Container Inventory** — Register your containers by type, size, brand, and color with photos; track total owned vs. in-use vs. missing.
2. **Location Tracking** — Tag each container's current location: home fridge, home freezer, work fridge, gym bag, lent to [person], dishwasher, or unknown.
3. **Freezer Meal Catalog** — Log what is in each frozen container with contents, date frozen, and serving size; see everything in your freezer at a glance.
4. **Defrost Reminders** — Set reminders to move meals from freezer to fridge the night before, with notifications based on your meal plan.
5. **Expiration Alerts** — Track how long items have been frozen and alert when they approach recommended freezer storage limits by food type.
6. **Lending Tracker** — Record which containers you lent to whom, with gentle reminder notifications to ask for them back.
7. **Meal Prep Planning** — See how many clean containers are available in each size before planning the week's batch cook, avoiding the "I don't have enough containers" problem.
8. **Grocery Integration** — Flag when container inventory is low and suggest replacement sets based on your most-used sizes.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **Notes Apps / Spreadsheets** — Some organized meal preppers track containers manually, but most abandon the effort within weeks.
2. **Freezer Inventory Apps (CozZo, Fridge Pal)** — Track freezer contents but do not track containers themselves, lending, or capacity planning.
3. **Meal Prep Apps (Prepear, Mealime)** — Focus on recipes and shopping lists, not container management or freezer inventory.
4. **Label Makers / Tape** — Physical labeling helps with freezer contents but does not track container location, lending, or planning.
5. **Memory** — The current default; unreliable and responsible for the 79% of households that regularly lose containers.
