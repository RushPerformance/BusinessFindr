## Problem

Americans bought 36.2 million used cars in 2023, spending an average of $28,381 per vehicle — the second-largest purchase most people make after housing. Yet Consumer Reports found that 23% of used car buyers experience significant mechanical issues within the first year, costing an average of $4,600 in unexpected repairs. Carfax reports that 1 in 4 used cars on the market has an open recall, and the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration estimates that 4.2 million used cars on the road have undisclosed flood damage. Most buyers lack the knowledge to conduct even a basic pre-purchase inspection: 67% of used car buyers in a 2023 AAA survey said they would not know what to check beyond "does it start and drive." Professional pre-purchase inspections cost $100-$250 and require scheduling, driving the car to a mechanic, and waiting — steps that many buyers skip, especially in competitive markets where sellers want quick decisions.

---

## Solution

1. **Comprehensive Inspection Checklist** — 150+ point inspection checklist organized by system (exterior, interior, engine, transmission, brakes, tires, electrical, frame) with photo documentation at each step.
2. **Red Flag Detection** — Highlights critical warning signs that indicate hidden damage: mismatched paint (accident repair), musty smell (flood damage), fresh undercoating (rust cover-up), and more.
3. **VIN Decoder** — Enter the VIN to pull vehicle specifications, verify the seller's description accuracy, and check for open recalls.
4. **Market Value Check** — Compare the asking price against Kelley Blue Book, Edmunds, and local comparable listings to determine if the price is fair.
5. **Test Drive Guide** — Structured test drive checklist: what to listen for, what to feel for, specific maneuvers to perform (highway merge, hard braking, tight turns, cold start).
6. **OBD-II Code Reader** — Instructions for using a $20 Bluetooth OBD-II scanner to pull diagnostic codes and what each code means in plain English.
7. **Negotiation Calculator** — Based on inspection findings, calculates a fair offer price by deducting estimated repair costs from market value, with scripts for presenting the offer.
8. **Mechanic Inspection Prep** — If the car passes the initial checklist, generates a summary to bring to a professional mechanic for a focused (cheaper, faster) professional inspection.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **Professional Pre-Purchase Inspections** — Thorough but cost $100-$250, require scheduling, and many buyers skip them due to time pressure.
2. **Carfax/AutoCheck Reports** — Provide vehicle history but not a physical inspection checklist; miss issues not reported to insurance or service databases.
3. **YouTube Inspection Videos** — Helpful but generic, not interactive, cannot be customized to the specific vehicle, and hard to follow while standing in a parking lot.
4. **Reddit/Forum Buying Guides** — Community advice is valuable but scattered, unstructured, and not available as a step-by-step tool during the actual inspection.
5. **Dealership "Certified Pre-Owned"** — Provides peace of mind but adds $2,000-$4,000 to the price and is not available for private-party purchases.
