# Home Insurance Optimizer

**One-Line Pitch:** Compares home insurance quotes, identifies coverage gaps, and tracks claim history to find savings — because most homeowners are either overpaying, underinsured, or both.

## Problem

The average US homeowner pays $2,230/year for home insurance (2024 NAIC data), a cost that has increased 30% in just three years. Yet J.D. Power found that 60% of homeowners have never compared quotes from multiple insurers, and those who do compare save an average of $400-$700 annually. Beyond overpaying, coverage gaps are epidemic: the Insurance Information Institute reports that 2 in 3 homes are underinsured by an average of 22%, meaning the average homeowner would be $73,000 short on a total loss claim. The complexity makes optimization difficult — understanding replacement cost vs. actual cash value, extended replacement cost endorsements, flood exclusions, jewelry riders, umbrella policy needs, and deductible trade-offs requires insurance literacy that most homeowners lack. The typical homeowner reviews their policy only at renewal (once per year, if at all), missing mid-year changes that affect coverage needs (home improvements, new valuables, liability changes).

---

## Solution

1. **Policy Upload and Analysis** — Upload your current homeowners insurance declarations page; the app extracts coverage details, limits, deductibles, and premium information.
2. **Coverage Gap Detector** — Compares your coverage against recommended levels based on home value, location, contents estimate, and liability exposure — flagging specific gaps.
3. **Quote Comparison Engine** — Pulls competitive quotes from partner insurers based on your home details and desired coverage, showing apples-to-apples comparisons.
4. **Deductible Optimizer** — Models the financial impact of different deductible levels based on your claim history and risk tolerance, finding the sweet spot between premium savings and out-of-pocket risk.
5. **Discount Finder** — Identifies available discounts you may not be claiming: bundling, security system, new roof, claims-free, loyalty, smart home devices, and professional organization memberships.
6. **Replacement Cost Calculator** — Estimates home reconstruction cost using local construction costs, square footage, and features — ensuring coverage limits match actual replacement needs.
7. **Claim History Tracker** — Log claims with details and track how they affect your premium over time, modeling when old claims drop off your record and premiums should decrease.
8. **Renewal Reminder** — Alerts 60 days before renewal with a fresh market comparison, ensuring you never auto-renew without checking competitive rates.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **Policygenius** — Insurance comparison marketplace but focused on life insurance; home insurance comparison is secondary and requires full applications.
2. **The Zebra** — Insurance comparison for auto and home but acts as a lead generator for agents, not a policy analysis or gap detection tool.
3. **Independent Insurance Agents** — Provide multi-carrier quotes but earn commissions that may bias recommendations, and offer no ongoing monitoring.
4. **Insurer Websites (State Farm, GEICO)** — Only show their own pricing, no coverage gap analysis, and no competitive comparison.
5. **NerdWallet/Bankrate** — Provide educational content and rate averages but no personalized policy analysis or optimization.

## Why This Wins

- **Immediate, measurable savings** — average savings of $400-$700/year makes the $4.99/mo subscription ($60/year) a no-brainer ROI.
- **Commission model alignment** — earning referral fees from insurers when users switch creates revenue beyond subscription fees and aligns with user interest in lower premiums.
- **Underinsurance urgency** — the 22% average coverage gap represents real financial risk; fixing it creates genuine value and user gratitude.
- **Renewal cycle creates annual re-engagement** — even low-engagement users return annually at renewal time, reducing churn.
- **Rising premiums increase demand** — as insurance costs surge (30% increase in 3 years), homeowners are increasingly motivated to shop around.
