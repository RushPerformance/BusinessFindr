## Problem

There are 44 million renter households in the US, and the average apartment hunt involves touring 5-10 apartments over 2-4 weeks. In competitive markets like New York, San Francisco, and Austin, renters may view 15-20 apartments and must make decisions within 24-48 hours of touring. A 2023 RentCafe survey found that 41% of renters felt overwhelmed during their apartment search, and 33% regretted their choice within 3 months — citing features they forgot to check or apartments they confused with each other. The current process involves a chaotic mix of Zillow screenshots, Notes app lists, text threads with partners/roommates, and fading memory. Application deadlines add pressure: the average application fee is $50-$75, and submitting multiple applications simultaneously (common in competitive markets) requires tracking different requirements, deadlines, and status updates across multiple landlords and management companies.

---

## Solution

1. **Apartment Profiles** — Create a profile for each apartment viewed with address, rent, square footage, bedrooms, bathrooms, move-in date, lease length, and application deadline.
2. **Photo and Screenshot Storage** — Attach listing photos, personal viewing photos, and listing screenshots to each apartment profile for easy reference.
3. **Feature Checklist** — Customizable checklist for each viewing: in-unit laundry, dishwasher, natural light, closet space, pet policy, parking, noise level, neighborhood feel, commute time.
4. **Side-by-Side Comparison** — Compare 2-4 apartments side-by-side on all tracked features, with a weighted scoring system based on your priorities.
5. **Pros and Cons Notes** — Free-form notes and voice memos captured during or immediately after each viewing, tagged to the specific apartment.
6. **Application Tracker** — Track application status (not started, submitted, under review, approved, denied) with deadline reminders and required document checklists.
7. **Roommate/Partner Collaboration** — Share the apartment journal with co-searchers so everyone can add notes, ratings, and preferences to the same list.
8. **Cost Calculator** — True monthly cost calculation including rent, utilities estimates, parking, pet fees, renters insurance, and commute costs for accurate comparison.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **Zillow/Apartments.com Saved Listings** — Save listings but no personal notes, no feature comparison, no application tracking, and listings disappear when rented.
2. **Notes App / Google Docs** — Flexible but unstructured; no comparison views, no application tracking, and hard to organize as the list grows.
3. **Spreadsheets** — More structured but no photo integration, cumbersome on mobile during viewings, and usually abandoned mid-search.
4. **Text Threads** — Sharing apartments with roommates via text works short-term but becomes unsearchable and overwhelming quickly.
5. **Pen and Paper** — Some organized renters bring notebooks to viewings but cannot attach photos, share with co-searchers, or generate comparisons.
