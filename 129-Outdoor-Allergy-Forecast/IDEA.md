## Problem

Over 50 million Americans suffer from seasonal allergies, spending $18 billion annually on treatment including $4.8 billion on medications alone. The American College of Allergy, Asthma & Immunology reports that allergies are the 6th leading cause of chronic illness in the US, causing 16.9 million doctor visits and 4 million missed workdays annually. Current allergy forecasts (from Weather.com, Pollen.com, or weather apps) provide generic "high pollen" alerts that are essentially useless — a person allergic to ragweed does not care about tree pollen levels, and someone sensitive to mold needs entirely different data than someone triggered by grass pollen. There are 1,500+ allergenic plant species in the US, and pollen counts vary dramatically by species, geography, and microclimate. Additionally, 80% of allergy sufferers take medication reactively (after symptoms begin) rather than proactively (before exposure), despite clinical evidence that pre-treatment reduces symptom severity by 60%.

---

## Solution

1. **Allergen Profile** — Users select their specific allergens (oak, birch, grass, ragweed, mold, dust mite) based on allergy test results or symptom history, creating a personalized sensitivity profile.
2. **Personalized Forecast** — Daily and 5-day forecasts showing levels specifically for the user's trigger allergens in their location, not generic pollen counts.
3. **Medication Reminders** — Proactive medication reminders triggered by forecast data — take antihistamines the morning before a high ragweed day, not after symptoms start.
4. **Symptom Diary** — Track daily symptoms (sneezing, congestion, itchy eyes, fatigue) and correlate with actual allergen levels to identify triggers and treatment effectiveness.
5. **Activity Planner** — Suggests optimal times for outdoor activities (running, gardening, playground visits) based on hourly allergen forecasts and wind patterns.
6. **Air Quality Integration** — Combines pollen data with EPA air quality index (AQI) and wildfire smoke data for a complete respiratory health forecast.
7. **Allergy Season Countdown** — Predicts when your specific allergy season starts and ends based on location, historical data, and current growing conditions.
8. **Provider Report** — Generate a symptom-and-exposure report for your allergist showing patterns between allergen levels and symptom severity over time.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **Pollen.com (IQVIA)** — Provides general pollen forecasts but not personalized to specific allergens; uses a generic 1-12 scale without species-level detail.
2. **Weather.com / AccuWeather** — Include allergy forecasts as a secondary feature with minimal detail, no personalization, and no medication integration.
3. **Zyrtec AllergyCast** — Branded allergy app but essentially a marketing tool for Zyrtec; generic forecasts with brand-specific medication promotion.
4. **Breezometer** — Air quality and pollen API provider but focused on B2B/developer market, not consumer-facing personalized forecasts.
5. **Allergist Appointments** — Allergists provide personalized guidance but at $150-$300/visit, typically only 2-4 times per year, not daily actionable data.

## Why This Wins

- **Personalization is the killer feature** — species-level pollen forecasts matched to individual allergen profiles is a step change from generic "high pollen" alerts.
- **Proactive medication timing** — clinical evidence shows 60% symptom reduction with pre-treatment; timing medication to forecasts delivers measurable health improvement.
- **Seasonal engagement pattern** — users engage intensely during their allergy season (3-6 months), with natural re-engagement each year as seasons return.
- **Pharma partnership potential** — OTC allergy medication brands (Zyrtec, Claritin, Allegra) will pay for targeted advertising to users experiencing peak symptoms.
- **Outdoor activity market overlap** — runners, cyclists, hikers, and gardeners with allergies represent an underserved, high-engagement niche.
