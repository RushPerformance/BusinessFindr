# Home Maintenance Calendar

> Personalized maintenance schedule for your specific home: reminds you to change HVAC filters, clean gutters, flush water heaters, and more — before expensive problems start.

## The Problem
The average homeowner spends $3,000-$4,000/year on home maintenance and repairs, but 35% of homeowners don't perform regular preventive maintenance (HomeAdvisor). A $30 HVAC filter change prevents a $5,000 compressor replacement. A $200 gutter cleaning prevents $10,000 in foundation damage. First-time homeowners (6M/year) are especially lost — they don't know what maintenance a house needs or when. r/homeowners and r/firsttimehomebuyer are dominated by "what should I be doing?" posts.

## The Solution
1. Enter home details: age, location, HVAC type, roof material, appliances, yard size
2. App generates a personalized annual maintenance calendar
3. Monthly task cards with: what to do, why it matters, how to DIY (video links), estimated cost if hiring
4. Push notification reminders tied to seasons and climate
5. Expense tracker: log what you spent on each maintenance task
6. Vendor directory: find rated local pros for tasks you don't want to DIY
7. Home value protection score: shows how maintenance protects resale value

## Existing Alternatives
- **Centriq**: Home manual/appliance tracker, limited maintenance scheduling
- **HomeZada**: $5.99/mo but complex, tries to do too much (inventory + finance + maintenance)
- **Thumbtack/Angi**: Service marketplaces, not maintenance planners
- **Google Calendar**: DIY approach, requires manual setup and research
- **Home binder templates**: PDF/spreadsheet checklists from real estate agents

## Why This Wins
- Personalization based on home specifics (climate, age, materials) is unique
- Preventive maintenance framing: "spend $30 now or $5,000 later"
- First-time homeowner segment is large, growing, and hungry for guidance
- Low churn: once configured, the app becomes your home's operating manual
