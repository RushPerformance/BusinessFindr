## Problem

There are 370,000 homeowners associations in the US governing 40 million homes, and every one of them is legally required to keep meeting minutes. Board meetings occur monthly or quarterly, producing 4-12 sets of minutes per year per association. Yet 92% of HOA board members are unpaid volunteers (Community Associations Institute), and taking accurate meeting minutes is consistently rated as the most disliked volunteer task. A 2023 CAI survey found that 45% of HOA boards have no designated secretary, 38% have experienced legal challenges where inadequate minutes were cited as evidence, and the average time to draft and distribute meeting minutes after a meeting is 11 days — far longer than the 48-72 hour best practice. Property management companies handle minutes for larger communities but charge $250-$500/month for management services, and many self-managed communities (55% of all HOAs) cannot afford professional management. The result is minutes that are late, inaccurate, incomplete, or missing entirely — creating legal liability and homeowner frustration.

---

## Solution

1. **Meeting Recording** — Record board meetings via phone or laptop with automatic speaker separation, capturing the full discussion for reference.
2. **AI-Powered Transcription** — Converts meeting recordings into full transcripts with speaker identification, timestamps, and agenda item tagging.
3. **Minutes Auto-Generation** — Generates formatted meeting minutes from the transcript, identifying motions, seconds, vote results, and action items automatically.
4. **Robert's Rules Compliance** — Ensures minutes follow Robert's Rules of Order formatting: call to order, roll call, approval of prior minutes, committee reports, old business, new business, adjournment.
5. **Action Item Tracker** — Extracts action items with assigned responsible parties and deadlines, carrying them forward to the next meeting agenda.
6. **Vote Record** — Records all motions with who moved, who seconded, and the vote count (for/against/abstain) in a legally compliant format.
7. **Homeowner Distribution** — One-click distribution of approved minutes to all homeowners via email, with an online archive accessible to the community.
8. **Meeting Archive** — Searchable archive of all past meeting minutes, recordings, and action items with full-text search across years of records.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **Property Management Companies** — Handle minutes as part of full management services but cost $250-$500/mo — overkill for communities that only need meeting documentation.
2. **Volunteer Board Secretary** — The default for self-managed HOAs; quality varies wildly, minutes are often late or inaccurate, and the role has high turnover.
3. **General Transcription Services (Otter.ai, Rev)** — Provide transcription but not formatted meeting minutes, no Robert's Rules compliance, and no action item extraction.
4. **HOA Management Software (AppFolio, Buildium)** — Full-featured property management platforms ($50-$200/mo) that include meeting features but are bloated and expensive for small communities.
5. **Google Docs / Microsoft Word** — Templates exist for meeting minutes but require manual note-taking during the meeting, missing the automation value entirely.
