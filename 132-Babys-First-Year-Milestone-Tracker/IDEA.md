## Problem

Approximately 3.6 million babies are born in the US each year, and 78% of first-time parents report feeling overwhelmed tracking their newborn's development across feeding schedules, sleep patterns, diaper output, and developmental milestones. The CDC's milestone checklist identifies 75+ key developmental markers in the first year alone, yet most parents rely on scattered notes, text messages to partners, and memory when visiting the pediatrician. Studies show that early identification of developmental delays (which affect roughly 1 in 6 children) leads to significantly better outcomes, but 40% of delays are not identified until school age because parents lack structured tracking tools. Additionally, 62% of new parents report sleep deprivation makes it nearly impossible to remember feeding times and amounts accurately.

---

## Solution

1. **CDC-Aligned Milestone Checklist** — Age-appropriate developmental milestone tracking based on CDC guidelines with explanations of what to watch for and when to discuss concerns with your pediatrician.
2. **Feeding Logger** — One-tap logging for breastfeeding (which side, duration), bottle feeding (formula type, amount), and solid food introduction with daily/weekly summary charts.
3. **Sleep Pattern Tracker** — Records naps and nighttime sleep with trend analysis showing total sleep hours, longest stretches, and pattern changes over time.
4. **Growth Charts** — WHO/CDC-standard growth curves for weight, length, and head circumference with percentile tracking and pediatrician-ready PDF exports.
5. **Diaper Tracker** — Wet and dirty diaper counting with alerts if output drops below pediatrician-recommended minimums (important newborn health indicator).
6. **Partner Sync** — Real-time sync between parents/caregivers so both know when baby last ate, slept, or had a diaper change without texting.
7. **Pediatrician Report** — Generates a clean summary report of all tracked data for well-baby visits, exportable as PDF with date ranges.
8. **Photo Timeline** — Monthly milestone photos with side-by-side growth comparison and optional sharing with family.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **Huckleberry** — Popular sleep tracking app with "SweetSpot" sleep predictions, but limited milestone tracking and $9.99/mo premium pricing feels steep.
2. **Baby Tracker (Nighp Software)** — Comprehensive logging app but overwhelming UI with too many features crammed into cluttered screens.
3. **Glow Baby** — Part of the Glow suite, solid tracking but aggressive upselling to premium and data privacy concerns.
4. **The Wonder Weeks** — Focuses on "leaps" and developmental phases but no feeding/sleep/diaper tracking — purely educational.
5. **CDC Milestone Tracker** — Free government app with milestone checklists but no feeding, sleep, or growth tracking capabilities.
