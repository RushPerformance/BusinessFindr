## Problem

Property taxes represent the single largest recurring expense for most homeowners, averaging $2,690/year nationally but exceeding $8,000/year in high-tax states like New Jersey, Connecticut, and Illinois. Studies indicate that 30-60% of all properties in the US are over-assessed, yet only about 5% of homeowners ever file an appeal. The reason: the process is confusing, varies dramatically by jurisdiction (3,000+ county assessors), and most homeowners don't know how to find comparable sales data to support their case. Those who do appeal win reductions 40-60% of the time, saving an average of $500-$1,500 per year. Professional property tax appeal firms charge $300-$1,000+ or take 30-50% of the first year's savings, putting professional help out of reach for average homeowners.

---

## Solution

1. **Assessment Analysis** — Pulls your current property tax assessment from public records and compares it to recent comparable sales within a 1-mile radius to determine if you're over-assessed.
2. **Comparable Sales Finder** — Automatically identifies 5-10 comparable properties that sold below your assessed value, with adjustments for square footage, lot size, condition, and age.
3. **Appeal Form Generator** — Pre-fills your county's specific appeal forms with property details, comparable data, and supporting arguments — covering 500+ US counties at launch.
4. **Deadline Tracker** — Monitors your county's appeal filing deadlines (which vary from 30 to 120 days after assessment notices) and sends timely reminders.
5. **Evidence Package Builder** — Compiles photos, comparable sales printouts, and a written argument into a professional PDF package ready to submit.
6. **Hearing Prep Guide** — Step-by-step guidance for informal and formal hearing processes, including what to say, what to bring, and common mistakes to avoid.
7. **Savings Calculator** — Shows estimated annual and 5-year savings if your appeal succeeds, helping you decide if it's worth pursuing.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **Ownwell** — Full-service property tax appeal company that handles everything but takes 25-35% of savings as their fee, making it expensive for moderate over-assessments.
2. **PTAX (Property Tax Advisors)** — Traditional consulting firms charging $500+ retainers, primarily serving commercial property owners.
3. **County Assessor Websites** — Free but confusing, with no comparable sales analysis and forms that vary wildly by jurisdiction.
4. **TurboTax Property Tax Tools** — Limited to deduction tracking, not appeal assistance.
5. **Local Real Estate Agents** — Some help informally with comps but don't specialize in appeals and can't prepare formal documentation.

## Why This Wins

- **Self-service at 1/10th the cost** — At $29.99 vs. $300-1,000 for professional services, homeowners keep 100% of their savings instead of sharing 30-50% with a firm.
- **Jurisdiction-specific automation** — Pre-filled forms for 500+ counties eliminate the most intimidating part of the process — figuring out what to file and where.
- **Data-driven confidence** — Automatically pulls comparable sales data so homeowners can see objectively whether they have a strong case before investing time.
- **Recession-proof demand** — When home values drop (as in 2008-2011), appeal volumes surge 300-400% as assessments lag behind market reality — this app thrives in downturns.
- **One-time purchase model** — No subscription fatigue; homeowners pay only when they need it, typically once every 2-3 years.
