## Problem

The US home improvement and repair market is worth $510 billion annually, yet homeowners have almost zero pricing transparency when hiring contractors or handymen. A survey by HomeAdvisor found that 72% of homeowners feel unsure whether a repair quote is fair, and 45% have paid significantly more than average market rates for common repairs. The same plumbing fix can cost $150 in rural Alabama and $650 in San Francisco, but homeowners have no easy way to know their local market rate. Contractor pricing is deliberately opaque — only 12% of handyman services publish pricing online. This information asymmetry costs American homeowners an estimated $36 billion annually in overpayment, and it also prevents many from getting needed repairs done because they assume costs are higher than they actually are.

---

## Solution

1. **Zip Code Pricing Database** — Searchable database of average costs for 200+ common home repairs organized by zip code, including low/average/high ranges.
2. **Quote Comparison Tool** — Enter a contractor's quote and instantly see how it compares to the local market rate, with a "fair/high/very high" rating.
3. **Job Scope Estimator** — Describes what a standard job should include so homeowners can spot missing items or unnecessary upsells in quotes.
4. **Seasonal Pricing Trends** — Shows when repair costs typically drop (e.g., HVAC servicing in spring vs. emergency calls in winter) to help homeowners time non-urgent repairs.
5. **DIY vs. Hire Calculator** — For each repair type, estimates DIY cost (materials + tools) vs. professional cost, with difficulty ratings and time estimates.
6. **Material Cost Tracker** — Current prices for common materials (lumber, drywall, copper pipe) from local Home Depot/Lowe's, so homeowners can evaluate the materials portion of a quote.
7. **Negotiation Tips** — Specific negotiation strategies for each repair type, including what to ask for, common discounts, and red flags.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **HomeAdvisor/Angi** — Provides cost guides but primarily a lead-generation platform for contractors, creating incentive to overstate costs to justify referral fees.
2. **Thumbtack** — Connects homeowners with pros and shows price ranges, but ranges are extremely broad and not localized to zip code level.
3. **Fixr.com** — Good cost estimation website but desktop-only, no mobile app, and no quote comparison features.
4. **HomeWyse** — Detailed cost calculators with zip code adjustment but dated UI, no app, and calculations feel overly complex for simple questions.
5. **Google Search** — Most homeowners just Google "how much does X cost" but get national averages that can be 50-200% off from local rates.

---

## Why This Wins

- **Hyper-local accuracy** — Zip code-level pricing versus competitors' broad regional or national averages, using data from actual contractor invoices and municipal permit records.
- **One-time purchase simplicity** — $4.99 once is an impulse buy that removes subscription friction — homeowners use it when they need it without worrying about canceling.
- **Quote validation** — The "is this quote fair?" feature is uniquely action-oriented — no competitor lets you paste a real quote for instant market comparison.
- **DIY cost comparison** — Helping homeowners decide whether to hire or DIY is a decision no competitor helps with quantitatively.
- **Offline access** — Downloaded pricing data works without internet, useful when getting quotes at the hardware store or during contractor visits.
