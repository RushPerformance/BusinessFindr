## Problem

Over 131 million Americans — 66% of all adults — take at least one prescription medication, and 40% take five or more. Adverse drug interactions send 1.3 million Americans to the emergency room annually, causing an estimated 125,000 deaths per year and $30 billion in unnecessary healthcare costs. Yet 67% of patients don't know about potential interactions between their medications, and 55% of adults take at least one supplement that could interact with their prescriptions. Pharmacists catch some interactions, but with average consultation times of 90 seconds, complex multi-drug regimens are frequently missed. The problem is worst for seniors: adults over 65 take an average of 7 medications and see 4+ different prescribers who may not have complete medication lists.

---

## Solution

1. **Medication Scanner** — Scan pill bottles, medication packaging, or prescription labels with your phone camera to instantly add medications to your profile.
2. **Interaction Matrix** — Checks all medication pairs for known interactions (major, moderate, minor) using FDA and medical database cross-referencing.
3. **Food & Beverage Warnings** — Flags specific food interactions (grapefruit, dairy, alcohol, leafy greens with blood thinners) with clear explanations.
4. **Optimal Timing Schedule** — Creates a personalized daily medication schedule considering absorption requirements (empty stomach, with food, spacing requirements).
5. **Supplement Screening** — Checks OTC supplements, vitamins, and herbal remedies against prescription medications for hidden interactions.
6. **Pharmacist Summary** — Generates a clean medication list with interaction concerns to share with your pharmacist or doctor at appointments.
7. **Refill Reminders** — Tracks medication supply and sends reminders before you run out, with pharmacy hours for your preferred location.
8. **Caregiver Sharing** — Allows family members or caregivers to view medication lists and receive alerts for elderly family members.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **Drugs.com Interaction Checker** — Comprehensive web-based tool but no mobile-first experience, no scheduling, and no camera scanning capability.
2. **Medisafe** — Popular pill reminder app with some interaction checking, but interaction data is limited and premium costs $9.99/mo.
3. **Epocrates** — Medical professional drug reference with interaction checker, but designed for doctors, not patients — UI is impenetrable for consumers.
4. **WebMD App** — Includes a basic interaction checker but buried deep in the app, with limited food interaction data and no scheduling features.
5. **GoodRx** — Focused on price comparison and coupons, not interaction checking or medication management.

## Why This Wins

- **Camera-first input** — Scanning pill bottles is dramatically easier than manually typing drug names with complex spellings, making it accessible to elderly users.
- **Holistic checking** — Includes supplements and food interactions, not just Rx-to-Rx, which is where 40% of dangerous interactions hide.
- **Plain-language explanations** — Medical database results translated into clear, actionable language ("take this 2 hours apart from your calcium supplement" vs. "chelation interaction noted").
- **Affordable premium** — At $3.99/mo vs. Medisafe's $9.99/mo, the premium tier is accessible to seniors on fixed incomes.
- **Caregiver features** — Multi-profile support lets adult children manage aging parents' medications remotely — a feature none of the free checkers offer well.
