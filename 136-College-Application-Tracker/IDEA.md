## Problem

Each year, 3.7 million US high school seniors apply to college, submitting an average of 7-10 applications each. That means tracking 25-40 unique deadlines per student across Early Decision, Early Action, Regular Decision, and Rolling Admission timelines. A College Board survey found that 38% of students miss at least one deadline during the application process, and 52% of parents report the process as more stressful than buying a home. Financial aid alone involves coordinating FAFSA, CSS Profile, institutional aid forms, and merit scholarship applications — each with different deadlines and requirements. Meanwhile, 61% of students manage this entire process using spreadsheets, sticky notes, or their memory. School counselors handle an average caseload of 415 students each, making individualized support nearly impossible.

---

## Solution

1. **Application Dashboard** — Central hub showing all target schools with application status (not started, in progress, submitted, decision received), deadlines, and requirements at a glance.
2. **Deadline Calendar** — Auto-populated deadlines for 4,000+ US colleges organized by Early Decision, Early Action, Regular Decision, and Rolling Admission with push notification reminders.
3. **Essay Tracker** — Manages essay prompts by school, tracks word counts and completion status, identifies which essays can be reused across applications.
4. **Recommendation Manager** — Tracks which teachers/counselors have been asked, accepted, and submitted letters, with gentle reminder email templates.
5. **Financial Aid Organizer** — FAFSA, CSS Profile, and school-specific aid deadlines with document checklists and status tracking for each school.
6. **Decision Comparison Matrix** — Side-by-side comparison of acceptance offers including cost of attendance, financial aid packages, program rankings, and campus characteristics.
7. **Parent Dashboard** — Separate view for parents showing progress, upcoming deadlines, and financial aid status without full editing access.
8. **Document Vault** — Secure storage for transcripts, test scores, activity lists, and essays accessible across all applications.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **Common App** — Manages the application itself but provides zero organizational tools for tracking deadlines, essays, or financial aid across schools.
2. **Naviance/Scoir** — School-purchased platforms with college matching but clunky interfaces and limited student-facing organizational features.
3. **College Abacus** — Net price comparison tool but no application management, deadline tracking, or essay organization.
4. **Google Sheets Templates** — Free but requires manual setup, no deadline notifications, and breaks down as complexity grows beyond 5-6 schools.
5. **Going Merry** — Scholarship search platform with some application tracking, but narrowly focused on financial aid rather than full application management.

## Why This Wins

- **Purpose-built for students** — Unlike school-purchased platforms (Naviance/Scoir) that serve administrators first, this is designed entirely around the student's workflow.
- **Seasonal pricing model** — $9.99/mo for just the 6-month application season (July-January) feels fair and avoids year-round subscription fatigue.
- **Essay reuse detection** — Automatically identifies which supplemental essay prompts are similar enough to reuse responses, saving students 10+ hours of writing.
- **Financial aid comparison** — The decision matrix normalizes financial aid packages so families can truly compare the real cost of attendance, not just sticker price.
- **Parent access without overreach** — The separate parent dashboard addresses family dynamics elegantly — parents can see progress without micromanaging.
