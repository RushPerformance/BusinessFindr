## Problem

Package theft affects 49 million Americans annually, with 64% of online shoppers reporting at least one stolen package. The average stolen package is worth $112, costing consumers a total of $5.4 billion per year. Meanwhile, there are over 100,000 alternative pickup locations across the US — Amazon Lockers, UPS Access Points, FedEx Hold locations, USPS PO boxes, Parcel Pending lockers, and retailer pickup points — but they're scattered across different carrier apps and websites with no unified view. Apartment dwellers are particularly affected: 43% of apartment residents have had packages stolen from lobbies, and many buildings lack secure package rooms. Remote workers and travelers face similar challenges when they're away from home during deliveries. Despite the explosion of alternatives, 71% of consumers don't know about pickup options within a mile of their home.

---

## Solution

1. **Unified Locker Map** — Aggregates all package pickup alternatives (Amazon Locker, UPS Access Point, FedEx Hold, USPS, Parcel Pending, Luxer One, retailer lockers) onto a single searchable map.
2. **Proximity Search** — Find the closest pickup option to your home, office, gym, or any saved location with walking/driving time estimates.
3. **Carrier Filtering** — Filter by carrier compatibility (Amazon, UPS, FedEx, USPS) since not all lockers accept all carriers.
4. **Hours & Availability** — Shows real-time hours of operation, current capacity status where available, and holiday schedules.
5. **Size Guide** — Indicates which locations can handle oversized packages vs. standard boxes vs. envelopes only.
6. **Favorite Locations** — Save preferred pickup spots and get the locker code/address formatted for easy copy-paste during online checkout.
7. **Theft Heatmap** — Crowdsourced porch piracy reports by neighborhood, helping users decide when to ship to home vs. a locker.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **Amazon App** — Shows Amazon Locker locations but only for Amazon deliveries; no UPS, FedEx, or USPS alternatives.
2. **UPS My Choice / FedEx Delivery Manager** — Carrier-specific apps that only show their own network's pickup locations.
3. **Google Maps** — Can search for "package lockers" but results are incomplete, often outdated, and lack carrier compatibility info.
4. **Parcel Pending / Luxer One Apps** — Building-specific locker apps that only work for their installed hardware, not carrier-agnostic.
5. **PackageGuard** — Anti-theft hardware solution (porch lockbox) rather than a pickup alternative finder.

---

## Why This Wins

- **True aggregation** — The only app combining ALL carrier pickup networks into a single map, solving the fragmentation problem that forces users to check 4-5 different apps.
- **Zero-friction free model** — Ad-supported with no subscription barrier means maximum adoption; the value is in the network effect and data.
- **Copy-paste convenience** — Formatted locker addresses ready to paste into checkout fields removes the friction of using alternative pickup points.
- **Theft heatmap motivation** — Showing users their neighborhood's theft risk creates an emotional hook that drives adoption and retention.
- **Partnership potential** — Locker providers and carriers have incentive to promote the app since higher locker utilization benefits their unit economics.
