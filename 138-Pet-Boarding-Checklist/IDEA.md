## Problem

78 million US households own pets, and 68% of pet owners travel at least once per year, creating 53 million annual boarding or pet-sitting occasions. Yet 41% of pet owners report significant stress around boarding preparation — gathering vaccination records, communicating feeding schedules, medication instructions, emergency vet contacts, and behavioral notes. Boarding facilities report that 35% of drop-offs are delayed because owners forget required vaccination records (rabies, bordetella, DHPP), and 28% of pet emergencies during boarding are complicated by incomplete medical information. Pet sitters receive critical instructions via scattered text messages that get buried in conversation threads. The problem is especially acute for first-time boarders: 62% of new pet owners don't know what documentation and supplies are needed.

---

## Solution

1. **Smart Boarding Checklist** — Dynamic checklist customized by pet type (dog, cat, exotic), boarding type (kennel, pet sitter, friend), and trip duration with nothing overlooked.
2. **Vet Record Vault** — Store vaccination records, medical history, medication schedules, and allergy information in shareable digital format accepted by most boarding facilities.
3. **Emergency Contact Card** — Generates a printable/digital card with emergency vet info, poison control numbers, pet insurance details, and owner contact information.
4. **Care Instructions Sheet** — Creates a professional one-page care guide covering feeding schedule, medication timing, exercise needs, behavioral notes, and comfort items.
5. **Packing List Generator** — Customized packing list for your pet based on duration, facility type, and pet needs (food, medications, favorite toys, bedding).
6. **Boarding Facility Comparison** — Rate and compare local boarding options based on price, services, vaccination requirements, and user reviews.
7. **Return Home Checklist** — Post-boarding checklist for reintegration: health check items to watch for, readjustment tips, and follow-up vet visit reminders.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **Rover** — Pet sitting marketplace focused on finding sitters, not on boarding preparation or document organization.
2. **PetDesk** — Vet-facing app for appointment management; some pet record storage but not boarding-oriented.
3. **Google Keep/Notes** — Many owners create manual checklists but miss critical items and can't share vet records digitally.
4. **11pets** — Pet care tracking app with some health records but overly complex for the simple boarding prep use case.
5. **Boarding facility intake forms** — Paper-based, must be filled out fresh each time, and don't help with preparation or packing.

## Why This Wins

- **Singular focus** — While competitors try to be all-purpose pet apps, this solves exactly one stressful moment exceptionally well: preparing your pet to be cared for by someone else.
- **Impulse-buy pricing** — $2.99 one-time is less than a bag of dog treats, making it a no-brainer purchase when boarding stress hits.
- **Shareable care guides** — The professional care instruction sheet is something pet sitters and boarding facilities actually appreciate receiving, creating word-of-mouth growth.
- **Reusable vet records** — Once vaccination records are digitized, they're available for every future boarding, grooming, or daycare visit, creating lasting value.
- **Peace of mind** — The emotional value of knowing nothing was forgotten far exceeds the $2.99 price point for anxious pet parents.
