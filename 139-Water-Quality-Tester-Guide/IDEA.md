## Problem

Over 77 million Americans are served by water systems that violate federal health standards, according to the Natural Resources Defense Council. Lead contamination alone affects 22 million Americans, and PFAS ("forever chemicals") have been detected in the drinking water of 200+ million people. Yet most homeowners have no idea what's in their tap water — only 14% have ever tested it. Municipal water quality reports (Consumer Confidence Reports) are published annually but are dense, technical, and often 12+ months out of date. The 43 million Americans on private wells have no government testing at all and are entirely responsible for their own water safety. When homeowners do decide to test, they face a confusing market of 50+ testing kit brands, ranging from $15 strips that test 3 contaminants to $500 lab tests that cover 100+, with no guidance on which is appropriate for their area.

---

## Solution

1. **Local Contaminant Risk Profile** — Enter your zip code and water source (municipal/well) to see which contaminants are most likely based on local geology, industrial activity, and historical violations.
2. **Test Kit Recommender** — Recommends the right testing kit based on your risk profile and budget, from $15 basic strips to comprehensive lab panels.
3. **Test Result Interpreter** — Photograph or enter your test results and get plain-language explanations of what each number means vs. EPA maximums and health advisories.
4. **Filtration Matchmaker** — Based on your specific contaminants, recommends the right type of filter (pitcher, under-sink, whole-house, reverse osmosis) with product comparisons.
5. **Well Water Guide** — Specialized guidance for the 43M Americans on private wells, including seasonal testing schedules and contamination sources unique to well water.
6. **Municipal Report Decoder** — Pulls and translates your city's annual water quality report into understandable terms with highlighted areas of concern.
7. **Baby-Safe Water Checklist** — Specialized guidance for households with infants covering nitrate levels, lead concerns, and formula preparation safety.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **EWG Tap Water Database** — Free online database of municipal water test results, but no personalized guidance, no filter recommendations, and doesn't cover private wells.
2. **Tap Score (SimpleWater)** — Lab testing service starting at $99, excellent quality but expensive and provides results without actionable filtration recommendations.
3. **Water Quality Reports (EPA)** — Free municipal reports but incomprehensible to most consumers and only cover public water systems.
4. **Amazon Test Kit Reviews** — Homeowners browse kit reviews with no context about which contaminants they should actually be testing for.
5. **Local health departments** — Free or cheap well water testing in some areas, but limited contaminants tested and weeks-long wait for results.
