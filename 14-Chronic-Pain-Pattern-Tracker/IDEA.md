## The Problem
50 million US adults live with chronic pain, 20 million with "high-impact" chronic pain that limits daily activities (CDC, 2023). Patients spend years trying to identify what makes their pain better or worse. Doctors ask "what triggers your flares?" and patients guess. The reality is that triggers are personal and multi-factorial: a combination of weather changes + poor sleep + certain foods might trigger a flare, but no single factor alone does. Current pain tracking apps are just glorified journals — they let you log pain but don't analyze patterns or correlate with external data.

## The Solution
1. Quick daily check-in (30 seconds): pain level (0-10), location, quality (burning/stabbing/aching), medications taken
2. Auto-logs: weather (pressure, humidity, temperature), sleep (via Apple Health/Fitbit), step count
3. Optional logs: food, stress level, menstrual cycle, specific activities
4. AI pattern analysis: "Your pain increases 65% when barometric pressure drops AND you slept less than 6 hours"
5. Trigger alerts: "Pressure dropping tonight — high flare risk tomorrow"
6. Doctor-ready reports: printable summaries with charts showing correlations over time
7. Medication effectiveness tracking: "Ibuprofen reduces your pain by 2.1 points on average within 45 minutes"

- **Migraine Buddy**: Migraine-specific, not general chronic pain
- **Paper journals**: Extremely common, impossible to analyze for patterns
