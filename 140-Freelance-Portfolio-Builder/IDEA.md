---

## Problem

There are 73.3 million freelancers in the US (36% of the workforce), and 89% say a professional portfolio is the #1 factor in winning new clients. Yet 47% of freelancers don't have a portfolio website because building one requires web development skills, design ability, and ongoing maintenance they'd rather spend on billable work. The average freelancer spends 15-20 hours creating a portfolio site and $200-500/year on hosting, domain, and website builder subscriptions. Even with tools like WordPress, Squarespace, and Wix, freelancers struggle with layout decisions, project presentation, and mobile optimization. Meanwhile, 63% of potential clients say they've passed on a freelancer specifically because their portfolio was unprofessional or nonexistent. Testimonial collection is another pain point — 72% of freelancers say they rarely ask for testimonials because the process feels awkward.

---

## Solution

1. **AI Portfolio Generator** — Upload work samples, describe your services, and get a professional portfolio website generated in minutes with industry-specific layouts for designers, developers, writers, photographers, etc.
2. **Smart Project Pages** — Each project gets a detailed case study page with before/after, process descriptions, results metrics, and client attribution — auto-formatted from simple inputs.
3. **Testimonial Collector** — Automated testimonial request emails sent to past clients with a simple form, automatic display on portfolio with client approval.
4. **Custom Domain Support** — Connect your own domain name (yourname.com) with free SSL certificate and professional email forwarding.
5. **Analytics Dashboard** — Track portfolio visitors, time on page, most-viewed projects, and referral sources to understand what's attracting clients.
6. **Lead Capture** — Built-in contact form with project brief questions, budget ranges, and timeline expectations that qualify leads before you respond.
7. **Social Proof Integration** — Automatically pulls and displays ratings from Upwork, Fiverr, and LinkedIn recommendations alongside your portfolio.
8. **One-Click Update** — Add new projects from mobile with photo upload, quick description, and automatic portfolio refresh without touching code.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **Squarespace** — Beautiful templates but generic, not portfolio-specific, and $16-23/mo is pricey for freelancers starting out.
2. **Behance/Dribbble** — Creative community portfolios but limited to design fields, you don't own your URL, and you're competing with thousands on the same platform.
3. **WordPress** — Maximum flexibility but requires technical knowledge, plugin management, security updates, and 10+ hours to set up properly.
4. **Carrd** — Simple, cheap one-page sites but too basic for a professional portfolio — no project detail pages, no testimonials, no analytics.
5. **Carbonmade/Crevado** — Portfolio-specific builders but dated designs, limited templates, and minimal features beyond basic image galleries.

---

## Why This Wins

- **Freelancer-specific intelligence** — Templates and layouts designed for specific freelance niches (developer portfolios look different from photographer portfolios) rather than generic business sites.
- **Testimonial automation** — The automated request and collection workflow removes the social awkwardness of asking for testimonials, which no website builder addresses.
- **Price-performance sweet spot** — At $7.99/mo (half of Squarespace), freelancers get a portfolio-specific tool that's faster to set up and better optimized for client conversion.
- **Built-in lead qualification** — Contact forms with budget ranges and project scopes mean freelancers stop wasting time on $50 inquiries for $5,000 services.
- **Mobile updates** — Adding a new project from your phone after a client meeting takes 3 minutes, not the 30-minute desktop session other builders require.
