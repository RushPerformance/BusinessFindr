## Problem

The average American owns 103 items of clothing, yet wears only 20% of them regularly. In four-season climates, seasonal wardrobe rotation is a biannual chore that 67% of adults dread. The typical closet swap takes 3-5 hours, and without a system, people routinely forget what's in storage — leading to redundant purchases of items they already own. Americans spend $368 billion annually on clothing, and studies suggest 30-40% of those purchases are duplicates of forgotten items or impulse buys to replace things they can't find. Small-space living compounds the problem: the average US closet is 25 square feet, and apartment dwellers in major cities often have half that. The fast fashion waste cycle is also driven partly by poor wardrobe management — 85% of textiles end up in landfills, and the average American discards 81 pounds of clothing per year, much of it barely worn.

---

## Solution

1. **Digital Closet Inventory** — Photograph and catalog every clothing item with automated category tagging (tops, bottoms, outerwear, etc.), color detection, and season assignment.
2. **Seasonal Rotation Scheduler** — Reminds you when it's time to swap based on local weather patterns, not arbitrary calendar dates, with a guided step-by-step rotation process.
3. **Storage Box Tracker** — Assign items to labeled storage bins/bags with a searchable index so you always know exactly which box your winter boots are in.
4. **"Haven't Worn" Alerts** — Flags items you haven't worn in 6+ months with suggestions to donate, sell, or try in new outfits before the next rotation.
5. **Outfit Planner** — Mix-and-match outfit suggestions from your current season's wardrobe, including weather-appropriate combinations.
6. **Donation/Resale Helper** — Items flagged for removal get estimated resale values and one-tap listing to Poshmark, ThredUp, or local donation center locator.
7. **Shopping Gap Analysis** — Identifies actual wardrobe gaps (e.g., "you have no waterproof layer for fall") vs. things you already own, preventing duplicate purchases.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **Cladwell** — Outfit suggestion app with closet digitization, but $7.99/mo pricing is steep and no storage/rotation tracking features.
2. **Stylebook** — Well-designed closet app for iOS but no seasonal rotation features, no storage tracking, and no sustainability-focused features.
3. **Whering** — UK-based wardrobe app with outfit planning but limited US user base and no seasonal rotation or storage management.
4. **Acloset** — Basic closet inventory app with outfit planning but dated UI, no smart rotation, and no "haven't worn" analytics.
5. **Physical wardrobe organization (Marie Kondo method)** — Books and manual systems that require discipline but provide no ongoing tracking or digital inventory.

## Why This Wins

- **Storage tracking is unique** — No closet app tells you which storage box an item is in, solving the most frustrating part of seasonal rotation.
- **Weather-triggered rotation** — Smart timing based on actual local weather patterns rather than calendar dates means you rotate at exactly the right time.
- **Sustainability angle** — "Haven't worn" alerts and shopping gap analysis directly reduce unnecessary purchases, appealing to eco-conscious consumers.
- **Affordable entry point** — $2.99/mo is accessible enough to maintain during months when active rotation isn't happening, reducing churn.
- **Anti-fast-fashion positioning** — Marketing around reducing waste and shopping smarter resonates with the growing segment of consumers who want to buy less but better.
