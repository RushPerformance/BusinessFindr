---

## Problem

Local events are scattered across 10+ platforms with no unified discovery experience. A single weekend's activities might be listed across Facebook Events, Eventbrite, Meetup, library websites, parks department calendars, local newspaper event pages, venue websites, and Instagram stories — and none of these platforms show each other's events. Research shows 74% of people say they'd attend more local events if they could easily find them, and 56% have missed events they would have attended because they didn't know about them until afterward. New residents are particularly affected: 68% of people who move to a new city say finding local events and community activities is their biggest social challenge. Event organizers suffer too — small venues and community organizations report that fragmented listings mean 40% of their marketing effort is spent just posting the same event to multiple platforms.

---

## Solution

1. **Unified Event Feed** — Aggregates events from Facebook, Eventbrite, Meetup, library systems, parks departments, local news, and venue websites into a single chronological and filterable feed.
2. **Smart Categorization** — Auto-tags events by type (music, food, family, sports, arts, networking, outdoor, free) with filters for date, distance, price, and age-appropriateness.
3. **Personalized Recommendations** — Learns your interests from saved and attended events to surface discoveries you'd otherwise miss.
4. **Free Events Spotlight** — Dedicated section highlighting free community events (library programs, park events, gallery openings) that are hardest to discover.
5. **Family Filter** — One-tap filter showing only kid-friendly events with age range tags, helping parents plan weekend activities.
6. **Social Planning** — Share events with friends, see which events your connections are attending, and coordinate group attendance.
7. **Event Organizer Tools** — Free event submission for local organizers not on major platforms, plus analytics on views and saves.
8. **Weekly Digest** — Customizable email/notification digest of upcoming events matching your interests and location.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **Facebook Events** — Largest event database but requires a Facebook account, heavy algorithmic filtering buries smaller events, and doesn't include non-Facebook-listed events.
2. **Eventbrite** — Strong for ticketed events but misses free community events, library programs, and events on other platforms.
3. **Meetup** — Focused on recurring group activities rather than one-time events, with a $16/mo organizer fee that deters small groups.
4. **Google Events** — Appears in Google Search results but inconsistent coverage, no app, and no personalization or social features.
5. **Local newspaper event calendars** — Curated but only cover a fraction of events, usually behind paywalls, and no mobile-first experience.

---

## Why This Wins

- **True aggregation across all platforms** — No existing app pulls from Facebook, Eventbrite, Meetup, AND local government/library calendars simultaneously.
- **Free event emphasis** — Highlighting free community events serves an underserved need and differentiates from Eventbrite's ticketed-event focus.
- **Family-friendly filtering** — Parents spend hours searching for age-appropriate weekend activities; a one-tap family filter is a killer feature competitors overlook.
- **New resident acquisition** — Partnering with relocation services and apartment complexes provides a natural acquisition channel of highly motivated users.
- **Local organizer network effects** — When small organizers use the free submission tool, they promote the app to their audiences, creating viral growth loops.
