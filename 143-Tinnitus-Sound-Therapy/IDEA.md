## Problem

Tinnitus — the perception of ringing, buzzing, or hissing in the ears without an external source — affects approximately 50 million Americans (15% of the population), with 20 million experiencing chronic tinnitus and 2 million finding it debilitating. There is no cure for tinnitus, making sound therapy the primary management strategy recommended by 90% of audiologists. Yet professional sound therapy devices cost $1,500-6,000, and audiologist-guided therapy programs run $2,000-5,000 for a course of treatment. Generic white noise apps don't address tinnitus specifically — they play broadband noise rather than targeting the specific frequency of a patient's tinnitus, which research shows is 40-60% more effective at providing relief. Sleep disruption is the #1 complaint among tinnitus sufferers, with 67% reporting difficulty falling asleep and 48% experiencing regular sleep interruption.

---

## Solution

1. **Frequency Matcher** — Guided assessment to identify your specific tinnitus frequency (pitch matching) so sound therapy can be precisely targeted for maximum masking effectiveness.
2. **Customizable Sound Library** — 100+ therapeutic sounds across categories: notched noise, nature sounds, ambient environments, musical tones, and broadband options — all tunable to your tinnitus frequency.
3. **Sleep Mode** — Gradual volume reduction timer, pillow speaker optimization, and sounds specifically designed for sleep onset with tinnitus, including bedtime sound routines.
4. **Notched Sound Therapy** — Plays music or sounds with your tinnitus frequency "notched out," a research-backed technique shown to reduce tinnitus perception over time.
5. **Daily Relief Tracker** — Log tinnitus severity, triggers (stress, caffeine, noise exposure), and therapy effectiveness to identify patterns and optimize your treatment.
6. **Mixing Board** — Layer multiple sounds (rain + brown noise + gentle tone) with individual volume controls to create your perfect masking blend.
7. **Offline Playback** — Download your custom sound profiles for airplane mode, areas without service, or to reduce data usage during all-night playback.
8. **Audiologist Report** — Generates a summary of your frequency profile, usage patterns, and relief scores to share with your hearing care provider.
