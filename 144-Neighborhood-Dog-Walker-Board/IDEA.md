## Problem

There are 65 million dog-owning households in the US, and the vast majority walk their dogs within a 1-mile radius of home — yet most dog owners don't know other dog owners in their neighborhood beyond chance encounters at the park. Research shows that dog walking is one of the strongest predictors of neighborhood social connection, but 58% of dog owners walk alone because they don't know who else walks the same routes at similar times. Meanwhile, lost pet situations are time-critical — 10 million pets are lost annually, and the first 24 hours are crucial for recovery, yet neighborhood-specific alert systems are limited to stapling flyers to poles. Dog owners also share practical concerns: 37% of park-goers report conflicts with off-leash dogs, and communities lack organized ways to communicate about dog-friendly spaces, temporary closures, or hazards (poisoned bait reports, coyote sightings).

---

## Solution

1. **Neighborhood Walk Board** — Post your usual walking route and time to find neighbors who walk similar schedules for group walks and socialization.
2. **Dog Profiles** — Create profiles for your dogs with breed, temperament, size, and play style so you can find compatible walking companions.
3. **Lost Pet Alerts** — Instant push notifications to all users within a customizable radius when a pet is reported lost, with photo, description, and last-seen location.
4. **Hazard Reports** — Community-reported alerts for hazards: toxic bait, aggressive wildlife, broken fences, icy sidewalks, or off-leash dog complaints.
5. **Poop Bag Stations** — Crowdsourced map of poop bag dispensers, dog-friendly water fountains, and waste bins along common walking routes.
6. **Dog-Friendly Spot Guide** — User-rated database of dog-friendly parks, trails, patios, stores, and off-leash areas in your neighborhood.
7. **Playdate Matcher** — Connect dogs of similar size, age, and temperament for backyard or park playdates when owners want more than a walk.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **Nextdoor** — Has pet sections but they're buried among general neighborhood complaints, not purpose-built for dog owners.
2. **BarkHappy** — Dog-focused social network but primarily for finding dog-friendly businesses, not neighborhood walking groups.
3. **Rover** — Professional dog walking marketplace, not a community platform for neighbors walking their own dogs together.
4. **Pawscout** — Pet tracking tag with community features, but requires hardware purchase and community features are secondary.
5. **Facebook Groups** — Local dog owner groups exist but are unstructured, hard to discover, and cluttered with unrelated content.

---

## Why This Wins

- **Hyperlocal by design** — Everything is within walking distance of your home, making connections immediately actionable rather than aspirational.
- **Lost pet alerts are a killer feature** — The emotional urgency of a lost pet drives rapid adoption and ensures users keep notifications enabled.
- **Dog compatibility matching** — Matching by dog temperament and size prevents bad encounters, which no general social platform considers.
- **Community safety value** — Hazard reporting (toxic bait, coyote sightings) provides genuine safety value that keeps users engaged even when not actively seeking social walks.
- **Low price point for premium** — $1.99/mo for enhanced features like extended alert radius and premium dog profiles is low enough to feel like supporting the community rather than a subscription burden.
