## Problem

There are 35 million fully remote workers in the US, and an additional 41 million work hybrid arrangements. Multi-state tax obligations have become a maze: 14 states have "convenience of the employer" rules that can tax remote workers even if they never set foot in the office state, and each state has different thresholds (ranging from 1 day to 60 days) for triggering filing requirements. A survey by the American Institute of CPAs found that 63% of remote workers are unaware of their multi-state filing obligations, and 41% have likely underpaid state taxes without knowing it. The IRS home office deduction alone — worth $1,500 on average — is missed by 58% of eligible workers due to confusion about qualification rules. CPAs charge $300-800 for multi-state tax advice, pricing out the typical remote worker making $65,000/year who just needs to know if they owe taxes to their employer's state.

## Solution

1. **State Tax Profile Builder** — Answer questions about where you live, where your employer is based, and where you've worked to get a personalized multi-state tax obligation assessment.
2. **Filing Requirement Checker** — Determine if you need to file in multiple states based on days worked, income thresholds, and your specific state combination's rules.
3. **Home Office Deduction Calculator** — Step-by-step qualification check and calculation for both simplified ($5/sq ft) and regular method, maximizing your deduction legally.
4. **Convenience Rule Explainer** — Plain-language explanations of which states apply "convenience of the employer" rules and how they affect your specific situation.
5. **Digital Nomad Tax Tracker** — For workers who traveled to multiple states/locations, track days in each state to determine filing requirements and tax liability.
6. **Tax Credit Finder** — Identifies available credits for remote workers: home office, internet/phone, equipment, professional development, and state-specific remote work credits.
7. **CPA-Ready Summary** — Generates a document summarizing your remote work situation, states involved, and identified deductions to streamline your CPA consultation and save billable hours.
8. **State Comparison Tool** — Compare tax implications of relocating to different states as a remote worker, factoring in income tax, property tax, and cost of living.
