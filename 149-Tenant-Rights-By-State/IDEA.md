## Problem

There are 44 million renter households in the US, representing 36% of all households, yet tenant rights vary dramatically across 50 states and thousands of municipal jurisdictions. A National Low Income Housing Coalition survey found that 71% of renters don't know their basic rights — including how much notice a landlord must give before entry (ranges from no notice to 48 hours by state), security deposit return deadlines (14 to 60 days), or their rights regarding repair requests. Tenant violations by landlords are rampant: 48% of renters have experienced at least one rights violation but only 12% took action because they didn't know the violation occurred or how to respond. Illegal evictions, wrongful security deposit withholding, and habitability failures cost American renters an estimated $9.4 billion annually. Legal aid for tenants is severely underfunded — 86% of tenants facing eviction have no legal representation.

---

## Solution

1. **State/City Rights Database** — Searchable, plain-language database of tenant rights covering all 50 states and 200+ major cities, organized by topic: security deposits, eviction, repairs, entry rights, lease terms, and discrimination.
2. **Rights Checker** — Answer questions about your situation and get a personalized assessment of which rights apply and whether they've been violated.
3. **Violation Letter Templates** — Pre-written, legally-informed letter templates for common violations: demand for security deposit return, repair request, illegal entry complaint, and habitability notice.
4. **Evidence Documentation** — Photo, video, and note logging tool for documenting violations with timestamps and location data, organized for potential legal use.
5. **Legal Resource Finder** — Directory of free/low-cost legal aid organizations, tenant unions, and legal clinics by location, with eligibility information.
6. **Eviction Defense Guide** — Step-by-step guidance for responding to eviction notices: timeline, court procedures, available defenses, and emergency resources by state.
7. **Lease Clause Checker** — Flags potentially illegal or unenforceable clauses in your lease based on your state's laws (e.g., waiving right to habitable conditions).

## Existing Alternatives

1. **Nolo.com** — Comprehensive legal reference with tenant rights sections, but overwhelming, text-heavy, and not actionable for specific situations.
2. **LawHelp.org** — Legal aid directory but no rights database, no templates, and difficult to navigate.
3. **State attorney general websites** — Official tenant rights information but written in legal language, hard to find, and no tools for taking action.
4. **RentPrep/TenantCloud** — Landlord-focused platforms with some tenant features but designed to serve property owners, not protect tenants.
5. **Reddit r/TenantHelp** — Active community but inconsistent advice quality, not verified by jurisdiction, and answers take hours/days.

## Why This Wins

- **Jurisdiction-specific accuracy** — Unlike generic legal guides, every answer is specific to your state AND city, accounting for local ordinances that override state law.
- **Action-oriented, not just informational** — Letter templates and evidence documentation tools help renters actually DO something about violations, not just learn about them.
- **Affordability** — $4.99 is accessible to the low-income renters who need this most, vs. $100+/hour for legal consultations.
- **Recession-proof demand** — Economic downturns increase both rental demand and landlord-tenant disputes, making this app more valuable during recessions.
- **Legal aid force multiplier** — By helping tenants document violations and understand their rights before contacting legal aid, the app makes scarce legal aid resources more effective.
