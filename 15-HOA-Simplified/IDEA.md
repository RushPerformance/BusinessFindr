## The Problem
There are 365,000 HOAs in the US serving 75 million residents (Community Associations Institute). 60% are managed by volunteer board members, not professional management companies. These volunteers use a chaotic mix of spreadsheets, Venmo, email chains, and filing cabinets to manage dues, violations, maintenance requests, and community documents. Professional HOA management software (AppFolio, Buildium, CINC) costs $200-500/month and is designed for management companies, not volunteer boards. HOA management is the #1 complaint topic on r/HOA (150K members).

## The Solution
1. Community setup: add units, owners, dues schedule
2. Online dues payment: ACH and credit card via Stripe, auto-reminders for late payments
3. Violation tracking: log violations, send notices, track resolution
4. Document library: CC&Rs, bylaws, meeting minutes, financial reports
5. Announcement board: community-wide communications (replaces email chains)
6. Maintenance request submission and tracking
7. Annual budget tool: simple income/expense tracking for the HOA
8. Board meeting agenda builder and minute-taking
