## Problem

Power outages affect 180 million Americans annually, causing an estimated $150 billion in economic losses. The average US household experiences 1.3 outages per year lasting 4.5 hours each, but in some regions outage frequency has increased 67% over the past decade due to aging infrastructure, extreme weather, and growing demand. Yet homeowners receive zero advance warning — utility companies only report outages after they happen. Grid load data is publicly available from ISOs (Independent System Operators) but presented in incomprehensible technical dashboards designed for energy traders, not consumers. For the 7 million Americans who depend on electric-powered medical equipment (CPAP machines, oxygen concentrators, home dialysis), unexpected outages are not just inconvenient — they're dangerous. Remote workers also face mounting costs: a single 2-hour outage costs the average remote worker $120 in lost productivity.

---

## Solution

1. **Neighborhood Grid Status** — Real-time power grid load for your specific area, simplified from ISO data into an easy-to-understand "green/yellow/red" capacity indicator.
2. **Outage Prediction** — ML-based outage probability forecasting using weather data, historical outage patterns, grid load levels, and infrastructure age.
3. **Pre-Outage Alerts** — Push notifications when outage risk is elevated (extreme heat, ice storms, high grid demand) with preparation recommendations.
4. **Outage Map** — Real-time outage map aggregating data from major utility companies with estimated restoration times and affected customer counts.
5. **Preparation Checklist** — Customized preparation lists based on outage type (planned vs. storm, duration estimates) including device charging priorities and food preservation steps.
6. **Medical Equipment Mode** — Special alerts and preparation protocols for households with power-dependent medical equipment, including backup power facility locations.
7. **Solar/Battery Integration** — For homes with solar panels or battery backup, shows current charge levels and estimated backup duration based on typical usage.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **Utility company apps** — Most major utilities offer outage reporting and maps, but no predictive capabilities and maps are often delayed 30+ minutes behind reality.
2. **PowerOutage.us** — Aggregates utility outage data nationally but only shows current outages, no predictions, and the website is not mobile-optimized.
3. **Weather apps** — Provide severe weather alerts that imply outage risk but don't directly predict grid failures or provide preparation guidance.
4. **ISO dashboards (ERCOT, PJM, CAISO)** — Real grid load data but designed for energy market professionals, incomprehensible to consumers.
5. **Tesla Powerwall app** — Excellent for Tesla battery owners but limited to Tesla hardware ecosystem and doesn't help non-Tesla households.

## Why This Wins

- **Predictive, not reactive** — The only consumer app that forecasts outage probability before it happens, giving households time to prepare.
- **Medical safety angle** — Special features for power-dependent medical equipment users fills a life-safety gap that no consumer utility app addresses.
- **Plain-language grid data** — Translating ISO grid load data into simple "green/yellow/red" removes the expertise barrier to understanding grid stress.
- **Recession-proof utility** — Power outage concerns don't decrease during recessions, and the increasing frequency of extreme weather events grows the addressable market annually.
- **Actionable preparation** — Beyond alerts, the app tells you exactly what to do: charge these devices first, move these foods, contact these resources.
