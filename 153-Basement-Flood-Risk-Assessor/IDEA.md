## Problem

Basement flooding is the most common and costly home disaster in the US, affecting 14 million properties annually. The average basement flood costs $25,000 in damage and remediation, and standard homeowner's insurance does NOT cover flooding — only a separate flood insurance policy (which only 15% of homeowners carry) provides coverage. FEMA estimates that just 1 inch of basement water causes $11,000 in damage. Yet most homeowners have no idea about their flood risk: 41% of basement flood damage occurs outside FEMA-designated flood zones, meaning millions of homeowners mistakenly believe they're safe. Homebuyers are particularly vulnerable — basement water intrusion history is the #1 undisclosed issue in home sales, and standard inspections often can't detect intermittent flooding. Climate change is accelerating the problem: heavy rainfall events have increased 37% since 1958, and urban stormwater systems designed decades ago can't handle current volumes.

---

## Solution

1. **Risk Score Generator** — Analyzes your property's flood risk by combining elevation data, local topography, soil drainage characteristics, proximity to water bodies, and historical flooding patterns.
2. **Weather-Based Alerts** — Monitors weather forecasts and sends alerts when rainfall amounts are likely to overwhelm your area's drainage capacity, with estimated basement risk levels.
3. **Prevention Checklist** — Personalized recommendations based on your risk factors: grading corrections, French drain installation, sump pump sizing, gutter extensions, and waterproofing options.
4. **Sump Pump Monitor** — For homes with sump pumps, tracks run cycles (via smart plug integration) and alerts when activity spikes or the pump fails to activate during storms.
5. **Insurance Gap Finder** — Determines if your current insurance covers basement flooding and recommends appropriate flood insurance options with cost estimates.
6. **Homebuyer Report** — Pre-purchase flood risk report for properties you're considering buying, using address-level data that home inspections typically miss.
7. **Cost-Benefit Calculator** — Compares prevention investment costs (sump pump: $1,200, French drain: $6,000, waterproofing: $8,000) against expected flood damage savings over 10 years.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **FEMA Flood Maps** — Official flood zone designations but only cover riverine and coastal flooding, not basement-specific risks from rainfall, grading, or groundwater.
2. **FloodFactor (First Street Foundation)** — Property-level flood risk scores but broad surface flooding focus, not basement-specific, and no prevention recommendations.
3. **Home inspectors** — Check for current water intrusion signs during inspections but can't predict future flooding or assess drainage patterns.
4. **Waterproofing companies** — Provide free assessments but with inherent sales bias — they want to sell $5,000-15,000 waterproofing jobs whether or not you need them.
5. **Insurance agent consultations** — Can discuss flood insurance options but don't assess actual property-level risk or recommend prevention measures.

---

## Why This Wins

- **Basement-specific risk model** — Unlike FEMA/FloodFactor which focus on surface flooding, this analyzes factors specific to basement water intrusion: grading, foundation type, soil, and groundwater.
- **Actionable prevention** — Goes beyond risk identification to provide prioritized, cost-effective prevention recommendations, saving homeowners from both floods and unnecessary waterproofing sales pitches.
- **Homebuyer use case** — Pre-purchase flood risk reports fill a critical gap in the home buying process that standard inspections don't address.
- **Recession-proof demand** — Weather doesn't care about the economy; basement flooding risk persists and often worsens during economic downturns when maintenance is deferred.
- **Insurance guidance** — Helping homeowners understand their coverage gap (most have no flood insurance) creates urgency that drives report purchases.
