## Problem

Freelancers face a massive information asymmetry when evaluating potential clients. While clients can review freelancers on platforms like Upwork, Fiverr, and Glassdoor, there is no equivalent system for freelancers to vet clients. The consequences are severe: 71% of freelancers have experienced late payment, 58% have dealt with significant scope creep, and 29% have completed work for a client who then refused to pay — collectively costing US freelancers an estimated $12 billion in unpaid invoices annually. The Freelancers Union reports that the average freelancer writes off $6,000/year in bad debts. Beyond payment, 44% of freelancers have worked with clients who were abusive, dishonest about project scope, or impossible to communicate with — wasting an average of 8 hours per bad engagement on work that never generates revenue. Yet freelancers have no way to warn each other about problematic clients because no centralized, anonymous review platform exists.

---

## Solution

1. **Client Review Database** — Searchable, anonymous reviews of companies and individuals who hire freelancers, covering payment reliability, scope creep tendency, communication quality, and overall professionalism.
2. **Red Flag Score** — Aggregate score (1-100) for each client based on all reviews, with color-coded risk levels: green (reliable), yellow (proceed with caution), red (avoid).
3. **Payment Reliability Rating** — Specific metric tracking whether a client pays on time, pays the agreed amount, and processes payments without excessive follow-up.
4. **Scope Creep Index** — Quantified measure of how often a client expands project scope beyond original agreements without additional compensation.
5. **Anonymous Submission** — Fully anonymous review submission to protect freelancers from client retaliation, with verification mechanisms to prevent fake reviews.
6. **Contract Red Flag Scanner** — Paste a proposed contract to identify concerning clauses: unlimited revisions, IP ownership overreach, non-compete restrictions, and kill fee absence.
7. **Pre-Engagement Checklist** — Based on a client's review profile, generates a customized list of protective measures: upfront deposits, milestone payments, detailed scope documents.
8. **Community Alerts** — Real-time alerts when a new red-flag review is posted for a client you're currently working with or evaluating.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **Glassdoor** — Employee reviews of companies but focused on full-time employment, not freelance engagements; no payment reliability or scope creep metrics.
2. **Freelancer forums/Slack groups** — Informal word-of-mouth warnings about bad clients, but fragmented, unsearchable, and limited to specific communities.
3. **ClientScorecard** — Early-stage concept with limited database; hasn't achieved critical mass of reviews to be useful.
4. **Upwork/Fiverr client ratings** — Platform-locked, only cover transactions on that platform, and clients can retaliate by leaving negative freelancer reviews.
5. **Better Business Bureau** — Company ratings focused on consumer complaints, not freelancer experiences; most problematic clients are too small to be listed.

## Why This Wins

- **Fills a genuine market gap** — No established platform lets freelancers review clients, despite this being the #1 requested tool in freelancer surveys.
- **Anonymous protection** — True anonymity with verification prevents the retaliation fear that stops freelancers from sharing experiences publicly.
- **Quantified risk metrics** — Specific scores for payment reliability and scope creep provide actionable intelligence beyond generic star ratings.
- **Contract scanner adds immediate value** — Even before the review database reaches critical mass, the contract red flag scanner provides standalone value that drives adoption.
- **Recession-proof demand** — During recessions, freelance work increases as companies cut full-time staff, and payment reliability becomes even more critical.
