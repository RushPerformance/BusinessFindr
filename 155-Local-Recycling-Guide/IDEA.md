## Problem

Recycling rules in the US are maddeningly inconsistent: what's recyclable in one city may be landfill trash 10 miles away. There are 9,800+ municipal recycling programs in the US, each with different accepted materials, preparation requirements, and collection schedules. A study by the Recycling Partnership found that 62% of Americans are "wish-cycling" — putting items in the recycling bin that aren't actually recyclable locally, contaminating entire loads. Contamination rates average 25%, and heavily contaminated loads are sent to landfills entirely, wasting the effort of every other recycler in that load. The confusion is justified: 76% of consumers don't know their local recycling rules, and 94% of those who do know admit they've guessed about at least one item in the past month. The result is $300 million annually in contamination processing costs borne by municipalities and ultimately taxpayers.

## Solution

1. **Municipality-Specific Rules** — Enter your address to get exact recycling rules for YOUR curbside program: what's accepted, what's not, and how to prepare each material type.
2. **Barcode Scanner** — Scan any product's barcode to get an instant recyclable/not-recyclable verdict based on your local program's accepted materials.
3. **Photo Recognition** — Take a photo of any item to identify its material type and get recycling instructions specific to your location.
4. **Contamination Warnings** — Highlights common "wish-cycling" mistakes in your area (e.g., "plastic bags are NOT recyclable in curbside programs in your city").
5. **Collection Calendar** — Your specific pickup schedule with reminders, including special collections for electronics, hazardous waste, and yard debris.
6. **Drop-Off Locator** — Map of nearby recycling drop-off centers for materials not accepted curbside (electronics, batteries, textiles, Styrofoam).
7. **Impact Tracker** — Estimates the environmental impact of your correct recycling: pounds diverted from landfill, CO2 saved, and resources conserved.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **iRecycle (Earth911)** — Drop-off center locator but no curbside program rules, no barcode scanning, and incomplete location coverage.
2. **Recycle Coach** — Municipal recycling app used by some cities, but only available where cities pay to participate and varies in feature quality.
3. **Municipal websites** — Most cities publish recycling guides but they're PDFs or text-heavy pages with no search, no scanning, and hard to access on mobile.
4. **How2Recycle labels** — On-package recycling labels from some brands, but only present on 15% of products and don't account for local rule variations.
5. **Google Search** — "Can I recycle [item]" gives national generic answers, not your local municipality's specific rules.
