## Problem

Pedestrian safety after dark is a pervasive concern: 72% of women and 38% of men report feeling unsafe walking alone at night. Street-level crime increases by 30-50% after dark in most US cities, and poorly-lit areas are 3x more likely to experience violent crime. Yet navigation apps optimize purely for speed or distance, routing pedestrians through dark alleys, empty lots, and isolated shortcuts that save 2 minutes but dramatically increase risk. The problem is acute for specific populations: 11 million nightshift workers commute during dark hours, 20 million college students walk campuses after dark, and women in particular modify their behavior — taking $15 Uber rides instead of 10-minute walks — costing an estimated $600/year in "safety tax." Despite massive investment in navigation technology, no major mapping platform considers pedestrian lighting, open business density, or crime data when suggesting walking routes.

---

## Solution

1. **Safest Route Algorithm** — Calculates walking routes that prioritize well-lit streets, areas with open businesses, populated sidewalks, and lower crime statistics — even if slightly longer than the fastest route.
2. **Street Lighting Map** — Crowdsourced and satellite-derived data on street lighting coverage, including broken streetlight reporting to municipal authorities.
3. **Open Business Layer** — Real-time overlay showing currently open businesses (24-hour stores, restaurants, gas stations) along your route as potential safe havens.
4. **Emergency Resource Map** — Locations of blue-light emergency phones, police stations, fire stations, and hospital emergency rooms along and near your route.
5. **Live Location Sharing** — One-tap share your real-time walking location with trusted contacts who receive alerts if you deviate from route or stop moving.
6. **Quick Alert Button** — Prominent emergency button that simultaneously calls 911, texts your emergency contacts your GPS location, and begins audio/video recording.
7. **Community Reports** — Crowdsourced reports of harassment, suspicious activity, construction detours, and temporary hazards (dark areas from outages) with time-of-day context.
8. **Campus Safety Mode** — Specialized routes for college campuses incorporating blue-light phone locations, campus escort service numbers, and campus police coverage areas.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **Google Maps / Apple Maps** — Dominant navigation but zero safety considerations in route selection; purely optimizes for time and distance.
2. **Citizen** — Real-time crime alerts and incident map but no route planning, no safety-optimized navigation, and often increases anxiety without actionable guidance.
3. **bSafe / Noonlight** — Personal safety apps with SOS features and location sharing, but no safety-optimized route planning or street lighting data.
4. **Companion** — Student safety app with live tracking but limited route optimization and small user base confined to some college campuses.
5. **Life360** — Family location sharing with driving safety features, but not designed for pedestrian route safety optimization.

## Why This Wins

- **Route optimization for safety, not just speed** — The fundamental innovation: no major navigation platform factors in lighting, open businesses, or crime data for pedestrian routing.
- **Practical risk reduction** — Routing through well-lit, populated areas provides concrete safety improvement, unlike panic-button apps that only help after an incident.
- **Cost savings framing** — If users can safely walk routes they'd otherwise Uber ($8-15 per ride), the app saves $50-100/month, far exceeding the $2.99 subscription.
- **Campus market entry point** — College campuses provide a concentrated, safety-conscious user base for initial launch and refinement before expanding citywide.
- **Municipal partnership potential** — Cities and universities have safety mandates and budgets; this app could be deployed institutionally for students and residents.
