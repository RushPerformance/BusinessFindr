## Problem

Unequal household labor distribution is the #1 source of conflict in 62% of cohabiting relationships. Research from the Bureau of Labor Statistics shows that women still perform 37% more housework than men on average, spending 2.6 hours/day vs. 1.9 hours/day on household activities. But the problem isn't just gendered — it's about perception gaps: 79% of partners believe they do more than their fair share, creating a mathematical impossibility that fuels resentment. "Invisible labor" (mental load) — planning meals, scheduling appointments, remembering supplies, managing family logistics — is almost entirely uncounted in these disputes because there's no way to quantify it. Marriage therapists report that chore conflicts rank among the top 5 reasons couples seek counseling ($150-300/session), and studies show that perceived unfairness in household labor increases divorce risk by 50%.

---

## Solution

1. **Chore Logging** — Both partners log completed household tasks with automatic time estimates based on task type, creating an objective record of who does what.
2. **Fairness Dashboard** — Visual breakdown of household labor distribution by partner: hours spent, task categories, and balance percentage over time (weekly/monthly views).
3. **Invisible Labor Tracker** — Specific category for mental load tasks: meal planning, appointment scheduling, gift buying, school communication, home maintenance coordination.
4. **Task Preference Matching** — Each partner rates their preferences and skills for different chore types, enabling fair division based on preferences rather than arbitrary splitting.
5. **Rebalancing Suggestions** — When distribution skews beyond a configurable threshold, suggests specific task swaps based on preferences to rebalance without finger-pointing.
6. **Gamification & Appreciation** — Points, streaks, and thank-you notifications that make chore acknowledgment routine, replacing the "no one notices what I do" resentment cycle.
7. **Recurring Task Scheduler** — Auto-assigns recurring tasks (trash night, bathroom cleaning, grocery shopping) with alternating schedules and completion reminders.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **Sweepy** — Household chore scheduling app but designed for individuals, not couples; no fairness tracking or labor distribution analysis.
2. **OurHome** — Family chore app with points and rewards, but child-focused (designed for parents assigning kids' chores), not partner-equality-focused.
3. **Tody** — Smart cleaning schedule app but purely task-management, no partner comparison, no invisible labor tracking.
4. **Couples therapy** — Addresses chore conflicts but at $150-300/session, costs 100x more than an app and requires scheduling and commitment.
5. **Shared spreadsheets** — Some couples create Google Sheets trackers, but they're tedious to maintain, lack time estimates, and don't provide balance analysis.

## Why This Wins

- **Objective data replaces subjective arguments** — Replacing "I feel like I do everything" with actual logged data transforms unproductive fights into productive conversations.
- **Invisible labor visibility** — The mental load tracker is a breakthrough feature that quantifies the planning, organizing, and remembering that causes the deepest resentment.
- **Preference-based fairness** — Fair doesn't mean 50/50 on every task; matching tasks to preferences means both partners do more of what they don't mind, increasing overall satisfaction.
- **Gamification reduces tension** — Thank-you notifications and appreciation features turn chore tracking from a weapon into a relationship tool.
- **Therapist endorsement potential** — Marriage therapists can recommend this app as a between-session homework tool, creating a professional referral channel.
