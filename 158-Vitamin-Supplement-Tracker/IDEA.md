## Problem

The US supplement market is worth $56 billion annually, with 77% of American adults taking at least one dietary supplement and the average user spending $56/month on vitamins and supplements. Yet this massive market operates with remarkably little guidance: 52% of supplement users don't know if their supplements interact with each other or their medications, 68% take at least one supplement that studies show provides no benefit for their specific health profile, and 34% are unknowingly doubling up on nutrients (e.g., vitamin D from both a multivitamin and a standalone supplement). The supplement industry is largely unregulated by the FDA, and a Consumer Reports investigation found that 30% of supplements don't contain what their labels claim. Without blood work analysis, most people are guessing about what their body actually needs, wasting an average of $240/year on unnecessary supplements.

---

## Solution

1. **Supplement Cabinet** — Log all current supplements with dosages, brands, and timing for a complete picture of daily intake.
2. **Interaction Checker** — Cross-references supplements against each other and any medications for known interactions (calcium blocks iron absorption, vitamin K affects blood thinners, etc.).
3. **Nutrient Overlap Detector** — Identifies when multiple supplements contain the same nutrients, flagging potential megadose risks (e.g., vitamin A toxicity from multiple sources).
4. **Lab Integration** — Upload blood work results to identify actual deficiencies and assess whether your current supplement stack addresses them or wastes money.
5. **Diet-Based Assessment** — Food diary integration estimates nutrient intake from diet, showing which supplements fill genuine gaps vs. which duplicate what you're already eating.
6. **Dosage Timing Optimizer** — Schedules supplement intake for maximum absorption: fat-soluble vitamins with meals, iron away from calcium, probiotics on empty stomach.
7. **Evidence Grading** — For each supplement, shows the strength of scientific evidence for its claimed benefits using peer-reviewed research, rated on a clear A-F scale.
8. **Cost Optimization** — Identifies unnecessary supplements and suggests eliminating or consolidating, showing estimated monthly savings.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **Examine.com** — Excellent evidence-based supplement research database but purely informational, no personal tracking, no interaction checking, and no lab integration.
2. **MyFitnessPal** — Tracks food intake with some supplement logging, but no interaction checking, no evidence grading, and supplements are an afterthought.
3. **Medisafe** — Medication reminder app with some supplement tracking, but focused on Rx medications rather than supplement optimization.
4. **Care/of / Ritual** — Personalized supplement subscriptions with quizzes, but they're selling supplements, not objectively evaluating whether you need them.
5. **InsideTracker** — Blood work-based health optimization ($500-900 per panel), but prohibitively expensive for ongoing supplement management.

## Why This Wins

- **Honest assessment** — Unlike supplement companies (Care/of, Ritual) that always conclude you need their products, this app may tell you to STOP taking certain supplements, building trust.
- **Lab-backed personalization** — Connecting actual blood work to supplement recommendations is dramatically more accurate than quiz-based personalization.
- **Money-saving angle** — Showing users they're wasting $20-40/month on unnecessary supplements makes the $4.99 subscription feel like a savings tool.
- **Interaction safety** — Supplement-supplement and supplement-medication interaction checking fills a gap that pharmacists rarely address (they focus on Rx-Rx interactions).
- **Evidence transparency** — Grading supplements by research quality helps users make informed decisions in an industry rife with unsubstantiated claims.
