## Problem

Americans hold an estimated 165,000 garage sales every week, generating $227 billion in annual secondhand transactions. Yet running a garage sale is chaotically analog: pricing is done with masking tape and Sharpies, totals are calculated on scrap paper or mental math, change-making from a cigar box is error-prone, and at the end of the day there's no record of what sold, at what price, or how much money was actually made. A survey of garage sale organizers found that 54% couldn't accurately report their total sales at the end of the day, 38% made errors making change, and 71% of unsold items ended up in donation bins because re-listing them individually online felt too time-consuming. The shift toward mobile payment (31% of garage sale buyers now prefer Venmo/PayPal) adds another layer of complexity that masking tape and cigar boxes can't handle.

---

## Solution

1. **Item Cataloger** — Quick-add items with photo, description, and price, generating a complete inventory for your sale with optional printable price tags.
2. **Quick Checkout** — Tap items as they sell to keep a running total, calculate change for cash payments, and maintain a real-time sales log.
3. **Cash Drawer Tracker** — Start with your opening cash float, track every transaction, and reconcile at end of day with automatic profit calculation.
4. **Mobile Payment Integration** — Generate Venmo/PayPal/Zelle QR codes for cashless buyers with automatic logging to your sales record.
5. **Bulk Discount Calculator** — Apply quick discounts ("everything half off after 2 PM," "buy 3 get 1 free") with automatic price adjustments across tagged items.
6. **Unsold Item Lister** — One-tap export unsold items to Facebook Marketplace, Craigslist, or OfferUp with photos and descriptions already populated.
7. **Sales Summary Report** — End-of-sale report showing total revenue, items sold, average price, best sellers, and remaining inventory value.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **Square POS** — Professional POS system that's overkill for a one-day garage sale, requires account setup, and takes 2.6% per card transaction.
2. **Calculator app** — Most garage salers use their phone calculator, but it provides no running total, no inventory tracking, and no sales records.
3. **Pen and paper** — Traditional garage sale tracking method that's error-prone, produces no usable records, and can't handle mobile payments.
4. **Yard Sale Treasure Map** — Helps FIND garage sales, not RUN them — completely different use case despite the related market.
5. **Spreadsheet templates** — Some organizers create Google Sheets inventory lists, but using a spreadsheet during a busy sale is impractical.

## Why This Wins

- **Purpose-built for the chaos of a garage sale** — Designed for speed (one-tap checkout), simplicity (no account required), and the unique needs of a one-day sale.
- **Unsold item export is the killer feature** — Automatically listing leftover items online with photos/descriptions already created eliminates the biggest friction point in the post-sale process.
- **Impulse purchase price** — $2.99 is a no-brainer the night before a garage sale when you're writing prices on masking tape and dreading the math.
- **Cash + mobile payment tracking** — The only solution that cleanly tracks both cash and Venmo/Zelle transactions in one place for accurate end-of-day reconciliation.
- **Recession-proof demand** — Garage sales increase during economic downturns as both sellers (need cash) and buyers (need deals) become more active.
