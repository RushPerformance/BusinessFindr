## Problem

There are over 3 trillion trees on Earth and approximately 228 billion in the US alone, yet the average person can identify fewer than 5 tree species by name. For homeowners, tree ignorance is expensive: 72% don't know what species are on their property, which means they can't provide proper care, identify disease early, or make informed decisions about removal. Tree diseases like emerald ash borer (which has killed 100+ million ash trees), oak wilt, and Dutch elm disease cause $4.2 billion in annual damage, and early identification can save individual trees worth $1,000-10,000 in property value each. The US landscaping industry is worth $34 billion, and arborist services specifically generate $8 billion annually, but homeowners frequently hire the wrong services because they can't accurately describe their trees. Hikers, students, and nature enthusiasts also desire identification capability — "what tree is this?" is one of the most common nature-related searches.

---

## Solution

1. **Photo ID Engine** — Take a photo of a leaf, bark, flower, fruit, or overall tree shape and get species identification with confidence percentage and similar-species comparisons.
2. **Multi-Feature ID** — Combine multiple photos (leaf + bark + fruit) for more accurate identification when a single photo isn't conclusive.
3. **Seasonal ID Guide** — Identification guidance adjusted for season, since trees look dramatically different in spring (flowers), summer (full canopy), fall (color), and winter (bare branches).
4. **Tree Care Guide** — Species-specific care instructions: watering needs, pruning timing, fertilization, and common pest/disease vulnerabilities.
5. **Disease & Pest Alerts** — Photo-based disease/pest identification with local alerts when threatening pests (emerald ash borer, spotted lanternfly) are detected in your area.
6. **Property Tree Inventory** — Map and catalog all trees on your property with species, estimated age, size, and health notes for arborist consultations.
7. **Local Arborist Finder** — Directory of ISA-certified arborists in your area with specialties, ratings, and estimated pricing for common services.
8. **Educational Collection** — Build a personal tree collection as you identify species on hikes and walks, with fun facts and geographic distribution maps.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **PictureThis** — Popular plant identification app with good tree coverage, but $29.99/year subscription, aggressive upselling, and less focused on tree-specific care/disease.
2. **iNaturalist** — Excellent community science platform with AI identification, but academic tone, complex interface, and not focused on practical homeowner needs.
3. **LeafSnap** — Early tree ID app from Columbia/Smithsonian, but outdated, limited species database, and no longer actively maintained.
4. **Google Lens** — Can sometimes identify trees but results are inconsistent, no care advice, and no disease identification capability.
5. **Arborist consultations** — Expert identification and care advice but $150-300 per visit, overkill for casual identification questions.

---

## Why This Wins

- **Tree-focused depth** — While PictureThis covers all plants broadly, this app goes deep on trees: bark ID in winter, disease detection, property inventory, and arborist connections.
- **Practical homeowner orientation** — Beyond identification, immediately connects to actionable next steps: care instructions, disease treatment, arborist finder, and property value impact.
- **Disease early warning** — Regional pest/disease alerts are genuinely valuable — early detection of emerald ash borer can save a tree worth thousands in property value.
- **Seasonal adaptation** — Most plant ID apps struggle with winter identification; multi-feature ID with bark and branch structure fills this gap.
- **Educational engagement** — The collection-building gamification appeals to hikers and nature lovers, creating a distinct user segment from homeowners.
