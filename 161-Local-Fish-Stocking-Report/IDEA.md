## Problem

Recreational fishing is a $50 billion industry with 55 million Americans casting a line at least once per year. State fish and wildlife agencies stock over 1.5 billion fish annually across thousands of lakes, rivers, and streams, but stocking schedules are buried in obscure government websites, PDF reports, and agency social media posts that most anglers never see. Knowing when and where fish were recently stocked is the single biggest predictor of a successful outing — yet 73% of anglers report difficulty finding this information. Fishing regulations compound the confusion: each state has different seasons, bag limits, size minimums, and license requirements that vary by water body, species, and even section of river. An estimated 12% of fishing citations are issued to anglers who violated regulations unknowingly. Catch reports — what's biting where — are shared through fragmented word-of-mouth, local bait shops, and scattered Facebook groups with no centralized, real-time database.

---

## Solution

1. **Stocking Alert Map** — Real-time map of recent fish stockings by state agencies, including species, quantity, size, and exact stocking location with push notifications for your saved waters.
2. **Regulation Lookup** — Location-specific fishing regulations: seasons, bag limits, size limits, gear restrictions, and license requirements for any water body, by species.
3. **Community Catch Reports** — User-submitted catch reports with species, size, bait/technique, and location creating a crowdsourced "what's biting where" database.
4. **Stocking Predictions** — Historical stocking pattern analysis to predict upcoming stockings before they're officially announced, based on seasonal schedules.
5. **License Compliance Checker** — Verify your fishing license is valid for the water you're planning to fish, including stamp/endorsement requirements for specific species.
6. **Water Conditions** — Current water temperature, flow rate, clarity, and recent weather data for stocked waters, helping anglers time their visits optimally.
7. **Fishing Buddy Finder** — Connect with nearby anglers for shared trips, knowledge exchange, and buddy fishing for safety on boats.
8. **Tackle Recommendations** — Species-specific and season-specific bait and tackle suggestions based on what's working at each location from community reports.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **Fishbrain** — Popular fishing social network with catch logging, but limited stocking data, no regulation lookup, and premium pricing at $9.99/mo.
2. **State fish and wildlife websites** — Official stocking reports and regulations, but each state's format is different, hard to navigate on mobile, and often delayed.
3. **Fishing Hot Spots Maps** — Detailed lake maps with structure markers but no real-time stocking data, no regulations, and $9.99+ per map.
4. **Facebook fishing groups** — Active community knowledge sharing but unsearchable, location-fragmented, and unreliable for accurate stocking or regulation info.
5. **FishAngler** — Fishing social app with some community catch reports but smaller user base, no stocking integration, and limited regulation coverage.

---

## Why This Wins

- **Stocking data aggregation** — No existing app consolidates real-time stocking reports from all 50 state agencies into a single, searchable, alert-enabled map.
- **Regulation confidence** — Instant, location-specific regulation lookup prevents the confusion and citation risk that affects millions of anglers annually.
- **Timing intelligence** — Combining stocking schedules with water conditions and catch reports helps anglers pick the perfect day, not just the right lake.
- **Affordable compared to Fishbrain** — At $3.99/mo vs. $9.99/mo, the premium tier is accessible to the budget-conscious weekend angler.
- **Community catch reports with stocking context** — When catch reports are overlaid with stocking data, patterns emerge that help everyone fish smarter.
