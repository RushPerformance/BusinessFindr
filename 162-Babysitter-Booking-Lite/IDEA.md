## Problem

The US childcare market is worth $57 billion, and informal babysitting — the neighbor's teenager, the college student down the street, the retired teacher next door — remains the most affordable and trusted option for date nights, errand runs, and occasional childcare. Yet booking these sitters is stuck in the text message era: parents text 3-5 sitters hoping someone is available, track responses across multiple threads, negotiate rates verbally, and pay in cash (often requiring an ATM stop). Care.com, the dominant platform, charges $39/month for premium membership and focuses on professional nannies rather than casual neighborhood sitters. UrbanSitter charges $25/month. These platforms are overkill for parents who need a sitter twice a month and have a trusted neighborhood network they'd prefer to use. For teen sitters, the barrier is equally frustrating: they have no way to communicate availability, get booked consistently, or build a reputation beyond word-of-mouth.

---

## Solution

1. **Quick Booking** — Send a sitting request to multiple trusted sitters simultaneously with date, time, number of kids, and offered rate — first to accept gets the booking.
2. **Sitter Availability Calendar** — Sitters set their available hours so parents can see who's free without the back-and-forth texting.
3. **In-App Payment** — Secure payment at booking completion with automatic rate calculation (hourly rate x hours + late-night premium if applicable), eliminating the ATM run.
4. **Trusted Network** — Build your personal sitter network by inviting known sitters — this isn't a marketplace of strangers, it's a booking tool for existing relationships.
5. **Sitter Profiles** — Age-appropriate profiles for sitters with certifications (CPR, first aid), experience, parent ratings, and a brief bio.
6. **Booking History** — Complete record of all past sittings with dates, hours, rates, and sitter, useful for tax deductions (dependent care FSA) and budgeting.
7. **Emergency Info Sharing** — Automatically sends sitters a one-page emergency info card (allergies, medications, emergency contacts, pediatrician) when a booking is confirmed.
8. **Rate Guide** — Local average babysitting rates by number of children and time of day, helping both parents and sitters negotiate fairly.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **Care.com** — Dominant childcare platform but $39/month subscription, focused on professional nannies, and background check requirements create friction for casual neighborhood sitting.
2. **UrbanSitter** — Booking-focused platform at $25/month, but still premium-priced for occasional users and oriented toward professional sitters.
3. **Text messages** — The current "system" for 80% of casual babysitting arrangements, with all the friction of multi-thread coordination and cash payment.
4. **Nextdoor** — Neighbors sometimes post babysitting needs, but it's not designed for booking, scheduling, or payment.
5. **Sittercity** — Similar to Care.com with subscription model ($35/month) and professional sitter focus, not designed for neighborhood networks.

---

## Why This Wins

- **No subscription fee** — Commission-only model means parents pay nothing until they actually use a sitter, unlike Care.com/UrbanSitter's $25-39/month regardless of usage.
- **Built for existing relationships** — This isn't a marketplace of strangers; it's a booking tool for the sitters you already know and trust, removing the biggest barrier to platform adoption.
- **Teen sitter friendly** — Designed for the reality that most neighborhood babysitters are 15-22, with age-appropriate profiles and parent-managed invitations.
- **Eliminates text message chaos** — The simultaneous request feature (first to accept wins) replaces the exhausting process of texting sitters one by one and waiting for responses.
- **Payment convenience** — In-app payment eliminates the ATM stop and creates a tax-documentable record for dependent care FSA claims.
