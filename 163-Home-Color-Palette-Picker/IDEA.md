## Problem

Painting is the most common home improvement project, with 55% of homeowners repainting at least one room per year. Yet color selection is the most paralyzing decision in the process: the average homeowner spends 3-5 hours agonizing over paint chips, buys 2.3 sample quarts ($8-12 each) before committing, and 22% still regret their color choice after the room is painted — a $200-400 mistake in wasted paint and labor. The challenge is that paint colors look dramatically different depending on lighting conditions, surrounding furniture, flooring, and room size — factors that tiny 1-inch color chips in fluorescent-lit hardware stores can't convey. The paint industry offers 3,500+ colors across brands (Benjamin Moore, Sherwin-Williams, Behr, PPG), making selection overwhelming. Interior designers solve this problem but charge $150-500 per room, pricing out the 82% of homeowners who DIY their painting.

---

## Solution

1. **Room Photo Analysis** — Upload photos of your room and AI identifies existing colors (furniture, flooring, cabinetry, fixtures) to suggest harmonious paint colors.
2. **Lighting Assessment** — Analyzes your room's natural light direction (north/south/east/west facing) and intensity to recommend colors that will look great in YOUR specific lighting.
3. **Virtual Paint Preview** — See how recommended colors look on your actual walls using AR overlay on your room photos, showing both daylight and evening lighting conditions.
4. **Paint Brand Matching** — Every recommended color is matched to exact paint codes from Benjamin Moore, Sherwin-Williams, Behr, Farrow & Ball, and PPG — ready to order.
5. **Color Harmony Engine** — Suggests complementary accent wall colors, trim colors, and ceiling options that work together as a cohesive palette.
6. **Style Presets** — Choose your interior style (modern, farmhouse, coastal, minimalist, maximalist, Scandinavian) and get style-appropriate color palettes for your room.
7. **Sample Ordering** — Direct links to order paint samples from major brands, or schedule free color consultation at local paint stores.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **Benjamin Moore Color Portfolio App** — AR color visualization for BM colors only, no AI recommendation, limited to one brand's palette.
2. **Sherwin-Williams ColorSnap** — Color matching and room visualization, but only SW colors and no AI-driven suggestions based on room contents.
3. **Palette (by Google)** — Generates color palettes from photos but for graphic design, not interior paint — no paint brand matching, no lighting consideration.
4. **Interior designers** — Expert personalized color advice but $150-500 per room puts them out of reach for most DIY painters.
5. **Pinterest / Instagram** — Infinite paint color inspiration but no personalization for YOUR room, YOUR lighting, and YOUR existing furniture.

---

## Why This Wins

- **Room-aware recommendations** — No existing app considers YOUR specific furniture, flooring, cabinetry, and lighting when suggesting colors — they all start from scratch.
- **Multi-brand matching** — Unlike brand-specific apps that only show their own colors, this suggests the best color regardless of brand and provides exact codes for all major paint lines.
- **Lighting intelligence** — Accounting for room orientation and natural light prevents the most common paint regret: "it looked great at the store but wrong on my wall."
- **Per-room pricing model** — $4.99 per room is far less than a designer visit and less than the average $25 wasted on wrong sample quarts.
- **Sample order integration** — Connecting analysis directly to sample ordering removes friction and creates potential affiliate revenue from paint retailers.
