## Problem

In a medical emergency, first responders need critical information immediately: emergency contacts, medications, allergies, blood type, medical conditions, and insurance details. Yet 68% of Americans don't carry this information in an accessible format. The "ICE" (In Case of Emergency) phone contact convention is widely known but inaccessible when phones are locked — which they almost always are. For the 133 million Americans with chronic health conditions, missing medication or allergy information during an emergency can lead to dangerous treatment decisions. Parents of young children face a similar gap: babysitters, schools, and camps need emergency contact cards that are current, complete, and quickly accessible. Travelers in foreign countries are especially vulnerable — if incapacitated abroad, responders may not be able to identify them or reach family members. Despite smartphones, the reliable, always-accessible emergency card — whether physical in a wallet or digital on a lock screen — remains the most dependable emergency information delivery method.

## Solution

1. **Card Designer** — Create professional emergency contact cards with customizable layouts for wallet-sized printable cards, phone lock screen wallpapers, and medical ID bracelet engravings.
2. **Medical Information Section** — Fields for allergies, current medications with dosages, chronic conditions, blood type, organ donor status, and physician contact information.
3. **Emergency Contact List** — Multiple emergency contacts with relationship, phone numbers, and priority order for first responders.
4. **Insurance Card Integration** — Store health insurance, auto insurance, and travel insurance information on the same card or a linked secondary card.
5. **Lock Screen Wallpaper** — Generates a phone lock screen wallpaper with critical emergency information visible without unlocking the phone.
6. **Family Card Set** — Create matching cards for entire families with one account: parents, children, elderly family members, with age-appropriate information.
7. **QR Code Card** — Generates a compact card with a QR code that links to a full digital profile with all medical information, accessible by anyone with a smartphone.
8. **Multi-Language Translation** — Translates emergency information into multiple languages for travelers, with phonetic pronunciation for critical medical terms.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **Apple Health Medical ID** — Built into iPhone but limited to Apple devices, not always visible on lock screen by default, and no printable card option.
2. **Google Emergency Information** — Android equivalent but similarly limited to Google devices and not accessible when phone is completely dead.
3. **MedicAlert bracelets** — Trusted medical ID system but $50+/year membership, physical bracelet only, and information limited to what fits on engraving.
4. **ICE phone contacts** — Widely recommended but inaccessible when phones are locked or dead, and doesn't include medical information.
5. **DIY printed cards** — Some people create their own cards, but they're typically incomplete, poorly formatted, and not updated when information changes.
