## Problem

Smoke and CO detector failures cause roughly 1,450 fire deaths per year in U.S. homes with non-working smoke alarms, according to NFPA data. In nearly half of these failures, the batteries were missing or dead. The typical 9V battery lasts 6-12 months, and many households have 6-10 detectors across multiple floors. Most people rely on the annoying low-battery chirp — which often sounds at 3 AM — or the twice-a-year daylight saving time reminder, which many still forget. Roughly 25% of U.S. smoke alarms have dead or missing batteries at any given time, representing over 30 million homes at elevated risk.

---

## Solution

1. **Device Inventory** — Catalog every smoke and CO detector by room, floor, brand, model, and battery type (9V, AA, 10-year lithium).
2. **Battery Change Logger** — One-tap log when batteries are replaced, with date stamps and optional photo verification.
3. **Smart Reminders** — Configurable push notifications based on battery lifespan (6 months for 9V, annually for lithium, etc.).
4. **Testing Schedule** — Monthly test reminders with instructions for each detector model.
5. **Expiration Tracking** — Smoke detectors expire after 10 years; the app tracks manufacture dates and flags replacements.
6. **Multi-Property Support** — Landlords and property managers can track detectors across multiple addresses.
7. **Battery Shopping List** — Auto-generates a list of all batteries needed with links to purchase.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **Phone Calendar Reminders** — Manual setup, no detector-specific intelligence, easy to dismiss and forget.
2. **Nest Protect** — Smart smoke detector with built-in battery monitoring, but costs $120+ per unit and requires full hardware replacement.
3. **HomeZada** — Broad home maintenance app, smoke detectors are just one of hundreds of items, no specialized features.
4. **Daylight Saving Time Habit** — The traditional "change clocks, change batteries" approach, but DST changes don't align with actual battery life.
