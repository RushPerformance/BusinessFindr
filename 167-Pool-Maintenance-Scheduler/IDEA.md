## Problem

There are approximately 5.7 million residential swimming pools in the United States. Pool owners spend an average of $1,200-$1,800 per year on chemicals, maintenance, and repairs. Maintaining proper water chemistry requires testing pH, chlorine, alkalinity, and stabilizer levels multiple times per week — imbalances cause algae blooms, equipment corrosion, and skin irritation. Roughly 40% of pool owners overspend on chemicals due to improper dosing, and another 30% experience at least one algae bloom per season from inconsistent maintenance. Equipment failures from neglected maintenance cost $500-$3,000 per repair.

---

## Solution

1. **Chemical Log & Dosing Calculator** — Log test strip results and get exact chemical dosing recommendations based on pool volume.
2. **Maintenance Calendar** — Automated schedule for skimming, brushing, vacuuming, filter cleaning, and winterizing tasks.
3. **Equipment Tracker** — Log pump, filter, heater, and salt cell ages with replacement reminders based on manufacturer specs.
4. **Weather Integration** — Adjusts maintenance recommendations based on local weather (rain dilution, heat evaporation, freeze warnings).
5. **Service Provider Directory** — Find and book local pool service companies, read reviews, and schedule one-time or recurring visits.
6. **Cost Tracker** — Log chemical purchases and service costs to track annual pool expenses.
7. **Seasonal Guides** — Step-by-step opening and closing procedures customized to pool type (chlorine, salt, biguanide).
8. **Photo History** — Visual timeline of water clarity and pool condition over the season.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **Pool Math by TFP** — Comprehensive chemistry calculator popular with enthusiasts but has steep learning curve and dated UI.
2. **Sutro Smart Monitor** — Hardware-based automated water testing ($299 + $15/mo) — expensive and requires physical device.
3. **Pool Doctor** — Basic pool calculator app, limited maintenance scheduling, no service provider integration.
4. **pHin** — Smart water monitor (discontinued/acquired), demonstrated market interest but hardware dependency limited scale.
5. **Spreadsheets/Notebooks** — Many pool owners manually track chemistry in notebooks or Excel, tedious and error-prone.

## Why This Wins

- **Software-only solution** — no expensive hardware required, just input test strip results.
- **$4.99/mo is trivial** compared to $1,200+/yr pool maintenance costs — easy ROI argument.
- **Seasonal stickiness** — users return every pool season (April-October) creating predictable annual engagement.
- **Service marketplace potential** — connecting pool owners with local service providers adds a commission revenue layer.
- **Saves real money** — proper chemical dosing alone can save $200-$400/year in wasted chemicals.
