## Problem

Americans take roughly 2.3 billion trips per year, and surveys consistently show that 55-60% of travelers forget at least one essential item. Forgotten items cost travelers an average of $50-$100 per trip in replacement purchases — often at inflated airport or hotel prices. Business travelers forget chargers, adapters, and presentation materials. Family travelers forget children's medications, car seats, and comfort items. The problem compounds for multi-destination trips where weather varies dramatically. Most people either use the same generic list every time (missing activity-specific items) or spend 30-60 minutes building a custom list from scratch.

---

## Solution

1. **Smart List Generation** — Input destination, dates, and activities; the app pulls weather forecasts and generates a tailored packing list.
2. **Activity Templates** — Pre-built packing modules for hiking, beach, skiing, business meetings, weddings, camping, and more.
3. **Weather-Adaptive Suggestions** — Rain gear, sunscreen, cold weather layers — automatically suggested based on forecast data.
4. **Check-Off Interface** — Satisfying tap-to-pack experience with progress tracking and "all packed" confirmation.
5. **Family Mode** — Generate separate lists for each family member with age-appropriate items for children.
6. **Return Packing** — Reverse checklist for packing up at the hotel to ensure nothing is left behind.
7. **Carry-On vs. Checked Split** — Automatically sorts items by TSA-compliant carry-on vs. checked bag categories.
8. **Saved Profiles** — Remember clothing sizes, medication needs, tech gear, and preferences across trips.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **PackPoint** — The leading packing list app, but interface feels dated and weather integration is inconsistent.
2. **TripIt** — Comprehensive trip organizer, packing list is an afterthought bolted onto itinerary management.
3. **Google Keep / Apple Notes** — Manual list-making, no intelligence, easy to forget items you don't think of.
4. **Pinterest Packing Lists** — Static blog-style lists that aren't customized to specific trips or weather.
