# Warranty Wallet

> Stores all your product warranties and receipts in one place, sends expiration alerts, and auto-files warranty claims when products fail.

## The Problem
The average household has $15,000-20,000 in warrantied products but only 20% of valid warranty claims are ever filed (Consumer Reports). People lose receipts, forget warranty periods, and don't know the claims process. When a $1,200 dishwasher breaks at month 11 of a 12-month warranty, most people just buy a new one because they can't find the receipt or don't realize it's still covered. Email receipts are scattered across inboxes, paper receipts fade, and warranty cards get thrown away.

## The Solution
1. Scan receipts (paper/email) or import from email (Gmail/Outlook integration)
2. Auto-detect product, purchase date, and warranty period
3. Store warranty documents with searchable product catalog
4. Countdown alerts: "Your Samsung TV warranty expires in 30 days"
5. Guided claim filing: step-by-step instructions + pre-written claim letters
6. Manufacturer contact info and claim portals linked
7. Extended warranty marketplace: compare and buy extended warranties from third-party providers
8. Home inventory for insurance purposes

## Existing Alternatives
- **Centriq**: Home manual storage, some warranty tracking, limited
- **Warrantify**: Small app, basic features, low adoption
- **Evernote/Google Drive**: Manual storage, no alerts or claim assistance
- **Email search**: How most people "find" their receipts
- **Physical filing**: Folders/shoeboxes of receipts

## Why This Wins
- Auto-claim filing removes the biggest friction point (figuring out how to file)
- Expiration alerts before warranty lapses is a clear, measurable value proposition
- Extended warranty marketplace creates a secondary revenue stream (affiliate commissions)
- Email receipt import automates the most tedious part of setup
- High retention: people don't want to re-upload all their warranties elsewhere
