## Problem

Mold affects roughly 70% of U.S. homes to some degree, with 28% of people carrying genes that make them especially susceptible to mold-related illness. Professional mold remediation costs $1,500-$9,000 per incident. The EPA recommends maintaining indoor humidity between 30-50%, yet most homeowners have no idea what their current levels are. In humid climates (Southeast U.S., Pacific Northwest), indoor humidity routinely exceeds 60% without intervention. High humidity also damages hardwood floors ($8-$12/sq ft to replace), warps furniture, and creates conditions for dust mites — the #1 indoor allergen trigger affecting 20 million Americans.

---

## Solution

1. **Manual Humidity Logging** — Simple input screen for hygrometer readings with trend visualization over time.
2. **Smart Device Integration** — Connect with Bluetooth/Wi-Fi hygrometers (SensorPush, Govee, etc.) for automatic data collection.
3. **Room-by-Room Tracking** — Monitor humidity in different zones (basement, bathroom, bedroom) with room-specific thresholds.
4. **Mold Risk Alerts** — Push notifications when humidity exceeds safe levels for extended periods, with severity ratings.
5. **Seasonal Recommendations** — Adjusts humidity targets based on outdoor temperature and season (lower in winter to prevent condensation).
6. **Action Guide** — Specific recommendations: run dehumidifier, increase ventilation, check bathroom fan, inspect for leaks.
7. **Dehumidifier Runtime Calculator** — Estimates how long to run dehumidifier based on room size, current humidity, and target level.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **SensorPush App** — Companion app for SensorPush hardware; good data logging but no actionable recommendations or mold risk analysis.
2. **Govee Home App** — Bluetooth hygrometer companion app; basic alerts but no seasonal intelligence or room-by-room analysis.
3. **Nest Thermostat** — Shows humidity reading but no guidance on what to do about it.
4. **AcuRite** — Weather station companion app with humidity data but focused on outdoor conditions, limited indoor advice.
