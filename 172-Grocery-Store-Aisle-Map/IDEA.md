## Problem

The average American makes 1.6 grocery trips per week, spending 41 minutes per trip. That totals over 55 hours per year in grocery stores. Studies show that 60-70% of that time is spent navigating aisles looking for items, not actively selecting products. Unfamiliar stores compound the problem — people shopping at a store they don't know spend 30% more time per trip. This wasted time costs American shoppers an estimated $15 billion in lost productivity annually. Meanwhile, grocery stores have little incentive to help — longer time in-store correlates with 20-30% higher basket sizes due to impulse purchases.

---

## Solution

1. **Store Layout Maps** — Detailed aisle-by-aisle maps with product category locations for participating grocery stores.
2. **Shopping List Integration** — Import or build a shopping list and get an optimized walking route through the store.
3. **Route Optimization** — Algorithm sorts your list into the most efficient walking order, minimizing backtracking.
4. **Product Locator** — Search for specific products and see their exact aisle and section placement.
5. **Crowdsourced Data** — Community-contributed store maps for locations not yet officially partnered.
6. **Favorites & History** — Remember your usual store and frequently purchased items for faster list building.
7. **Store Comparison** — See which nearby stores carry all items on your list and compare trip efficiency.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **Store-Specific Apps (Kroger, Walmart)** — Individual retailer apps with aisle info, but only for their own stores; shoppers use multiple stores.
2. **AnyList / OurGroceries** — Excellent shopping list apps but zero store layout integration.
3. **Instacart** — Solves the problem by eliminating the trip entirely, but costs $3.99-$9.99/delivery plus markups.
4. **Google Maps Indoor** — Covers malls and airports but negligible grocery store coverage.

---

## Why This Wins

- **Cross-store solution** — unlike retailer apps, works across multiple grocery chains and stores.
- **Free for consumers** — partnership revenue model means no paywall limiting adoption.
- **Time savings are tangible** — users save 15-20 minutes per trip, a benefit they feel immediately.
- **Crowdsourcing scales coverage** — community mapping fills gaps until official partnerships are in place.
- **Grocery chains benefit too** — data on shopping patterns and opportunity to promote in-store offers.
