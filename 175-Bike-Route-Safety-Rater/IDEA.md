## Problem

Roughly 1,000 cyclists are killed and 130,000 injured in traffic crashes annually in the United States. Cycling participation has grown 47% over the past decade, now at 53 million regular riders, but infrastructure hasn't kept pace. Only 2.4% of U.S. road miles have protected bike lanes. The global cycling market is valued at $6.1 billion and growing. Existing map apps (Google Maps, Apple Maps) offer cycling directions but optimize for speed or distance, not safety. A route with a bike lane adds 5 minutes but is 90% safer — yet navigation apps won't suggest it by default. Rider surveys show 60% of potential commuter cyclists cite safety concerns as their #1 barrier.

---

## Solution

1. **Safety Score Algorithm** — Rates routes 1-100 based on bike lane coverage, traffic speed/volume, intersection density, road surface, and lighting.
2. **Accident Heat Map** — Overlays historical bike crash data from police reports and crowdsourced incidents.
3. **Route Comparison** — Shows fastest vs. safest route side-by-side with safety score and time difference.
4. **Infrastructure Labels** — Distinguishes between protected bike lanes, painted bike lanes, shared lanes, and no infrastructure.
5. **Community Reports** — Riders report hazards (potholes, construction, aggressive intersections) with photo evidence.
6. **Time-of-Day Adjustments** — Safety scores adjust for rush hour traffic, nighttime visibility, and school zone activity.
7. **Comfort Rating** — Separate score for hills, wind exposure, and road smoothness for less experienced riders.
8. **Ride Logging** — Track rides and contribute GPS data to improve route safety intelligence.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **Google Maps (Cycling)** — Offers bike routes but prioritizes efficiency over safety, minimal infrastructure detail.
2. **Strava** — Ride tracking and segment competition, not safety-focused route planning.
3. **Komoot** — Excellent for recreational cycling routes but focused on scenery and adventure, not urban commuter safety.
4. **RideWithGPS** — Route planning with some road surface data but no safety scoring or accident data.
5. **CyclOSM** — OpenStreetMap-based cycling map, good infrastructure data but no scoring, navigation, or mobile-first experience.
