## Problem

The CDC estimates 48 million Americans get food poisoning annually, causing 128,000 hospitalizations and 3,000 deaths. Improper refrigerator temperature is a leading cause — the USDA recommends 40 degrees F or below, but studies show 37% of home refrigerators operate above this threshold. Americans waste $1,500+ per household annually on spoiled food, and improper temperature accelerates spoilage. Most people never check their fridge temperature and don't know when power outages cause dangerous temperature spikes. A 2-hour period above 40 degrees F can make perishable food unsafe, and families often discover this after consumption.

---

## Solution

1. **Bluetooth Thermometer Pairing** — Connect compatible Bluetooth thermometers (SensorPush, Govee, ThermoPro) for continuous monitoring.
2. **Temperature Alerts** — Instant push notification when fridge or freezer temp exceeds safe thresholds.
3. **Power Outage Detection** — Identifies temperature spikes indicating power failures and estimates food safety window.
4. **Manual Logging** — For users without Bluetooth thermometers, simple manual temperature entry with trend tracking.
5. **Food Safety Guide** — Item-by-item guidance on what's safe to keep vs. discard after temperature excursions.
6. **Historical Charts** — 24-hour, 7-day, and 30-day temperature trend graphs for each monitored zone.
7. **Multi-Zone Support** — Separate tracking for fridge, freezer, garage fridge, wine cooler, etc.
8. **Calibration Reminders** — Periodic reminders to verify thermometer accuracy.
