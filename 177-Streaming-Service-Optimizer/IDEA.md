## Problem

The average American household subscribes to 4.7 streaming services, spending $61/month ($732/year) on streaming — up from $38/month in 2020. Studies show that 48% of subscribers say they pay for services they rarely or never use, representing $4.8 billion in wasted subscription spending annually. Streaming fatigue is real — 42% of consumers say they're overwhelmed by the number of services and struggle to decide which to keep. When popular shows end or move platforms, subscribers don't adjust their subscriptions. The average household could save $180-$240/year by optimizing to 2-3 services that match their actual viewing patterns.

---

## Solution

1. **Subscription Inventory** — Log all active streaming subscriptions with monthly costs and billing dates.
2. **Viewing Habit Survey** — Quick questionnaire about genres, shows, and viewing frequency per platform.
3. **Content Overlap Analysis** — Identifies shows and movies available on multiple platforms you subscribe to.
4. **Cost-Per-Hour Metric** — Calculates actual cost per hour of content watched on each platform.
5. **Keep/Cancel Recommendation** — Clear, data-driven recommendation for each service with savings projections.
6. **Rotation Strategy** — Suggests a monthly rotation plan (subscribe to one platform at a time to binge, then switch).
7. **New Release Alerts** — Notifies when anticipated shows launch so you can temporarily resubscribe to the right platform.
8. **Annual Savings Tracker** — Running total of money saved by following the app's recommendations.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **JustWatch** — Shows where content is streaming but doesn't analyze your habits or recommend cancellations.
2. **Trim (now Rocket Money)** — Subscription tracker that identifies recurring charges but no streaming-specific intelligence.
3. **Reelgood** — Cross-platform search engine for streaming content, no cost optimization features.
4. **Manual Spreadsheet** — Self-managed tracking, tedious, requires discipline to maintain and act on.
