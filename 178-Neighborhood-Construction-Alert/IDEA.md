## Problem

Over 1.5 million building permits are issued annually in the United States. Homeowners routinely discover major construction projects — apartment buildings, commercial developments, road widening — only when heavy equipment arrives next door. By then, it's too late to comment during public hearing periods or adjust plans. Construction noise complaints have risen 300% in major metros over the past decade. Nearby construction can reduce property values by 5-15% during active building. Prospective homebuyers frequently purchase without knowing about planned developments that will change the character of the neighborhood. Building permit data is technically public but scattered across hundreds of municipal websites in incompatible formats.

---

## Solution

1. **Address-Based Monitoring** — Enter your address and get alerts for all building permits filed within a configurable radius.
2. **Project Details** — Type of construction, estimated timeline, contractor information, and project value for each permit.
3. **Map Visualization** — Interactive map showing all active and planned construction projects near your home.
4. **Impact Assessment** — Estimates noise impact, traffic disruption, and potential property value effects based on project type and proximity.
5. **Public Hearing Alerts** — Notifications for upcoming zoning hearings and public comment periods related to nearby projects.
6. **Timeline Tracking** — Follow projects from permit filing through completion with status updates.
7. **Historical View** — See what was built in the neighborhood over the past 5-10 years for patterns.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **BuildZoom** — Contractor search with some permit data, but focused on finding contractors, not monitoring neighborhoods.
2. **PermitRoots** — Commercial permit data provider for B2B, not consumer-friendly.
3. **Local Government Websites** — Raw permit data available but scattered, hard to search, and rarely sends proactive alerts.
4. **Nextdoor** — Neighbors sometimes post about construction, but coverage is inconsistent and after-the-fact.
5. **Zillow** — Shows nearby construction as a factor but no active monitoring or alerts.

## Why This Wins

- **Proactive vs. reactive** — know about projects months before ground breaks, not after jackhammers start.
- **Aggregated data** — consolidates permit data from hundreds of municipalities into one searchable interface.
- **Buyer/seller utility** — real estate agents and homebuyers use it for due diligence before purchases.
- **Public hearing participation** — empowers homeowners to engage in zoning decisions that affect their property.
- **Data moat** — building permit data ingestion pipelines create a defensible competitive advantage over time.
