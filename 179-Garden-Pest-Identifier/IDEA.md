## Problem

There are 55 million home gardeners in the United States, and pests cause an estimated 20-40% of crop loss in home gardens. Most gardeners can't tell beneficial insects from harmful ones — ladybugs vs. cucumber beetles, hover flies vs. wasps. Misidentification leads to killing beneficial insects (reducing natural pest control) or applying the wrong pesticide (wasting $50-$100 on ineffective products). Home gardeners spend $3.6 billion annually on pest control products, with an estimated 30% wasted on wrong products or wrong timing. Extension office identification services take 3-7 days for a response, by which time pest damage has spread significantly.

---

## Solution

1. **Photo-Based ID** — AI-powered image recognition identifies common garden pests from smartphone photos.
2. **Pest Database** — Searchable library of 500+ common garden pests with high-quality images, lifecycle info, and damage patterns.
3. **Treatment Plans** — Organic and conventional treatment options ranked by effectiveness, with product recommendations.
4. **Beneficial Insect Guide** — Teaches users to identify and protect helpful insects (pollinators, predators, parasitoids).
5. **Timing Recommendations** — Optimal treatment timing based on pest lifecycle stage and local climate.
6. **Regional Alerts** — Notifications when specific pests are active in your growing zone based on community reports.
7. **Prevention Calendar** — Proactive measures by month to prevent common pest issues before they start.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **Google Lens** — General-purpose image search that can sometimes identify bugs, but no treatment advice or garden context.
2. **iNaturalist** — Excellent organism identification but focused on naturalist documentation, not pest management.
3. **Picture Insect** — Bug identification app, but not garden-specific and treatment recommendations are generic.
4. **Extension Office "Ask an Expert"** — Authoritative but slow (3-7 day turnaround), not mobile-friendly.
5. **Gardening Facebook Groups** — Community ID help is hit-or-miss and often confidently wrong.
