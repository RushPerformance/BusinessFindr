## The Problem
There are ~750,000 divorces per year in the US. The average divorce costs $15,000-$30,000, with financial advisor fees ($200-500/hour) being a major component. Most couples can't afford a Certified Divorce Financial Analyst (CDFA). Assets that seem simple (house, retirement accounts, stock options) have complex tax implications that affect the true value of a "50/50 split." A 50/50 split of assets is rarely actually equitable once taxes, liquidity, and future value are considered.

## The Solution
1. Asset inventory: list all shared assets (real estate, retirement accounts, bank accounts, vehicles, debts)
2. Net value calculator: adjusts for tax implications (e.g., $500K in a 401K ≠ $500K in a brokerage account)
3. Equitable split modeler: drag assets between columns to see real after-tax values for each spouse
4. Post-divorce budget projector: "Your monthly budget will look like this after the split"
5. Debt allocation worksheet
6. Alimony/child support estimator based on state formulas
7. Checklist of financial accounts to close, separate, or retitle
8. Document organizer for financial discovery
