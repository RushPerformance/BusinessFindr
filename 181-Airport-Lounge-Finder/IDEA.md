## Problem

There are over 4,000 airport lounges worldwide, accessible through a complex web of credit cards, airline loyalty programs, Priority Pass, Diners Club, day passes, and one-time access fees. The average business traveler carries 2-3 credit cards with some form of lounge access but uses only 30% of their available lounge visits because they don't know which card grants access at which airports. Priority Pass alone covers 1,300+ lounges, but participating lounges change monthly. Many travelers pay $40-$75 for day passes without realizing their existing credit card already provides free access. Airlines are adding and removing lounge partnerships constantly, making it impossible to track manually.

---

## Solution

1. **Card Wallet** — Input your credit cards and loyalty memberships to build a personal lounge access profile.
2. **Airport Lounge Search** — Search any airport and see which lounges you can access for free with your existing cards/memberships.
3. **Lounge Reviews** — Crowdsourced ratings covering food quality, seating, Wi-Fi speed, shower availability, and crowding levels.
4. **Day Pass Comparison** — When you don't have free access, compare day pass prices across all available options.
5. **Guest Policy Details** — Shows how many guests each access method allows and at what additional cost.
6. **Card Upgrade Suggestions** — Identifies credit card upgrades that would unlock the most additional lounge access based on your travel patterns.
7. **Real-Time Capacity** — Crowdsourced current occupancy estimates to avoid overcrowded lounges.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **LoungeBuddy (acquired by American Express)** — Was the leading lounge finder but now limited to Amex ecosystem, reduced coverage.
2. **Priority Pass App** — Only shows Priority Pass lounges, ignores credit card-specific access and airline lounges.
3. **The Points Guy / NerdWallet** — Blog articles about lounge access, but static content not personalized to your specific cards.
4. **Sleeping in Airports** — Community site with some lounge reviews, but focused on terminal sleeping, not lounge optimization.

## Why This Wins

- **LoungeBuddy gap** — American Express acquisition removed the best cross-platform lounge finder, creating a clear market opening.
