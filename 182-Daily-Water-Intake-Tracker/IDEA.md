## Problem

Chronic mild dehydration affects an estimated 75% of Americans. Even 1-2% dehydration reduces cognitive performance by 25% and physical performance by 10-15%. The recommended daily water intake varies significantly by body weight (0.5-1 oz per pound), activity level, climate, and altitude — yet most people rely on the generic "8 glasses a day" guideline that may be too much or too little. Studies show that people who track water intake drink 27% more water than non-trackers. Dehydration-related healthcare costs exceed $5 billion annually, including kidney stones, UTIs, and heat-related illness.

---

## Solution

1. **Personalized Daily Goal** — Calculates optimal water intake based on body weight, activity level, and local climate/weather.
2. **Quick-Log Interface** — One-tap logging for common amounts (8 oz glass, 16 oz bottle, 32 oz bottle).
3. **Progress Visualization** — Animated water fill meter showing daily progress toward your goal.
4. **Smart Reminders** — Configurable reminder intervals that pause during sleep hours and increase during exercise.
5. **Beverage Variety** — Track water, tea, coffee, sparkling water, and other beverages with hydration multipliers.
6. **Weekly/Monthly Trends** — Charts showing hydration consistency over time with streaks and averages.
7. **Widget Support** — Home screen widget for quick logging and progress checking without opening the app.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **WaterMinder** — Popular water tracking app, $4.99 one-time; feature-rich but complex for simple tracking.
2. **Plant Nanny** — Gamified water tracker where a virtual plant grows; cute but gimmicky and loses appeal.
3. **MyFitnessPal** — Includes water tracking but buried within comprehensive calorie/macro tracking, overwhelming for hydration-only users.
4. **Apple Health / Google Fit** — Basic water logging available but minimal goal-setting and no reminders.
5. **HidrateSpark** — Smart water bottle ($60) with app; excellent but requires proprietary hardware purchase.
