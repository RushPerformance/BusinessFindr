## Problem

The average U.S. household now has 22 connected devices, up from 10 in 2019. Yet 78% of households report Wi-Fi dead zones — areas where signal is weak or nonexistent. Poor Wi-Fi placement wastes bandwidth: a router in the wrong spot can reduce effective coverage by 50%. Americans spend $2.3 billion annually on Wi-Fi extenders and mesh systems, but many purchases are unnecessary — proper router placement alone can eliminate 60% of dead zones. Remote workers lose an estimated 40 minutes per week to connectivity issues in home offices. Most people place their router wherever the cable enters the house, not where it provides optimal coverage.

---

## Solution

1. **Walking Survey** — Walk through your home with your phone; the app continuously measures and geo-maps Wi-Fi signal strength.
2. **Heat Map Visualization** — Color-coded floor plan overlay showing signal strength from strong (green) to weak (red).
3. **Dead Zone Identification** — Clearly marks areas with insufficient signal for video calls, streaming, or general use.
4. **Router Placement Recommendations** — Suggests the optimal location for your router based on your home's signal map.
5. **Extender Placement Guide** — If extenders are needed, recommends exact placement for maximum coverage improvement.
6. **Before/After Comparison** — Re-scan after moving equipment to verify improvement and compare heat maps.
7. **Speed Test Integration** — Measures actual download/upload speeds at each location, not just signal strength.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **NetSpot** — Professional Wi-Fi survey tool, powerful but $49-$149 and complex for consumer use.
2. **Wi-Fi Analyzer (Android)** — Shows signal strength for nearby networks but doesn't create spatial heat maps.
3. **Eero/Google Wifi Apps** — Show signal strength between their own mesh nodes, only work with their hardware.
4. **WiFi Heatmap** — Basic heat map tool, limited functionality and poor user experience on mobile.
