---

## Problem

There are over 8,600 farmers markets operating in the United States, generating $3 billion in annual sales. Yet discovering markets remains surprisingly difficult — USDA's farmer market directory is outdated (30% of listings have incorrect hours or are defunct), and there's no single source for what vendors attend, what's in season, or whether SNAP/EBT is accepted. The 42 million Americans receiving SNAP benefits can use them at many farmers markets but don't know which ones participate. Vendors lose an estimated 15-20% of potential customers who simply don't know the market exists or what's available that week. Market managers rely on Facebook posts and word-of-mouth, missing reach to potential new customers.

---

## Solution

1. **Market Locator Map** — GPS-based map showing farmers markets near you with hours, dates, and directions.
2. **Vendor Directory** — Searchable list of vendors at each market with what they sell and their typical availability.
3. **Seasonal Produce Calendar** — What's in season at your local markets this week based on your region.
4. **SNAP/EBT Filter** — Filter markets that accept SNAP benefits, with details on Double Up Food Bucks programs.
5. **Vendor Profiles** — Farm stories, growing practices (organic, conventional, regenerative), and contact information.
6. **Market Alerts** — Notifications for market opening day, special events, holiday markets, and vendor spotlights.
7. **Favorites & Lists** — Save favorite markets and vendors, build shopping lists based on seasonal availability.

---

## Existing Alternatives

1. **USDA Farmers Market Directory** — Official database but outdated, no vendor details, clunky web interface.
2. **LocalHarvest** — Directory of local food sources including farmers markets; functional but dated design and limited mobile experience.
3. **Facebook Pages** — Individual market Facebook pages with updates, but fragmented and not searchable across markets.
4. **Yelp** — Some farmers markets listed but treated like restaurants, no seasonal or vendor-specific data.

---

## Why This Wins

- **Comprehensive and current** — combines USDA data with crowdsourced updates for the most accurate market directory.
- **SNAP/EBT focus** — serving an underserved population with real nutritional and financial impact.
- **Vendor revenue model** — vendors pay for enhanced listings rather than consumers, keeping the app free.
- **Seasonal intelligence** — knowing what's available this week drives repeat visits and builds market loyalty.
- **Community building** — connects consumers directly with local farmers, supporting the local food economy.
