## Problem

The global footwear market is worth $85 billion, with online shoe sales representing 35% and growing. Yet shoe sizing remains wildly inconsistent — a Nike size 10 fits differently from an Adidas size 10, which fits differently from a New Balance size 10. Online shoe return rates average 30-40%, the highest of any apparel category, with "wrong size" cited as the reason in 65% of returns. This costs retailers an estimated $7 billion annually in return shipping and processing. Consumers waste hours reading reviews about sizing ("runs small," "runs large") and often order multiple sizes to try at home. International size conversions (US/UK/EU/JP) add another layer of confusion for travelers and international shoppers.

---

## Solution

1. **Brand-Specific Converter** — Convert your known size in one brand to the equivalent in another, accounting for brand-specific fit differences.
2. **Foot Measurement Guide** — Step-by-step instructions to measure foot length and width at home using your phone.
3. **International Conversion** — Instant conversion between US, UK, EU, JP, and KR sizing systems.
4. **Fit Database** — Crowdsourced data on whether specific models run small, true-to-size, or large.
5. **Width Recommendations** — Guidance on which brands/models run narrow vs. wide for different foot shapes.
6. **My Size Profile** — Save your measurements and see your recommended size across all major brands.
7. **Purchase Links** — Direct links to buy in your recommended size with affiliate tracking.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **Amazon Size Charts** — Generic conversion charts per listing, no brand comparison or fit intelligence.
2. **FeetLot** — Shoe size comparison between two brands, limited database and basic interface.
3. **RunRepeat** — Running shoe reviews with some sizing commentary, limited to athletic shoes.
4. **Brand Size Guides** — Individual brand websites have their own guides but no cross-brand comparison.
5. **True Fit (B2B)** — Enterprise fit recommendation engine embedded in retailer websites, not consumer-facing.

## Why This Wins

- **Cross-brand intelligence** — the only tool that compares sizing across hundreds of brands, not just within one.
- **Crowdsourced accuracy** — real customer fit data is more accurate than manufacturer size charts.
- **Free for users** — affiliate model means zero friction, maximum adoption.
- **Reduces returns** — retailers benefit from fewer returns, creating potential B2B partnership opportunities.
- **Sneakerhead community** — passionate shoe buyers who research sizing obsessively are natural power users and evangelists.
