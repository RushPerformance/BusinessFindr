## Problem

There are 130,000 K-12 schools across the United States, and in northern states, schools close for weather an average of 3-7 days per year. Parents need to know about closures as early as possible to arrange childcare — last-minute closures cost families an estimated $1.3 billion annually in emergency childcare and lost wages. Students and teachers check weather obsessively the night before potential snow days. Most school districts don't announce closures until 5-6 AM, too late for many working parents. Historical data shows that snow day decisions follow predictable patterns — specific snowfall thresholds, temperature cutoffs, and timing patterns — that a well-trained model can predict with 80-90% accuracy the evening before.

---

## Solution

1. **Snow Day Probability Score** — Percentage chance of closure for your specific school district, updated hourly as forecasts evolve.
2. **District Profiles** — Learns each district's historical closure patterns and decision thresholds over time.
3. **Multi-School Tracking** — Parents with kids in different schools/districts see all predictions in one view.
4. **Push Notifications** — Alerts when probability crosses configurable thresholds (e.g., notify at 60%+).
5. **Historical Accuracy** — Shows the app's prediction accuracy for your district to build trust.
6. **Delay vs. Closure** — Separate predictions for 2-hour delays, early dismissals, and full closures.
7. **Social Feed** — Community discussion for each district where parents and students share intel and excitement.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **SnowDayCalculator.com** — The original snow day predictor website; basic and web-only, limited district-specific learning.
2. **Local News Apps** — TV station apps announce closures but only after the official decision, no prediction.
3. **Weather.com / AccuWeather** — Weather forecasts but no school-specific closure prediction.
4. **School District Apps (Remind, ParentSquare)** — Official closure announcements only, no advance prediction.

---

## Why This Wins

- **Prediction vs. notification** — predicts closures 8-12 hours before official announcements, giving parents time to plan.
- **District-specific learning** — improves accuracy over seasons by learning each district's unique decision patterns.
- **Built-in virality** — students share with friends, parents share with other parents; snow days are inherently social.
- **Seasonal engagement** — November through March drives intense, habitual usage in snow-belt states.
- **Emotional connection** — snow days are among the most exciting events in a student's year, creating strong brand affinity.
