## Problem

The average American household spends $1,700 per year on clothing, and stains are the #1 reason garments are discarded prematurely. Google searches for "how to remove [stain type]" total over 5 million per month in the U.S. alone. The problem is that stain removal is highly specific — the correct treatment varies dramatically based on both the stain source (wine, grease, ink, blood, grass) and the fabric (cotton, silk, polyester, wool). Generic "stain removal" advice frequently damages garments — using hot water on blood sets the stain permanently, using bleach on silk destroys the fabric. Dry cleaning costs $3-$15 per garment, and many stains can be treated at home with the right technique, saving $200-$400/year.

---

## Solution

1. **Stain + Fabric Selector** — Choose the stain type and fabric to get the exact correct treatment method.
2. **Step-by-Step Instructions** — Clear, numbered treatment steps with timing guidance (soak for 30 minutes, blot don't rub, etc.).
3. **Product Recommendations** — Specific cleaning products needed for each treatment with purchase links.
4. **Do's and Don'ts** — Critical warnings (never use hot water on blood, never rub silk) to prevent making stains worse.
5. **Emergency Mode** — Quick-access treatment for fresh stains when you're out (restaurant, office) with on-hand materials.
6. **Photo Before/After** — Document stain treatment results to track what works.
7. **Favorites** — Save frequently needed treatments (parents with young kids bookmark the same 5-10 stains repeatedly).
