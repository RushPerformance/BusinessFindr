## Problem

"Best sunset spots near me" is searched over 300,000 times per month on Google. Photographers spend hours scouting locations only to arrive and find obstructed views or unfavorable weather. Tourists visiting new cities waste golden hour opportunities on poorly chosen viewpoints. The difference between a mediocre and spectacular sunset viewing experience depends on elevation, western exposure, cloud cover, air quality, and obstruction-free horizon — factors that generic "things to do" apps don't evaluate. Weather apps tell you sunrise/sunset times but not whether the sky will be photogenic. Travel blogs list "top sunset spots" but don't account for seasonal sun position changes that make some spots great in summer but terrible in winter.

---

## Solution

1. **Viewpoint Map** — Curated and crowdsourced sunset/sunrise viewpoints with photos, directions, and accessibility info.
2. **Sky Quality Forecast** — Predicts sunset color vibrancy based on cloud cover, humidity, aerosols, and atmospheric conditions.
3. **Sun Position Calculator** — Shows exactly where on the horizon the sun will set from any viewpoint on any date.
4. **Best-Time Alerts** — Push notifications when weather conditions predict an exceptional sunset in your area.
5. **Photo Gallery** — Community-uploaded sunset photos from each viewpoint showing actual results by season.
6. **Seasonal Recommendations** — Different viewpoint suggestions based on seasonal sun angle changes.
7. **Trip Planning** — Find the best sunset spots at travel destinations and plan arrival timing.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **PhotoPills** — Photography planning app with sun position tools; powerful but complex ($10.99, steep learning curve).
2. **The Photographer's Ephemeris** — Sun/moon position planning; professional tool, not designed for casual sunset seekers.
3. **SunCalc** — Web-based sun position tool; functional but no viewpoint recommendations or community features.
4. **TripAdvisor/Yelp** — Some sunset viewpoints listed as attractions, but no timing, weather, or sky quality data.

## Why This Wins

- **For everyone, not just photographers** — casual sunset watchers vastly outnumber serious photographers; the app serves both.
- **Sky quality prediction** — the unique differentiator; knowing tonight's sunset will be spectacular is the killer feature.
- **Seasonal intelligence** — sun position changes mean the best spot in January isn't the best spot in July.
- **Social sharing natural fit** — sunset photos are among the most shared content on social media, driving organic discovery.
- **Low-commitment premium** — $1.99/mo for "will tonight's sunset be worth watching?" alerts is easy to justify.
