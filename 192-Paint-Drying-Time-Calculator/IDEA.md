## Problem

Americans spend $22 billion annually on paint and painting supplies. Recoating too early is the #1 DIY painting mistake — it causes peeling, bubbling, and uneven finishes that require sanding and repainting, wasting $50-$200 in materials and hours of labor. Paint can labels state generic drying times ("dry to touch in 1 hour, recoat in 4 hours") but actual times vary dramatically with conditions: high humidity can double dry times, cold temperatures can triple them, and lack of ventilation extends drying by 50% or more. Approximately 35% of DIY paint jobs require touch-ups or redo due to premature recoating. Professional painters intuitively adjust timing; homeowners don't have that experience.

---

## Solution

1. **Condition-Based Calculator** — Input paint type (latex, oil, primer), temperature, humidity, and ventilation for accurate dry/recoat times.
2. **Auto-Weather Integration** — Pulls current local temperature and humidity to pre-fill conditions.
3. **Timer with Alerts** — Set a timer when you finish a coat and get notified when it's safe to recoat.
4. **Paint Type Guide** — Drying characteristics for common paint types: latex, oil-based, chalk paint, spray paint, stain.
5. **Multi-Room Tracker** — Track drying status across multiple rooms when painting different areas simultaneously.
6. **Best Conditions Advisor** — Recommends the optimal time of day/week to paint based on upcoming weather forecast.
7. **Supply Calculator** — Estimate paint quantity needed by room dimensions and number of coats.
