## Problem

Recycling contamination rates in the United States average 25% — meaning one in four items placed in recycling bins doesn't belong there. This contamination costs municipal recycling programs $300 million annually in sorting and disposal. There are 7 resin identification codes for plastics alone, but most curbside programs only accept #1 and #2. The average consumer can't distinguish between these symbols or know which ones their local program accepts. "Wish-cycling" (tossing items in recycling hoping they'll be recycled) contaminates entire loads, sometimes sending them to landfill. Surveys show 62% of Americans are confused about what can and can't be recycled in their municipality.

---

## Solution

1. **Symbol Scanner** — Point your camera at a recycling symbol and get instant identification of the material type.
2. **Local Rules Lookup** — Enter your ZIP code to see which materials your local curbside program actually accepts.
3. **Symbol Database** — Comprehensive guide to all recycling symbols, resin codes, and material identification marks.
4. **Accept/Reject Verdict** — Clear thumbs-up or thumbs-down for whether the scanned item is recyclable in your area.
5. **Drop-Off Locations** — For materials not accepted curbside, find nearby drop-off locations for specialty recycling.
6. **Common Mistakes Guide** — Top 10 "wish-cycling" items that contaminate recycling (plastic bags, greasy pizza boxes, etc.).
7. **Impact Tracker** — Track how many items you've correctly recycled and estimated environmental impact.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **Earth911** — Recycling information website with search functionality, but web-only and no camera scanning.
2. **iRecycle** — Recycling location finder app, limited symbol identification, dated interface.
3. **Municipal Recycling Websites** — Each city has its own guide, but fragmented and not mobile-friendly.
4. **How2Recycle Labels** — Growing label standard on packaging, but still only on 30% of products.

## Why This Wins

- **Camera-first interaction** — scanning is faster and more intuitive than searching or reading labels.
- **Localized accuracy** — recycling rules vary dramatically by municipality; this app gives your specific answer.
- **Municipal partnership potential** — cities can promote the app to reduce contamination, offsetting marketing costs.
- **Environmental brand affinity** — sustainability-focused apps build strong emotional connections with eco-conscious users.
- **Educational mission** — teaching people to recycle correctly has lasting behavioral impact beyond app usage.
