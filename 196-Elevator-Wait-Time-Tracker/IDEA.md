## Problem

There are approximately 900,000 elevators in the United States, and office workers in tall buildings spend an estimated 16 hours per year waiting for elevators. In buildings with 20+ floors, peak-time elevator waits regularly exceed 3-5 minutes, yet people have no data to inform their decisions — they stand and wait with no idea if the elevator is 30 seconds away or 5 minutes. During morning rush (8:30-9:30 AM) and lunch hour (12:00-1:00 PM), elevator congestion in major commercial buildings causes significant productivity loss. Building residents and workers develop informal strategies (using service elevators, timing arrivals) but these aren't shared systematically.

---

## Solution

1. **Wait Time Reporting** — Quick one-tap report of how long you waited for an elevator at a specific building and time.
2. **Building Profiles** — Average wait times by hour for registered buildings, built from crowdsourced data.
3. **Peak Time Alerts** — Notifications showing the busiest and quietest times for your building's elevators.
4. **Elevator Bank Comparison** — In buildings with multiple elevator banks, compare which bank has shorter waits.
5. **Stairs vs. Elevator Calculator** — Calculates whether walking a certain number of floors is faster than the current expected wait.
6. **Building Leaderboard** — Rankings of buildings by average elevator wait time (gamification for building managers to improve).
7. **Real-Time Status** — Live reporting during peak hours to share current conditions.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **Building Management Systems** — Track elevator performance internally but don't share data with occupants.
2. **Destination Dispatch Systems (Otis, Schindler)** — Smart elevator routing in newer buildings, hardware-based and proprietary.
3. **Google Maps Indoor** — Maps building interiors but no real-time elevator performance data.
4. **Nothing Consumer-Facing** — This is largely an unaddressed problem for building occupants.

## Why This Wins
