## Problem

The U.S. houseplant market is worth $2.3 billion, with 66% of American households owning at least one houseplant. Yet overwatering is the #1 cause of houseplant death — responsible for an estimated 80% of plant fatalities. The average plant owner kills 7 plants before developing reliable care habits. Watering frequency varies dramatically by plant species (succulents every 2-3 weeks, tropical plants every 5-7 days), season (50% less in winter), and pot size. Most people either water on a rigid schedule that ignores these variables or forget entirely for weeks. New plant owners spend $20-$50 per plant only to kill it within months.

---

## Solution

1. **Plant Inventory** — Add plants by name or photo with automatic watering frequency lookup.
2. **Smart Reminders** — Push notifications when each plant needs water, adjusted by species and season.
3. **Seasonal Adjustment** — Automatically reduces watering frequency in fall/winter and increases in spring/summer.
4. **Watering Log** — Tap to mark plants as watered with timestamp history.
5. **Quick-Add Common Plants** — Pre-built profiles for the 100 most popular houseplants (pothos, snake plant, monstera, etc.).
6. **Light & Location Tags** — Note where plants are placed (bright window, low light corner) for better watering recommendations.
7. **Care Tips** — Brief care notes for each plant: light needs, humidity preferences, fertilizing schedule.
