---

## Problem

The U.S. food truck industry generates $2.7 billion in revenue annually, with over 36,000 food trucks and hundreds of thousands of street food vendors operating nationwide. The biggest challenge for both vendors and customers is discoverability — food trucks rotate locations daily or weekly, and there's no reliable way to know where a truck will be today. Vendors lose an estimated 20-30% of potential revenue due to customers not knowing their location. Customers who develop loyalty to specific trucks spend significant time checking multiple social media accounts (Instagram, Twitter, Facebook) to track down each truck's daily location. In cities like Portland, Austin, and Los Angeles, the food truck scene is vibrant but chaotic.

---

## Solution

1. **Live Vendor Map** — Real-time map showing food trucks and street vendors currently open and serving.
2. **Vendor Profiles** — Menus, photos, pricing, dietary options (vegan, halal, gluten-free), and customer ratings.
3. **Schedule & Locations** — Weekly schedule for vendors with recurring locations and special event appearances.
4. **Favorites & Alerts** — Follow your favorite vendors and get notified when they're serving near you.
5. **Cuisine Filters** — Search by food type (tacos, BBQ, Thai, desserts) or dietary preference.
6. **Vendor Self-Service** — Vendors update their own location, hours, and menu in real time.
7. **Event Integration** — Food truck rallies, festivals, and special events listed with participating vendors.
8. **Order Ahead** — Optional pre-order capability to skip the line at popular trucks.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **Roaming Hunger** — Food truck catering and booking platform; focused on events, not daily consumer discovery.
2. **Instagram/Twitter** — Where most vendors post daily locations, but requires following each vendor individually.
3. **Yelp** — Lists some food trucks but treats them like restaurants, no real-time location tracking.
4. **Google Maps** — Some food trucks are listed but locations are often outdated since trucks move daily.
5. **City-Specific Apps** — A few cities have local food truck finders (Portland, Austin) but no national solution.

---

## Why This Wins

- **Real-time location** — the critical feature that social media and review sites can't reliably provide.
- **Vendor self-service** — trucks update their own location, ensuring accuracy without admin overhead.
- **Free for customers** — vendor-funded model keeps the consumer experience frictionless.
- **Network effects by city** — once a critical mass of vendors joins in one city, the app becomes indispensable locally.
- **Event planning utility** — food truck rally organizers use the platform to coordinate and promote events.
