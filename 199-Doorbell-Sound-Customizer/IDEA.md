## Problem

Over 28 million American households now have smart doorbells (Ring, Nest, Eufy, etc.), and that number grows by 5-6 million per year. Yet customization options are severely limited — Ring offers fewer than 20 built-in tones, Nest offers fewer than 10. Shift workers, parents of napping babies, and work-from-home professionals all need different doorbell behaviors at different times of day. A doorbell at full volume at 2 PM is welcome; at 2 AM it's jarring. Smart doorbell platforms offer basic "do not disturb" modes but not nuanced volume scheduling or custom sounds. Pet owners report dogs barking at the default doorbell sound but being calm with different tones.

---

## Solution

1. **Sound Library** — Hundreds of doorbell sounds: classic chimes, modern tones, holiday themes, funny sounds, nature sounds.
2. **Custom Upload** — Record or upload your own doorbell sound from any audio file.
3. **Volume Scheduling** — Set different volume levels by time of day (loud during day, soft at night, silent during nap time).
4. **Visitor-Specific Tones** — Different sounds for recognized visitors (family gets a warm chime, delivery drivers get a standard tone).
5. **Seasonal Auto-Switch** — Holiday-themed doorbell sounds that automatically activate for Halloween, Christmas, etc.
6. **Do Not Disturb Plus** — Enhanced DND mode with exceptions for expected deliveries or family members.
7. **Compatible Device Setup** — Step-by-step integration guides for Ring, Nest, Eufy, and other popular smart doorbell brands.

## Existing Alternatives

1. **Ring App** — Built-in tone selection, but limited to fewer than 20 options with no volume scheduling.
2. **Google Home App (Nest)** — Basic chime selection for Nest devices, fewer than 10 options.
3. **Smart Home Automation (Alexa Routines, Google Routines)** — Can create some volume automation but requires technical setup and doesn't support custom sounds.
4. **Third-Party Ring Chimes** — Physical doorbell chime devices with different sounds, $30-$50 hardware purchase.
