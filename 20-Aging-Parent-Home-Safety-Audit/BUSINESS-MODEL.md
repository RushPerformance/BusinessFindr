# Business Model: Aging Parent Home Safety Audit

## Value Proposition
Adult children prevent falls — the #1 cause of injury death for seniors — by completing a guided, room-by-room home safety audit that prioritizes the $25-$200 modifications that prevent 30-40% of falls, without waiting 3-6 months for a professional assessment.

## Customer Segments
- **Primary**: Adult children ages 35-55 with aging parents (65+) who live independently. Household income $60K-$120K. They live 30+ minutes from their parents and worry about safety between visits. They've either witnessed a near-fall or are proactively concerned after a health change (hip replacement, vision decline, balance issues).
- **Secondary**: Seniors ages 65-80 who are proactive about aging in place and want to assess their own homes. Also occupational therapists looking for a structured assessment tool for clients.
- **Psychographics**: Anxious, guilt-driven, and action-oriented. They want a checklist they can complete on their next visit to Mom's house. They'll pay $20 for peace of mind. Active in the "sandwich generation" — caring for both kids and parents.

## Revenue Model
- **Free tier**: 1-room sample audit (bathroom only, since it's the highest-risk room); basic fall risk score; top 5 product recommendations
- **Paid tier**: $19.99 one-time per full audit — unlocks all rooms, comprehensive risk scoring, prioritized modification list with cost estimates, product links, before/after photo tracking, shareable family report, and contractor finder
- **Premium/Enterprise**: $4.99/month monitoring — 6-month reassessment reminders, updated product recommendations, new contractor availability, and capability change tracking as the parent ages
- **Projected conversion rate**: 15% free-to-paid (benchmarked against home assessment tools like AARP HomeFit's download-to-action rate; high emotional urgency drives conversion; one-room free trial demonstrates clear value)

## Go-to-Market: First 100 Users
1. **Caregiver Reddit communities (weeks 1-6)**: Share room-specific safety checklists in r/AgingParents (40K), r/CaregiverSupport (25K), r/eldercare (12K), and r/occupationaltherapy (35K). Post: "I audited my mom's bathroom this weekend — here are the 7 things I fixed for under $100" with photos.
2. **SEO (weeks 1-10)**: Target "home safety checklist for elderly," "how to prevent falls for seniors," "aging in place modifications," and "grab bar placement guide." These are high-intent queries from worried adult children, often searched late at night.
3. **Senior center and Area Agency on Aging partnerships (weeks 4-10)**: Contact 25 local senior centers and AAAs offering to co-brand the free bathroom audit as a community resource. They have 3-6 month waitlists for professional assessments — position the app as an immediate alternative.
4. **Home health agency referrals (weeks 6-12)**: Partner with 10 home health agencies whose nurses visit elderly patients. Nurses can recommend the app to family members. Offer agencies a co-branded version.

## Top 3 Risks
1. **User acts on recommendations without professional validation** — A non-expert might install a grab bar incorrectly or miss a hazard the app doesn't cover, creating a false sense of security. → Mitigation: Include prominent warnings that the app supplements but doesn't replace a professional OT assessment; provide installation guides and video links for all recommended modifications; flag "professional installation recommended" for complex items (stairlifts, walk-in tub conversions); partner with OTs who can offer $50 virtual consults as an upsell.
2. **One-time purchase limits revenue growth** — Unlike subscription products, most users buy once and leave. The aging parent's home doesn't change rapidly enough to justify frequent re-audits. → Mitigation: Push the $4.99/mo monitoring subscription that includes reassessment reminders as capabilities change; expand into adjacent products (medication management, emergency contact systems, caregiver coordination); build a contractor marketplace with per-referral revenue that generates income independent of audit volume.
3. **Sensitive family dynamics** — Adult children suggesting modifications to a parent's home can be perceived as patronizing, leading to family conflict and negative associations with the app. → Mitigation: Frame the app as collaborative ("Do this together with Mom") not directive; include conversation scripts for bringing up safety with a resistant parent; offer a "self-assessment" mode where the senior can audit their own home and share results; emphasize independence preservation, not loss of capability.
