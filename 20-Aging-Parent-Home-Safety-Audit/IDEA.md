## The Problem
Every year, 1 in 4 Americans aged 65+ falls, resulting in 3 million ER visits and $50B in medical costs (CDC). 90% of seniors want to age in place, but most homes aren't safe for aging. A professional home safety assessment costs $200-500 and has long waitlists through Area Agencies on Aging. Adult children worry but don't know what to look for or which modifications to prioritize. Simple changes (grab bars: $25, better lighting: $15, removing throw rugs: free) prevent 30-40% of falls.

## The Solution
1. Room-by-room guided assessment: app walks you through each room with a photo-based checklist
2. Risk scoring: rates each room and overall home on fall risk (1-10)
3. Modification recommendations prioritized by impact and cost
4. Product links: direct links to buy grab bars, non-slip mats, motion lights, etc.
5. Contractor finder: local aging-in-place remodelers for bigger modifications (walk-in showers, stairlifts)
6. Before/after comparison photos
7. Reassessment reminders every 6 months as capabilities change
8. Shareable report for family members and occupational therapists

## Existing Alternatives
- **Area Agency on Aging assessments**: Free but 3-6 month waitlists
- **Occupational therapist home visits**: $150-300, requires referral
- **AARP HomeFit Guide**: Free PDF checklist, not interactive or personalized
- **YouTube videos**: Scattered, not structured or trackable
- **Nothing**: Most families wait until after a fall to modify the home

## Why This Wins
- Interactive, guided format is far more engaging than PDF checklists
- Photo-based assessment creates a visual record families can share
- Contractor marketplace generates affiliate revenue
- Product affiliate links (Amazon, Home Depot) add passive revenue
- Emotionally resonant: "protect mom before she falls, not after"
