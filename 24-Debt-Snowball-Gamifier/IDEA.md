## The Problem
The average American has $104,215 in total debt (Experian, 2023). 77% of American households have some form of debt. Debt repayment is psychologically grueling — it takes years and provides no dopamine hits along the way. Dave Ramsey popularized the "debt snowball" (pay smallest debt first), and mathematically the "avalanche" (pay highest interest first) saves more money. But no app makes either strategy sticky and motivating. Most people start a debt payoff plan and abandon it within 3 months because progress feels invisible.

## The Solution
1. Enter all debts: balances, interest rates, minimum payments, due dates
2. Choose strategy: snowball (smallest first), avalanche (highest interest first), or custom
3. Visual payoff timeline: animated snowball or avalanche visualization that grows as you pay
4. Milestone celebrations: "You just destroyed your Best Buy card! 🎯"
5. Daily/weekly payoff counter: "You've eliminated $X,XXX in debt since joining"
6. "What if" calculator: see how an extra $50/month accelerates your payoff date
7. Community accountability: anonymous debt payoff forums organized by debt level
8. Interest saved calculator: "By using avalanche, you'll save $3,247 in interest"
9. Integration with bank accounts to auto-detect debt payments

## Existing Alternatives
- **Undebt.it**: Free web-based calculator, functional but ugly and no gamification
- **Debt Payoff Planner**: $1 one-time purchase, basic tracker, no community
- **Tally**: Automates credit card payments but recently shut down (2023)
- **YNAB**: Budgeting-focused, not debt-focused
- **Dave Ramsey's EveryDollar**: Budget app, debt is secondary

## Why This Wins
- Gamification is the missing piece — debt payoff apps are functional but not motivating
- Tally's shutdown left a gap in the automated debt management space
- Community accountability (like AA for debt) creates retention and virality
- Visual progress mechanics (watching your snowball grow) drive daily engagement
- r/debtfree and r/personalfinance communities are natural distribution channels
