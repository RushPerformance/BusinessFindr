> Weekly check-in tool for family caregivers that tracks burnout indicators, suggests respite resources, and alerts family members when a caregiver needs help.

## The Problem
53 million Americans are unpaid caregivers. 40% of caregivers experience clinical-level depression or anxiety (AARP). Caregiver burnout manifests gradually — sleep disruption, social isolation, resentment, health neglect — and caregivers often don't recognize it until crisis. There is no tool that systematically monitors caregiver wellbeing the way chronic disease apps monitor patient symptoms. Family members of the caregiver often want to help but don't know when or how.

## The Solution
1. Weekly 2-minute check-in: rate stress, sleep quality, social connection, own health care, resentment level
2. Burnout score (1-100) with trend tracking
3. Early warning alerts: "Your burnout indicators have been rising for 3 weeks"
4. Family notification: optional alert to siblings/family when burnout score is critical
5. Respite resource finder: local adult day programs, respite care services, support groups
6. Self-care task suggestions calibrated to available time (5 min, 15 min, 1 hour)
7. Community forum: connect with other caregivers in similar situations
8. Monthly wellbeing report shareable with own doctor

## Existing Alternatives
- **AARP resources**: Articles and guides, not interactive or personalized
- **Caregiver Action Network**: Helpline and resources, not an app
- **Therapy apps (BetterHelp, etc.)**: General mental health, not caregiver-specific
- **Support groups**: Helpful but time-consuming, not always accessible
- **Nothing**: Most caregivers don't actively monitor their own wellbeing

## Why This Wins
- First tool specifically designed to monitor caregiver (not patient) wellbeing
- Family alert system addresses the coordination problem among siblings
- Respite resource finder solves the "I need a break but don't know where to turn" problem
- Low-friction check-ins (2 minutes) respect that caregivers have no spare time
- Strong emotional resonance and word-of-mouth potential
