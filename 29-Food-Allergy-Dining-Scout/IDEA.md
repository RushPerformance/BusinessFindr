# Food Allergy Dining Scout

> Crowdsourced restaurant safety database for food allergy sufferers: verified allergen menus, community reviews, and "safe dish" recommendations for every major allergen.

## The Problem
32 million Americans have food allergies, including 6 million children (FARE). Dining out is anxiety-inducing: restaurant menus rarely list all allergens, staff may not know ingredients, and cross-contamination is hard to assess. Allergic reactions from restaurant food send 200,000 Americans to the ER annually. Parents of allergic children are especially anxious. Currently, allergy sufferers rely on calling restaurants ahead, scanning menus online, or trial and error.

## The Solution
1. Restaurant search by allergen safety (peanut-free, gluten-free, dairy-free, etc.)
2. Community reviews focused on allergy accommodation: "Staff was knowledgeable about cross-contamination"
3. Verified allergen menus (restaurants opt in to have menus verified)
4. "Safe dish" database: community-verified dishes at specific restaurants
5. Allergy card generator: printable cards to give to servers in 12 languages
6. Emergency info: nearest hospital, EpiPen reminders
7. Travel mode: find allergy-safe restaurants in any city
8. Chain restaurant allergen database (compiled from official allergen guides)

## Existing Alternatives
- **Find Me Gluten Free**: Celiac-specific only, not multi-allergen
- **Spokin**: Allergy app but limited restaurant database, more product-focused
- **AllergyEats**: Dated UX, limited reviews, not mobile-first
- **Google Maps + manual research**: Time-consuming, unreliable
- **Facebook allergy groups**: Helpful but not structured or searchable

## Why This Wins
- Multi-allergen platform (not just gluten-free) serves a wider market
- Community-verified "safe dishes" at specific restaurants is unique and high-value
- Allergy parents are incredibly engaged and willing to pay for their child's safety
- Restaurant verification creates a B2B revenue stream (restaurants pay to get verified badge)
- Network effects: more reviews = more value = more users
