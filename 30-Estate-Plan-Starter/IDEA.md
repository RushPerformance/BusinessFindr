## The Problem
67% of Americans don't have an estate plan (Caring.com, 2023). The average attorney-prepared estate plan costs $1,000-$3,000. Most people avoid estate planning because it's morbid, confusing, and expensive — not because they don't need it. When someone dies without a will, state intestacy laws decide who gets what, often contradicting the deceased's wishes. Additionally, most people have digital assets (email, social media, crypto, subscriptions) that aren't covered by traditional estate plans.

## The Solution
1. Guided questionnaire: family situation, assets, wishes (plain English, not legal jargon)
2. Will template generator: state-specific, covers common scenarios
3. Beneficiary audit: "Here are 7 accounts where you should update beneficiaries" (retirement, insurance, bank)
4. Digital asset inventory: list online accounts, crypto wallets, password manager access
5. Advance directive / living will template
6. Healthcare power of attorney template
7. Letter of instruction: funeral wishes, pet care, personal messages
8. Annual review reminder: "Has anything changed? Marriage, kids, new assets?"
9. Secure document vault for storing completed documents
