## The Problem
The average person has 100+ online accounts — social media, email, subscriptions, banking, cloud storage, crypto wallets, and more. When someone dies, families face a nightmare: they don't know which accounts exist, can't access them, and don't know each platform's death/memorialization policies. Facebook alone processes millions of memorialization requests annually. Meanwhile, paid subscriptions keep billing, crypto assets become inaccessible, and sensitive accounts remain vulnerable. Families spend 20-40 hours tracking down and closing digital accounts, often over months or years.

## The Solution
1. Digital account inventory: guided walkthrough to catalog all online accounts (social, financial, email, subscriptions, cloud storage, crypto)
2. Per-account instructions: what should happen to each account (memorialize, delete, transfer, archive)
3. Platform policy database: each platform's specific death/legacy process and required documentation
4. Trusted executor assignment: designate someone with instructions for each account
5. Subscription cancellation checklist with estimated monthly savings for estate
6. Crypto and digital asset transfer instructions (seed phrases, wallet locations, exchange contacts)
7. Email forwarding setup: auto-forward important emails to designated contact
8. Social media legacy instructions: last post, memorialization settings, download archives
9. Secure vault: encrypted storage for credentials, accessible only by designated executor

## Existing Alternatives
- **Google Inactive Account Manager**: Google-only, limited to Google services
- **Facebook Legacy Contact**: Facebook-only memorialization
- **Everplans**: $75/year, broader estate planning, complex
- **Dead Man's Switch**: Sends pre-written emails after inactivity, very narrow
- **Password managers (1Password, LastPass)**: Store passwords but no death-specific workflow
- **Attorneys**: Can handle digital assets but most don't understand the landscape

## Why This Wins
- Platform policy database is unique — no tool tells you exactly how to memorialize/delete each service
- One-time pricing matches the one-time nature of the task (not a subscription for something you set up once)
- Crypto transfer instructions fill a critical gap — billions in crypto are lost when holders die without sharing access
- Subscription cancellation saves the estate real money immediately, providing tangible value
- Growing urgency: the first generation with massive digital footprints is reaching estate planning age
