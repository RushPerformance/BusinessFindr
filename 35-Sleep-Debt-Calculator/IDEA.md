## The Problem
1 in 3 American adults don't get enough sleep (CDC). Sleep debt is cumulative — missing one hour per night for two weeks creates the cognitive equivalent of two full nights without sleep. Most people don't realize how impaired they are because they've adapted to feeling tired as their baseline. Chronic sleep debt increases risk of heart disease, diabetes, obesity, depression, and accidents. Yet no mainstream app focuses specifically on quantifying cumulative sleep debt and providing structured recovery plans. Sleep trackers show last night's data but don't contextualize it within your ongoing deficit.

## The Solution
1. Daily sleep logging: manual entry or sync with Apple Health/Google Fit/wearables
2. Sleep debt calculator: running total of hours owed vs. your optimal sleep need
3. Cognitive impact dashboard: "Your current sleep debt is equivalent to X — here's how it affects reaction time, memory, and mood"
4. Health risk meter: visualize how your accumulated debt correlates with health risks
5. Personalized recovery plan: "Go to bed 30 minutes earlier for the next 10 days to erase your debt"
6. Weekend recovery optimizer: how much extra sleep on weekends actually helps (and how much is wasted)
7. Nap calculator: optimal nap timing and duration to reduce debt without disrupting nighttime sleep
8. Trend analysis: weekly and monthly sleep debt trends with lifestyle correlation

## Existing Alternatives
- **Sleep Cycle**: Tracks sleep quality and wake timing, not cumulative debt
- **Oura Ring**: Hardware + app, $299 ring + $6/mo, tracks sleep stages but not debt recovery
- **Apple Health / Google Fit**: Basic sleep duration logging, no debt calculation
- **Pillow**: Sleep tracking app, iOS-only, no recovery planning
- **WHOOP**: $30/mo, focuses on strain/recovery for athletes, not general sleep debt
- **SleepScore**: Tracks sleep quality but doesn't quantify cumulative deficit

## Why This Wins

- Low-cost software solution vs. expensive hardware (Oura, WHOOP)
