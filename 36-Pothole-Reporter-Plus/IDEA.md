## The Problem
Pothole damage costs US drivers $3 billion annually in vehicle repairs (AAA). The average driver pays $300 per year in pothole-related damage — tires, alignments, suspension. Most municipalities have reporting systems, but they're buried in government websites, require finding the right department, and offer no follow-up. Citizens don't report because it's too much effort, so hazards persist for months. Meanwhile, municipalities lack data on which roads need priority attention. Cyclists face even greater danger — potholes can cause serious injuries.

## The Solution
1. One-tap hazard reporting: snap a photo, GPS auto-tags location, select hazard type (pothole, broken pavement, sinkhole, debris)
2. Auto-file municipal complaint: app determines correct department and submits report on your behalf
3. Crowdsourced hazard map: see reported hazards on your route before driving
4. Repair status tracking: follow up on your reports and see when repairs are scheduled/completed
5. Severity rating: community upvotes prioritize the worst hazards
6. Damage claim assistant: if your car is damaged, generate a claim letter to the municipality
7. Route hazard alerts: get notified about hazards on your daily commute
8. Analytics dashboard: "Your reports have led to X repairs in your city"

## Existing Alternatives
- **SeeClickFix**: Municipal issue reporting, but city-dependent and broad (not road-focused)
- **311 apps**: City-specific, inconsistent UX, no tracking
- **Waze**: Reports hazards in real-time but no municipal filing or tracking
- **Google Maps**: Can report road closures but not potholes
- **Calling city hall**: Time-consuming, no follow-up, often routed incorrectly

## Why This Wins
- Auto-filing municipal complaints removes the friction that prevents most people from reporting
- Crowdsourced hazard map creates real value for commuters and cyclists (avoid damage before it happens)
- Damage claim assistant adds financial value — helps recover repair costs
- Impact tracking ("Your reports fixed 12 potholes") creates emotional engagement and retention
- Potential for municipal partnerships: cities could pay for aggregated data to prioritize repairs
