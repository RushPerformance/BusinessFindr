## The Problem
The average US wedding costs $30,000 (The Knot, 2023), and 56% of couples exceed their original budget. Wedding planning is emotionally charged — couples make dozens of high-stakes financial decisions in a short period while under social pressure. The wedding industry is designed to upsell: "premium" packages, hidden fees, and "you only do this once" pressure. Existing wedding planning apps (The Knot, Zola) are vendor marketplaces that profit when you spend more, creating a misaligned incentive. No tool is designed specifically to protect the couple's budget.

## The Solution
1. Budget-first setup: set your total budget and allocate by category (venue, catering, photography, etc.) using industry benchmarks
2. Real-time spending tracker: log every expense and see remaining budget by category
3. Budget impact alerts: before committing to a vendor, see "This will put your catering budget 23% over — here's what you'd need to cut elsewhere"
4. Vendor cost benchmarks: "The average photographer in your zip code charges $2,800-4,200"
5. Hidden fee detector: checklist of common hidden costs (service charges, cake cutting fees, overtime rates)
6. Trade-off calculator: "If you upgrade the venue, you could downgrade flowers and DJ to stay on budget"
7. Payment schedule: track deposits, due dates, and remaining balances across all vendors
8. Post-wedding settlement: final vendor payments, tip calculator, and reconciliation

## Existing Alternatives
- **The Knot**: Free budget tool but primarily a vendor marketplace (incentivized to increase spending)
- **Zola**: Registry + planning, budget tool is basic
- **WeddingWire**: Vendor reviews and budget tracker, not budget-protection focused
- **Google Sheets**: Flexible but no benchmarks, alerts, or automation
- **Wedding Planner (human)**: $1,500-5,000, helps with logistics not budget discipline
- **Mint / YNAB**: General budgeting, not wedding-specific

## Why This Wins
- Budget-protection positioning is unique — every competitor profits from couples spending more
- Vendor cost benchmarks by zip code prevent overpaying (couples have no pricing context)
- Budget impact alerts create a "guardrail" moment before each expensive decision
- Hidden fee detector saves real money on things couples don't know to look for
- Limited-duration subscription matches the wedding planning timeline (not an indefinite commitment)
