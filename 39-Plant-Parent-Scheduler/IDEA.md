## The Problem
The houseplant market boomed during COVID and remains elevated. 77% of US adults now own houseplants, but overwatering is the #1 killer of houseplants — responsible for more plant deaths than underwatering, pests, and disease combined. Each plant species has different needs based on season, pot size, soil type, and light conditions. Most plant owners follow generic advice ("water once a week") that doesn't account for their specific environment. The result: dead plants, wasted money, and frustrated plant parents who think they have a "black thumb."

## The Solution
1. Plant library: add your plants by species (or photo ID) with pot size, soil type, and light level
2. Personalized watering schedule: calculated by species, pot size, season, and indoor conditions
3. Push notification reminders: "Time to water your Monstera" with specific amount guidance
4. Fertilizing calendar: species-specific fertilizing schedules with product recommendations
5. Repotting alerts: "Your snake plant has been in this pot for 18 months — here are signs it's time to repot"
6. Seasonal adjustments: automatic schedule changes as daylight and humidity shift through the year
7. Plant journal: log growth, problems, and treatments with photos
8. Diagnosis tool: "My plant's leaves are turning yellow" with species-specific troubleshooting

## Why This Wins
- Environmental personalization (pot size, light conditions, season) creates more accurate schedules than generic apps
- Lower price point than Planta ($3 vs. $8) targets casual plant owners, not just enthusiasts
- Repotting alerts and fertilizing schedules go beyond watering — comprehensive care in one app
- Diagnosis tool keeps users engaged even when not watering
- Plant parents are emotionally attached to their plants — willingness to pay for their survival is real
