## The Problem
100+ million Americans have credit scores below 720, limiting their access to favorable interest rates on mortgages, auto loans, and credit cards. Credit Karma and similar services show you your score for free, but they don't tell you specifically what to do about it. Generic advice ("pay bills on time, reduce utilization") isn't actionable — people need to know which specific account to pay down first, whether to dispute an error, or if becoming an authorized user would help. The difference between a 650 and 750 credit score can mean $50,000+ in extra interest payments on a mortgage.

## The Solution
1. Credit score analysis: connect credit report and see score with factor breakdown
2. Personalized action plan: ranked list of specific actions to improve your score, ordered by expected impact
3. Utilization optimizer: "Pay $400 on your Chase card to drop utilization below 30% — estimated +15 points"
4. Dispute assistant: identify potential errors on your credit report and generate dispute letters
5. Score simulator: "What happens if I pay off this card? Open a new card? Close an old account?"
6. Authorized user strategy: identify family/friends whose accounts could boost your score
7. Payment impact tracker: see your score change over time as you complete action items
8. Goal-based planning: "I need a 720 to qualify for a mortgage — here's your 6-month roadmap"
9. Credit monitoring: alerts for new inquiries, new accounts, and score changes

## Existing Alternatives
- **Credit Karma**: Free score + monitoring, but monetizes via financial product recommendations (misaligned incentives)
- **Experian Boost**: Adds utility/phone payments to credit file, narrow use case
- **Credit Sesame**: Similar to Credit Karma, free score with product recommendations
- **MyFICO**: $29.95/mo for FICO scores, informational but not action-oriented
- **Credit repair companies**: $50-150/mo, often scammy, dispute-focused only
- **Self Credit Builder**: $25 account to build credit, single strategy

## Why This Wins
- Action-oriented approach is the key differentiator — not just "here's your score" but "here's exactly what to do"
- Score simulator lets users see the impact before taking action (reduces anxiety)
- Utilization optimizer with specific dollar amounts is uniquely actionable
- No misaligned incentives: subscription model means the app profits from improvement, not product sales
- Goal-based planning (mortgage readiness, auto loan prep) creates urgency and clear timelines
