# Business Model: Noise Complaint Documenter

## Value Proposition
Chronic noise sufferers get a court-ready evidence package that forces landlords and authorities to act -- turning months of ignored verbal complaints into documented, legally actionable cases in under 2 weeks.

## Customer Segments
- **Primary**: Urban renters aged 25-45, living in multi-unit apartments, dealing with chronic noise (upstairs neighbors, barking dogs, construction). Skews female (55%), college-educated, income $40K-$80K. Psychographically frustrated, sleep-deprived, and feeling powerless against unresponsive landlords.
- **Secondary**: Suburban homeowners aged 35-65 in HOA communities dealing with neighbor disputes (loud music, barking dogs, backyard parties). More likely to escalate formally through HOA or municipal code enforcement.
- **Tertiary**: Property managers and landlords who need documentation to justify enforcement actions against problem tenants.

## Revenue Model
- **Free tier**: Manual noise logging (up to 5 entries), basic decibel meter, one complaint letter template
- **Paid tier**: $4.99/month -- Unlimited noise logging, calibrated decibel recording, evidence package PDF generator, full template library, noise ordinance lookup, escalation guide
- **One-time purchase**: $14.99 -- Lifetime access to all paid features (for users with a single dispute)
- **Projected conversion rate**: 8% (benchmarked against Decibel X Pro's ~6-10% conversion on utility-focused apps with high pain points)

## Go-to-Market: First 100 Users
- **Reddit**: Post detailed noise resolution guides in r/Apartments (430K members), r/neighborsfromhell (95K), r/legaladvice (3.2M), r/TenantHelp (15K). Share success stories of resolved noise disputes using documentation.
- **SEO content**: Target "how to document noise complaints," "noise complaint letter template," "decibel levels for noise ordinance" -- high-intent searches with low competition.
- **TikTok/Instagram Reels**: Create relatable "noisy neighbor" content showing the app in action -- this category has strong viral potential (noise complaint content regularly hits 1M+ views).
- **Apartment review forums**: Partner with ApartmentRatings.com and post in Facebook groups for local apartment complexes.
- **Legal aid clinics**: Provide free access to tenant legal aid organizations who frequently handle noise disputes.

## Top 3 Risks
1. **Low retention after dispute resolves** -- Users churn once their noise issue is fixed. Mitigation: One-time purchase option captures full value upfront; build ongoing monitoring features for users in noisy areas.
2. **Decibel accuracy on consumer phones** -- Phone microphones vary widely in calibration. Mitigation: Provide calibration guidance, show relative readings with disclaimers, and note that even uncalibrated data shows patterns over time.
3. **Legal liability from evidence packages** -- Users may rely on reports in legal proceedings. Mitigation: Clear disclaimers that reports are documentation aids, not legal instruments; partner with legal review services for users pursuing formal action.
