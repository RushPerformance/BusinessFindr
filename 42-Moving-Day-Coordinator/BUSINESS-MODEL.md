# Business Model: Moving Day Coordinator

## Value Proposition
Movers complete every administrative task on time and lose zero security deposits -- replacing the chaos of scattered notes with a single guided timeline that handles the 80+ tasks most people forget.

## Customer Segments
- **Primary**: First-time apartment renters aged 22-30, moving within or between cities, income $35K-$65K. Psychographically anxious about adulting logistics, overwhelmed by the number of entities that need address updates, and willing to pay $10 for peace of mind.
- **Secondary**: Families aged 30-45 relocating for jobs, managing a more complex move (kids' school transfers, spouse's job, multiple vehicles). Higher willingness to pay, more tasks to manage.
- **Tertiary**: Military families and frequent movers (corporate relocations) who move every 2-3 years and value a repeatable system.

## Revenue Model
- **Free tier**: Basic checklist (20 tasks), move date countdown, manual box inventory (up to 10 boxes)
- **Paid tier**: $9.99 one-time per move -- Full 80+ task timeline, address change hub with direct links, utility transfer manager, security deposit photo documentation, box inventory with search, post-move checklist
- **Premium/Enterprise**: $19.99 per move -- Everything in paid, plus moving company quote comparison, shared access for household members, priority email support
- **Projected conversion rate**: 12% (benchmarked against Sortly's conversion rate for one-time utility purchases with clear immediate value)

## Go-to-Market: First 100 Users
- **Reddit**: Share moving tips and checklists in r/moving (35K members), r/Apartments (430K), r/FirstTimeHomeBuyer (120K), r/personalfinance (18M). Post "The complete moving checklist I wish I had" content.
- **SEO**: Target "moving checklist," "what to do when you move," "address change checklist," "how to get security deposit back" -- these are seasonal high-volume searches (peaks May-September).
- **College move-in season**: Partner with university housing offices and post in college Facebook groups in August/September.
- **Real estate agent referrals**: Provide agents with free codes to share with closing clients -- natural touchpoint at the moment of need.
- **Moving company partnerships**: Offer co-branded versions to small moving companies as a value-add for their customers.

## Top 3 Risks
1. **Seasonal revenue concentration** -- 60% of moves happen May-September, creating cash flow valleys. Mitigation: Build content marketing flywheel that captures intent year-round; target military/corporate moves that happen off-season.
2. **Low repeat purchase frequency** -- Average American moves every 5-7 years. Mitigation: Focus on volume (28M movers/year is a massive funnel); encourage gifting to friends who are moving; add referral incentives.
3. **Competition from free checklists** -- Moving.com, USPS, and real estate sites offer free static checklists. Mitigation: Differentiate on interactivity (personalized timeline, box search, deposit photos) -- the value is in the dynamic tool, not the list itself.
