## The Problem
There's no Kelley Blue Book for babysitting rates. Parents don't know if they're overpaying or underpaying, and babysitters don't know what to charge. Rates vary wildly by geography, experience, number of children, and duties (driving, cooking, homework help). Underpaying drives away quality sitters; overpaying strains family budgets. Babysitters — often teenagers or young adults — feel awkward negotiating rates. The result is an opaque market where both sides feel uncertain, leading to uncomfortable conversations and mismatched expectations.

## The Solution
1. Rate calculator: enter zip code, number of kids, ages, hours, and special duties to see the local going rate
2. Rate range display: low, average, and high rates for your area with data sources
3. Sitter rate card builder: babysitters create professional rate cards listing their services and prices
4. Duty-based pricing: additional rate suggestions for driving, overnights, pets, cooking, and multiple families
5. Holiday and late-night premium calculator: what to pay on New Year's Eve, after midnight, etc.
6. Tip guide: appropriate tipping norms for regular sitters, date night sitters, and nannies
7. Rate comparison by city: see how your area compares to nearby cities
8. Annual rate review: inflation-adjusted rate suggestions for long-term sitter relationships

## Existing Alternatives
- **Care.com**: Job board with some rate data, but rates are self-reported by sitters, not market-based
- **UrbanSitter**: Booking platform with rate ranges, focused on booking not rate education
- **Sittercity**: Similar to Care.com, limited rate transparency
- **Facebook parent groups**: Anecdotal rate discussions, inconsistent and localized
- **Google search**: "How much to pay a babysitter in [city]" returns outdated or generic articles

## Why This Wins
- Solves a specific, universal pain point for both parents and sitters
- Sitter rate card builder empowers babysitters (typically young people) to present rates professionally
- Duty-based pricing goes beyond hourly rate to capture the real complexity of childcare
- Low price point ($2) means impulse-purchase territory with massive volume potential
- Two-sided value: useful for both the parent hiring and the sitter being hired
