# Curb Appeal Scorer

> AI scores your home's curb appeal from a photo and suggests improvements ranked by ROI for resale value.

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Category | Home |
| Target Audience | Mass Market — homeowners preparing to sell or improve their property |
| Monetization | Hybrid — $4.99 per report or $2.99/mo subscription |
| Recession Resistance | Low-Medium — home improvement spending declines in recessions, but sellers always need to maximize value |
| Solo-Dev MVP Timeline | 6 weeks |
| Composite Score | 46/100 |

## The Problem
Curb appeal is the #1 factor in a buyer's first impression — 76% of real estate agents say it's the most important thing sellers can improve (NAR). Yet homeowners have no objective way to assess their home's exterior appearance. They either over-invest in improvements that don't add value (expensive landscaping that buyers don't care about) or miss cheap fixes that would dramatically improve perception (power washing, new house numbers, front door paint). The $510B home improvement market is driven largely by guesswork about what actually matters for resale.

## The Solution
1. Photo analysis: snap a photo of your home's exterior, AI scores curb appeal (1-100)
2. Factor breakdown: scores for landscaping, paint/siding condition, front door, driveway, lighting, and overall symmetry
3. Improvement suggestions: ranked list of changes ordered by ROI (cost vs. estimated value added)
4. Before/after visualization: AI-generated preview of how suggested changes would look
5. Cost estimates: approximate cost for each improvement in your area
6. Seasonal recommendations: best improvements for spring vs. fall selling
7. Neighborhood comparison: how your curb appeal compares to recently sold homes in your area
8. Contractor connection: find local pros for recommended improvements

## Existing Alternatives
- **Real estate agent opinions**: Subjective, available only when listing
- **Zillow/Redfin**: Show home values but don't assess or improve curb appeal
- **HomeAdvisor**: Contractor matching, no curb appeal assessment
- **Pinterest/Houzz**: Inspiration boards but no personalized analysis of your home
- **AI home design apps**: Interior-focused (room design), not exterior curb appeal
- **Professional stagers**: $500-5,000, focus on interior staging

## Why This Wins
- AI-powered objectivity replaces subjective opinions — data-driven curb appeal is a new category
- ROI-ranked improvements prevent wasted spending on low-impact changes
- Before/after visualization is compelling and shareable (marketing gold)
- Per-report pricing captures value at the moment of highest motivation (listing a home)
- Potential real estate agent partnership: agents recommend to sellers before listing
