## The Problem
Americans pay $28 billion in parking fees and fines annually. The average parking ticket costs $50-100, and the average urban driver gets 1-3 tickets per year. Meters expire while you're in a meeting, appointment, or store. Street cleaning schedules are posted on tiny signs that are easy to miss, resulting in $65-100 tickets. Most parking apps (ParkMobile, SpotHero) help you find or pay for parking but don't actively prevent tickets by alerting you before time expires. The mental load of tracking parking time creates low-grade anxiety during every errand.

## The Solution
1. Timer with alerts: set your parking duration and get push notifications at 10 minutes and 5 minutes before expiration
2. One-tap extension: where supported by parking apps/meters, add time without returning to your car
3. Street cleaning calendar: enter your address or park location, see cleaning schedule and get alerts the night before
4. Meter rate comparison: see prices at nearby meters/lots to find the cheapest option
5. Parking history: track spending on parking by month (meters, garages, tickets)
6. Ticket appeal assistant: if you do get a ticket, guidance on when and how to appeal
7. Favorite spots: save your regular parking locations with their rules and schedules
8. Neighborhood parking rules: digest complex signage into plain-English summaries

## Existing Alternatives
- **ParkMobile**: Pay for parking, but limited expiration alerts
- **SpotHero**: Find and book parking, garage-focused, not street parking
- **Meter Beater (various)**: Simple timer apps, no cleaning schedules or payment extension
- **Phone timer/alarm**: Manual, no context, easy to forget to set
- **Google Calendar**: Can set reminders but requires manual setup each time

## Why This Wins
- Street cleaning calendar integration prevents the most frustrating and expensive tickets
- One-tap payment extension solves the "I need 10 more minutes" problem without walking back
- Ticket appeal assistant adds value after the problem occurs (recovery, not just prevention)
- Parking spending tracker reveals how much people actually spend — often a shock that drives retention
- Low subscription price ($2/mo) pays for itself with a single prevented ticket
