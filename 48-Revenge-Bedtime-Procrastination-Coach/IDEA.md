## The Problem
"Revenge bedtime procrastination" is the phenomenon of delaying sleep to reclaim personal time lost to a demanding day. It affects an estimated 40-50% of adults, particularly working parents and high-stress professionals. People who feel they had no personal time during the day stay up 1-3 hours past their intended bedtime scrolling social media, watching shows, or browsing the internet. They know they should sleep but feel they "deserve" this time. The result: chronic sleep deprivation, worse next-day performance, and a vicious cycle where tiredness makes the next day feel even more demanding.

## The Solution
1. Bedtime procrastination assessment: identify your triggers, patterns, and underlying needs
2. "Me time" audit: calculate how much personal time you actually get vs. how much you need
3. Micro-routine builder: 10-30 minute satisfying wind-down activities that replace doomscrolling (journaling prompts, stretching sequences, creative exercises, puzzles)
4. Gradual bedtime shift: move bedtime earlier by 10 minutes per week to avoid feeling deprived
5. Evening boundary alerts: "It's 10 PM — start your wind-down routine" with a satisfying ritual suggestion
6. Screen-time replacement: curated offline activities that satisfy the "me time" need without blue light
7. Morning "me time" reallocation: help shift personal time to mornings for people who are willing
8. Progress tracking: sleep time trends, personal satisfaction scores, and energy level improvements

## Existing Alternatives
- **Sleep apps (Calm, Headspace)**: Meditation/sleep stories for falling asleep, don't address procrastination behavior
- **Screen time tools (iOS Screen Time, Digital Wellbeing)**: Block apps but don't address the underlying need for personal time
- **Therapy/CBT-I**: Clinical insomnia treatment, not designed for behavioral procrastination
- **Sleep hygiene articles**: Generic advice that doesn't address the emotional driver
- **Alarm apps**: Wake you up, don't help you go to bed

## Why This Wins
- Names and addresses a specific, recognized phenomenon (revenge bedtime procrastination went viral as a concept)
- Addresses the root cause (unmet personal time needs) rather than just the symptom (late bedtime)
- Micro-routine builder provides the "replacement behavior" that screen-blocking tools lack
- Gradual approach respects that this is an emotional coping mechanism, not just a bad habit
- Strong resonance with parents and overworked professionals — a large, identifiable audience
