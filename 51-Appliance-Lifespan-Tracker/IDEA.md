## The Problem
The average home has 15-20 major appliances and systems (HVAC, water heater, dishwasher, washer, dryer, refrigerator, etc.) with lifespans ranging from 6-25 years. Homeowners have no visibility into which appliances are approaching end-of-life until they fail — often catastrophically (flooding, no heat in winter). A surprise HVAC replacement costs $5,000-12,000. If you know it's coming in 6 months, you can budget and shop for deals. 67% of homeowners say unexpected appliance failures are a top financial stressor (HomeAdvisor).

## The Solution
1. Add appliances with brand, model, purchase/install date, and warranty info
2. Lifespan database: shows expected remaining life based on brand/model reliability data
3. Failure probability timeline: "Your water heater is 9 years into a 10-12 year lifespan — 40% failure probability in next 2 years"
4. Repair vs. replace calculator: "Repairing this 8-year-old dishwasher costs $350 — a new one is $600. We recommend replacing."
5. Maintenance reminders that extend appliance life (clean dryer vents, flush water heater, etc.)
6. Budget alerts: "We recommend setting aside $200/month for upcoming replacements"
7. Energy cost comparison: newer models vs. your aging unit
8. Local contractor recommendations for replacements
