## The Problem
Noise is the #2 reason people move (after cost), affecting 45 million Americans. Apartment hunters visit during the day but noise problems emerge at night. There's no way to know if a neighborhood is loud before you sign a lease. Flight paths, highway noise, construction, nightlife, and train lines create predictable noise patterns that current apartment listing sites don't show. 1 in 3 apartment residents say noise was worse than expected after moving in.

## The Solution
1. Crowdsourced noise reports: users record and submit ambient noise levels with their phone mic
2. Noise map: color-coded by average decibel level, filterable by time of day
3. Noise source tagging: traffic, construction, nightlife, airports, trains, neighbors
4. Building-level ratings: "This apartment complex has thin walls" reviews
5. Time-of-day profiles: "This block is 45dB at 2pm but 72dB at 11pm on weekends"
6. Alert system: "This address is under a flight path / near a freeway on-ramp"
7. Soundproofing tips and product recommendations for noisy areas
8. Integration potential with apartment listing platforms

## Existing Alternatives
- **HowLoud**: Noise scores based on transit/airport data, but no crowdsourced real-world levels
- **SoundPrint**: Restaurant noise levels only
- **Apartment review sites**: Occasional noise mentions but not structured data
- **Walking the neighborhood**: Time-consuming and unreliable (one visit ≠ typical conditions)

## Why This Wins
- Crowdsourced real-world decibel data is more accurate than modeled estimates (HowLoud)
- Time-of-day profiles solve the "visited during the day, loud at night" problem
- Building-level reviews fill a gap that apartment listing sites don't address
- Strong network effects: more users = more data = better maps = more users
- B2B licensing opportunity to real estate platforms
