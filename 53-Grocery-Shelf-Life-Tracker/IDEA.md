## The Problem
The average American household wastes 31.9% of purchased food, worth ~$1,500/year (USDA). The top reason: forgetting about items until they spoil. People buy groceries, push older items to the back of the fridge, and discover them weeks later. The average fridge contains $60 worth of food that will be thrown away before it's eaten. Families with children waste even more. The environmental impact is significant: food waste is the #1 material in US landfills.

## The Solution
1. Scan grocery receipts or barcodes to auto-log purchases with estimated expiration dates
2. Fridge/pantry inventory with countdown timers
3. "Use soon" alerts: push notifications when items are approaching expiration
4. Recipe suggestions using items that need to be used first
5. Freezer tips: "Freeze this chicken by Thursday to extend its life 6 months"
6. Waste log: track what you threw away and why to identify patterns
7. Monthly waste report: "You wasted $47 of food this month, down from $63 last month"
8. Shopping list integration: don't buy what you already have

## Existing Alternatives
- **Too Good To Go**: Restaurant surplus food marketplace, not home tracking
- **NoWaste**: Basic food waste tracker, limited features, low adoption
- **Fridgely**: Fridge inventory app, minimal features
- **Supercook**: Recipe-by-ingredient, but no expiration tracking
- **Memory/habit**: Most people just check their fridge visually

## Why This Wins
- Receipt scanning automates the tedious data entry that kills food tracking apps
- Recipe suggestions for expiring items create a "use it" action, not just "you wasted it" guilt
- Monthly waste report with dollar amounts makes the savings tangible
- Environmental angle appeals to sustainability-conscious consumers
- Strong SEO: "reduce food waste app" and "fridge inventory tracker"
