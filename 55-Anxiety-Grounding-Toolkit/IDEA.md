## The Problem
40 million US adults (19.1%) have an anxiety disorder (ADAA). During a panic attack or acute anxiety episode, people need help in seconds, not minutes. Existing apps (Calm, Headspace) require navigating menus and loading content — unusable during a panic attack. CBT techniques (5-4-3-2-1 grounding, box breathing, cognitive reframing) are clinically proven but hard to remember when anxious. People need a "break glass in case of emergency" tool that works instantly, even offline.

## The Solution
1. One-tap panic button: immediately launches a guided grounding exercise (no navigation required)
2. 5-4-3-2-1 grounding: guided sensory awareness exercise with haptic feedback
3. Box breathing: visual animated guide with customizable timing
4. Cognitive reframing cards: "What evidence do I have that this fear is true?"
5. "Safe place" visualization: personalized guided imagery
6. Anxiety tracking: rate before and after exercises to show effectiveness
7. Works offline: all exercises cached locally
8. Widget: grounding exercise accessible from home screen without opening app
9. Customizable toolkit: favorite exercises pinned to top

## Why This Wins
- "Break glass in case of emergency" design: one-tap access during panic (competitors require 4+ taps)
- Offline-first: works when you're in a tunnel, on a plane, or without signal
- Widget access from home screen: zero-friction entry point
- Lower price than Calm/Headspace ($5/mo vs. $6/mo)
- CBT micro-tools are clinically validated, not just meditation
