## The Problem
26% of renters lose part or all of their security deposit when moving out, with the average loss being $600-$1,200 (Avail survey). Landlords often claim pre-existing damage was caused by the tenant. Without timestamped, organized photo evidence from move-in day, tenants have no proof. Most people take a few random photos on their phone that get lost among thousands of other images. When a dispute arises months or years later, they can't find the documentation. Security deposit disputes are the #1 landlord-tenant legal issue in small claims courts, with over 500,000 cases filed annually.

## The Solution
1. Room-by-room guided walkthrough: prompts you to photograph walls, floors, fixtures, appliances in every room
2. Damage annotation: circle and label existing damage directly on photos
3. Automatic timestamping with GPS location verification (proves when and where photos were taken)
4. Video walkthrough mode with narration support for comprehensive documentation
5. Generates a sharable PDF report: organized by room, timestamped, with annotations
6. Move-out comparison: side-by-side move-in vs. move-out photos for each area
7. Email delivery to landlord: creates a verified record that both parties received the report
8. Deposit dispute letter generator: auto-fills state-specific demand letters with evidence references

## Existing Alternatives
- **RentCheck**: Property inspection tool, but aimed at landlords/property managers, not renters
- **Phone camera**: No organization, no timestamps in report format, photos get buried
- **Written checklists**: Paper move-in forms are hard to reference later, no photo support
- **Zillow/Apartments.com**: Listing platforms with no documentation features
- **Lawyers**: Expensive ($200-500/hour) for deposit dispute representation

## Why This Wins
- Renter-first design: existing inspection tools serve landlords; this protects tenants
- Guided walkthrough ensures nothing is missed (most people forget to photograph closet interiors, under sinks, etc.)
- Timestamped PDF report is admissible evidence in small claims court
- One-time $4.99 price point is trivial compared to a $1,200 deposit at risk
- Move-out comparison feature makes disputes visually obvious and hard to contest
- State-specific dispute letter templates remove the need for legal help in most cases
