## The Problem
There are over 150,000 Little Free Libraries worldwide and thousands of informal community book exchanges, but they're nearly impossible to find systematically. The official LittleFreeLibrary.org map is incomplete and doesn't show what books are currently available. Book lovers walk past libraries without knowing they exist, and library stewards have no way to understand what their community wants. Parents looking for free children's books, students seeking textbooks, and avid readers hunting for specific titles have no efficient way to browse nearby community libraries.

## The Solution
1. Comprehensive map of Little Free Libraries, community book exchanges, and informal book-sharing spots
2. User-submitted library locations with photos and descriptions
3. Inventory snapshots: users photograph contents when they visit, creating a browsable catalog
4. Wish list feature: request specific books; get notified when one appears nearby
5. "Book drop" notifications: "Someone just stocked 12 new books at the library on Maple St"
6. Steward dashboard: library owners see visit frequency, popular books, and community requests
7. Reading challenges: "Visit 10 Little Free Libraries this month"
8. Book swap coordination: "I have X, looking for Y" matching within a neighborhood radius

## Existing Alternatives
- **LittleFreeLibrary.org**: Official map, but incomplete and no inventory tracking
- **Google Maps**: Some libraries are listed but with no book-specific features
- **Bookstagram/BookTok**: Community but no location-based discovery
- **Libby/OverDrive**: Digital public library apps, not physical community libraries
- **Nextdoor**: Occasional book sharing posts but not organized or searchable

## Why This Wins
- Inventory snapshots solve the "what's actually in there?" problem no other tool addresses
- Wish list notifications create a reason to keep the app installed and check regularly
- Strong community engagement loop: visit, photograph, share, discover
- Appeals to sustainability and buy-nothing movements gaining cultural momentum
- Low competition: LittleFreeLibrary.org is the only real player and their app is basic
- Family-friendly: parents love finding free children's books nearby
