## The Problem
US power outages have increased 64% since 2000 (Climate Central), with the average American experiencing 7+ hours of outages per year. Major outages cause $150B in annual economic damage. During an outage, homeowners face immediate questions: Is my food still safe? How long will my battery backup last? What should I unplug? When will power return? Most people are unprepared — 68% of households don't own a flashlight with working batteries (FEMA). After outages, food spoilage alone costs the average household $200-500 per event. Generator sizing mistakes are common: buying too small wastes money, too large wastes fuel.

## The Solution
1. Readiness assessment: personalized checklist based on your home, family size, climate, and outage risk
2. Generator sizing calculator: input your essential appliances, get exact wattage requirements and generator recommendations
3. Real-time outage map: pulls utility data to show active outages and estimated restoration times
4. Food safety timers: "Your fridge has been without power for 2 hours — food is still safe. At 4 hours, discard dairy and meat."
5. Battery/fuel calculator: "Your generator has 3.2 hours of fuel remaining at current load"
6. Emergency contact directory: local utility, hospital, fire department pre-loaded by location
7. Supply checklist with purchase links: flashlights, batteries, water, first aid, etc.
8. Post-outage checklist: what to check before turning systems back on (surge damage, frozen pipes, etc.)
