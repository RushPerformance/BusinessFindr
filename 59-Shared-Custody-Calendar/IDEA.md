## The Problem
Over 50% of US marriages end in divorce, and approximately 40% of children live with a single parent or in a shared custody arrangement. Co-parents must coordinate complex custody schedules (alternating weeks, split weekends, holidays, school breaks), track shared expenses ($233,610 average cost to raise a child to 18), and communicate about logistics — often while managing high-conflict relationships. Miscommunications about pickup times, forgotten expenses, and schedule disputes are the #1 source of post-divorce conflict. Courts increasingly ask for communication records, but text threads are chaotic and unsearchable.

## The Solution
1. Shared custody calendar: visualize alternating schedules, holidays, and special occasions
2. Schedule swap requests: propose changes with one tap, other parent approves/declines
3. Expense tracker: log child-related expenses with receipts, auto-calculate who owes what
4. In-app messaging: all communication in one searchable, court-admissible thread
5. Handoff logistics: "Dad is picking up at 3pm from soccer practice at Lincoln Park"
6. Medical/school info hub: shared access to doctors, medications, school contacts, allergies
7. Court-ready exports: download communication logs and expense reports as PDF
8. Multiple child support: manage different schedules for siblings
