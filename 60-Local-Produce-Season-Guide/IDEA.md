# Local Produce Season Guide

> Shows what produce is in season locally, where to find it (farmers markets, CSAs), with seasonal recipe suggestions and price comparisons.

## The Problem
Most Americans can't name which fruits and vegetables are currently in season in their region. Buying out-of-season produce means paying 2-3x more for items shipped from thousands of miles away, with lower nutritional value and worse flavor. The USDA reports that only 10% of Americans eat enough fruits and vegetables, and price is the #1 barrier. Meanwhile, 8,700+ farmers markets operate across the US, but finding them, knowing their hours, and understanding what's available is fragmented across dozens of websites and Facebook pages. CSA (Community Supported Agriculture) programs offer great value but have low visibility.

## The Solution
1. Seasonal produce calendar: what's in season right now in your specific region/state
2. Farmers market directory: locations, hours, payment methods (SNAP/EBT accepted), and vendor lists
3. CSA finder: local farm share programs with pricing, pickup locations, and season dates
4. Seasonal recipe suggestions: "It's peak peach season — here are 8 recipes"
5. Price comparison: in-season local vs. grocery store pricing
6. Harvest notifications: "Strawberry season starts next week in your area!"
7. Preservation guides: "Buy extra now and can/freeze for winter" tutorials
8. Seasonal meal planning: weekly meal plans built around what's in season

## Existing Alternatives
- **Seasonal Food Guide**: Basic website showing seasons by state, no market/CSA features
- **FarmFresh**: USDA farmers market directory, outdated and clunky
- **LocalHarvest.org**: CSA/farm search, but dated design and no mobile app
- **Grocery store apps**: No seasonal guidance, push year-round inventory
- **Pinterest/food blogs**: Seasonal recipes exist but aren't location-aware

## Why This Wins
- Location-aware seasonal data combined with farmers market discovery is unique
- Saving money on produce is a strong value proposition in any economy
- Harvest notifications create regular engagement and app reopens
- CSA discovery fills a gap that no mainstream app addresses
- Sustainability and locavore trends are accelerating (farm-to-table movement)
- Partnership potential with local farms and farmers market associations
