# Senior Scam Shield

> Scam call/email/text identifier for elderly users with simplified UI, family alerts when scams are detected, and education about common fraud patterns.

| Monetization | Subscription — $5.99/mo (typically paid by adult children) |
| Recession Resistance | High — scam targeting of seniors increases during recessions; elder fraud is recession-proof |
| Solo-Dev MVP Timeline | 7 weeks |
| Composite Score | 30/100 |

## The Problem
Americans over 60 lost $3.4 billion to fraud in 2023 (FBI IC3 report), a 11% increase from the prior year. Seniors are disproportionately targeted because they're less familiar with digital scams, more trusting, and often socially isolated. The average elderly scam victim loses $35,101 (FTC). Common scams include fake IRS calls, tech support fraud, romance scams, Medicare scams, and grandparent scams ("Your grandson is in jail"). 70% of seniors don't report being scammed due to embarrassment. Adult children worry constantly but have no visibility into whether their parents are being targeted.

## The Solution
1. Scam call screening: identifies known scam numbers and flags suspicious callers before pickup
2. Text/SMS scam detection: scans incoming texts for phishing links and known scam patterns
3. Email scam filter: highlights suspicious emails with plain-English explanations ("This email pretending to be from the IRS is a scam because...")
4. Family alert system: notifies adult children when a scam attempt is detected on parent's phone
5. Large-text, simplified UI: designed for seniors with vision/dexterity challenges
6. Weekly scam briefing: "Here are the top 3 scams targeting people in your area this week"
7. "Ask before you send" protection: alerts family if parent attempts a wire transfer or gift card purchase
8. Scam education: short, friendly videos explaining common fraud tactics

## Why This Wins

- Senior-optimized UI is not an afterthought — it's the primary design consideration
- Multi-channel protection (calls + texts + email) in one app vs. fragmented tools
- "Ask before you send" financial transfer alerts prevent the most devastating scams
- Emotional purchase decision: adult children buy this out of love and worry
- Growing market: aging population + increasing sophistication of scams
