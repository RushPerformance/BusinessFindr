## The Problem
92% of people fail to keep their New Year's resolutions. The #1 reason: trying to change too much at once. BJ Fogg's research at Stanford shows that tiny habits anchored to existing routines are 4x more likely to stick than standalone goals. Yet most habit-tracking apps treat every habit as an isolated daily checkbox, creating guilt when streaks break (the average Habitica user quits after 2 weeks). "All-or-nothing" streak thinking causes people to abandon entire habit systems after one missed day — the "what-the-hell effect."

## The Solution
1. Habit stacking: attach new habits to existing anchors ("After I pour my morning coffee, I will journal for 2 minutes")
2. Micro-habit sizing: start absurdly small ("Do 1 push-up" not "Do 50 push-ups") and scale over time
3. "Never break twice" philosophy: one missed day doesn't break a streak — two in a row does
4. Visual chain view: see your habit stacks as interconnected routines, not isolated checkboxes
5. Flexible scheduling: some habits are daily, some are MWF, some are "3x per week whenever"
6. Scaling prompts: "You've done 1 push-up for 14 days. Ready to try 3?"
7. Anchor analysis: identifies your strongest existing routines to attach new habits to
8. Weekly reflection: "What went well? What was hard? Adjust your stack."

## Why This Wins
- "Never break twice" removes the #1 reason people quit habit apps (broken streaks)
- Habit stacking is scientifically superior to isolated habit tracking (BJ Fogg, Stanford)
- Micro-habit starting size removes the intimidation barrier that kills ambition
- Visual chain view makes routines feel connected and meaningful, not like a checklist
- Scaling prompts prevent stagnation while respecting the user's pace
- Lower price than premium competitors ($4/mo vs. $5-8/mo)
