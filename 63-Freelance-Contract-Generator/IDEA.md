## The Problem
59 million Americans freelance, and 71% have had trouble getting paid at least once (Freelancers Union). The average freelancer loses $6,000/year to scope creep, late payments, and project cancellations without kill fees. Most freelancers don't use contracts because legal templates are expensive ($150-500 from a lawyer), generic templates from the internet are dangerously inadequate, and writing your own is time-consuming and legally risky. Industry-specific terms matter: a web developer's contract needs different clauses than a photographer's or copywriter's.

## The Solution
1. Industry-specific templates: pre-built contracts for designers, developers, writers, photographers, videographers, consultants, and more
2. Scope definition wizard: guided questions to precisely define deliverables, timelines, and milestones
3. Payment terms builder: deposit requirements, milestone payments, net-30/net-15, late fees
4. Revision limits: "3 rounds of revisions included; additional revisions at $X/round"
5. Kill fee clause: "If project is cancelled after kickoff, client owes 25% of total"
6. IP transfer terms: who owns the work, licensing options, portfolio rights
7. E-signature integration: send and sign without leaving the app
8. Contract performance tracker: log deliverables completed, payments received, revisions used

## Existing Alternatives
- **HelloSign/DocuSign**: E-signature platforms, but no contract generation
- **LegalZoom**: General legal documents, expensive ($79-359 per document), not freelancer-focused
- **AND CO (Fiverr)**: Invoice and contract features, but acquired by Fiverr, limited free version
- **Bonsai**: Freelance management suite with contracts, $24/mo, overkill for contract-only needs
- **Google "freelance contract template"**: Generic, legally questionable, missing industry-specific terms

## Why This Wins
- Industry-specific templates are the differentiator: a photographer's contract has very different needs than a developer's
- Scope definition wizard prevents the vague scoping that causes 90% of freelancer disputes
- Kill fee and revision limit clauses address the two biggest freelancer pain points
- $5/mo is dramatically cheaper than Bonsai ($24/mo) or LegalZoom ($79+ per doc)
- One-time $19.99 option appeals to occasional freelancers who don't want subscriptions
- 59M addressable users and growing as the gig economy expands
