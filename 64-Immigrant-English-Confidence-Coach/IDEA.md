## The Problem
There are 44.8 million immigrants in the US, and over 25 million speak English "less than very well" (US Census). Non-native speakers with accents earn 15-20% less than native speakers in comparable roles (Journal of Labor Economics). The biggest barrier isn't vocabulary or grammar — it's pronunciation confidence, workplace idioms ("let's circle back," "low-hanging fruit"), and cultural communication norms (small talk, meeting participation). Traditional language apps (Duolingo) teach beginner-level phrases, not professional workplace English. ESL classes are expensive ($200-500 per course) and time-consuming. Many immigrants are embarrassed to practice in front of others.

## The Solution
1. Pronunciation practice: AI-powered speech recognition rates your pronunciation and provides specific feedback
2. Workplace scenario simulations: practice job interviews, meetings, presentations, phone calls
3. American idiom library: "What does 'let's touch base' mean?" with context and usage examples
4. Small talk training: weather, sports, weekend plans — the social glue of American workplaces
5. Accent reduction exercises: targeted practice for common pronunciation challenges by native language
6. Confidence tracking: measure improvement in fluency, pronunciation accuracy, and speaking speed over time
7. Industry-specific vocabulary: tech, healthcare, finance, retail, hospitality
8. Daily 5-minute practice: bite-sized exercises that fit into a commute or lunch break

## Existing Alternatives

- **ELSA Speak**: Pronunciation-focused AI, good but limited workplace context

- **In-person ESL classes**: $200-500 per course, fixed schedule, group settings cause embarrassment
- **Toastmasters**: Public speaking practice, not language-specific

## Why This Wins
- Workplace-specific focus fills a gap between "learn English" apps and actual professional needs
- Private practice eliminates the embarrassment factor that stops many immigrants from improving
- Industry-specific vocabulary makes this immediately practical (not theoretical)
- Idiom library addresses the #1 complaint of professional immigrants: "I understand every word but not the meaning"
- 5-minute daily practice format respects the busy schedules of working immigrants
- Cultural coaching (meeting etiquette, email tone) goes beyond language to career success
