## The Problem
74 million Americans live in HOA-governed communities, and HOA complaints are the #1 source of neighbor-related stress (CAI survey). HOAs issue over 10 million violation notices per year, and many are subjective, inconsistent, or outright wrong. Homeowners receive a violation letter, have 14-30 days to respond, and often don't know their rights. Fighting violations without documentation leads to fines ($25-200 per violation, escalating with repeated offenses) and even property liens. HOA boards frequently apply rules selectively — your neighbor's identical fence gets approved while yours gets violated. Without organized records, homeowners can't prove selective enforcement.

## The Solution
1. Violation log: record every violation notice with date, category, and photographed evidence
2. Evidence documentation: timestamped photos of your property's condition and neighboring properties
3. Selective enforcement tracker: document identical conditions on other properties to prove inconsistent enforcement
4. Appeal letter templates: state-specific templates referencing relevant HOA statutes and CC&R provisions
5. Deadline calendar: never miss a response deadline (missing one usually means the fine sticks)
6. CC&R reference: searchable copy of your community's rules with plain-English explanations
7. Fine tracker: total fines assessed, disputed, and resolved
8. Communication log: record all interactions with the HOA board with dates and outcomes
