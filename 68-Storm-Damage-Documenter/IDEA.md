## The Problem
$120 billion in property insurance claims are filed annually in the US, with wind/hail and water damage being the top causes. Homeowners who poorly document storm damage receive 30-50% lower insurance payouts (NAIC). After a storm, people are stressed, emotional, and snap random photos that don't tell the story an adjuster needs. Key evidence is missed: wide-angle shots showing the scope, close-ups of specific damage, damage relative to pre-existing conditions, water lines, timestamps proving the damage is storm-related. By the time an adjuster arrives (often 2-6 weeks later), temporary repairs have obscured evidence. The average denied or underpaid storm claim costs homeowners $10,000-30,000.

## The Solution
1. Guided photo walkthrough: step-by-step prompts to capture all required angles (wide, medium, close-up for each damage area)
2. Damage categorization: wind, hail, water, fallen tree, structural — tagged for insurance terminology
3. GPS and timestamp verification: proves where and when photos were taken (critical for claims)
4. Pre-existing condition documentation: photo your property before storm season to establish baseline
5. Adjuster-ready PDF report: organized by damage area with categorized, timestamped photos
6. Temporary repair documentation: photos of emergency repairs (tarps, board-up) to show damage was real
7. Insurance claim checklist: step-by-step guide for filing, including required forms and deadlines
8. Contractor estimate comparison: store multiple repair estimates for negotiation with adjusters

## Existing Alternatives
