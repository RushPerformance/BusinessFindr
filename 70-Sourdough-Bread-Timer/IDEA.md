## The Problem
Sourdough bread baking has exploded in popularity (Google searches up 400% since 2020), but it's notoriously finicky. The #1 failure point is fermentation timing, which varies dramatically with temperature — dough that needs 4 hours at 78°F needs 8+ hours at 65°F. Beginners (and even experienced bakers) over- or under-ferment constantly. Starter maintenance is another challenge: feeding schedules depend on temperature and flour type, and a neglected starter takes days to revive. Existing recipe apps treat sourdough like any other recipe, ignoring the critical time-temperature relationship.

## The Solution
1. Temperature-adjusted bulk fermentation timer: enter your dough and room temperature, get precise fermentation time
2. Starter feeding schedule: personalized reminders based on your feeding ratio, flour type, and kitchen temperature
3. Starter health log: track rise times, peak height, and smell to monitor starter vigor
4. Bake day timeline: "Start autolyse at 8am → Mix at 9am → Fold at 9:30, 10:00, 10:30, 11:00 → Shape at 2pm → Cold retard overnight → Bake at 7am tomorrow"
5. Stretch-and-fold reminders: timed notifications for each fold during bulk fermentation
6. Troubleshooting guide: "My bread is too dense" → check fermentation time, shaping, and scoring
7. Recipe library: beginner loaf, high-hydration, whole wheat, rye, and enriched doughs
8. Bake journal: log each bake with photos, notes, and adjustments for continuous improvement
