## The Problem
There are 180+ internationally standardized laundry care symbols, and almost nobody knows what they mean. A 2019 YouGov survey found that only 27% of Americans could correctly identify more than 3 care symbols. The result: shrunken sweaters, ruined delicates, faded colors, and clothes that wear out prematurely. The average American household spends $1,700/year on clothing (BLS), and improper care reduces garment lifespan by 30-50%. Stain removal is another pain point — Googling "how to remove red wine stain" returns conflicting advice, and wrong treatments can set stains permanently.

## The Solution
1. Camera scan: photograph any care label and get plain-English translation instantly
2. Symbol decoder: visual dictionary of all 180+ laundry symbols with explanations
3. Fabric-specific care guides: how to wash silk, cashmere, linen, denim, leather, etc.
4. Stain removal wizard: identify the stain type, get step-by-step removal instructions by fabric
5. Garment wardrobe: save items with their care instructions for quick reference
6. Sorting helper: "These 5 items can go together on cold delicate; these 3 need warm regular"
7. Temperature and cycle guide: what "warm" actually means on your specific washer model
8. Product recommendations: best detergents for delicates, stain removers, fabric shavers

## Existing Alternatives
- **Laundry Lens**: Basic symbol decoder, no camera scanning, outdated design
- **Care Label Project**: Website-only reference, not mobile-friendly
- **Google Image Search**: Can sometimes identify symbols but unreliable
- **Clothing brand websites**: Care instructions buried in product pages
- **Asking mom/grandma**: The most common actual solution for most people

## Why This Wins
- Camera scanning is 10x faster than manually looking up symbols one by one
- Stain removal wizard with fabric-specific instructions prevents the "wrong treatment" disasters
- Garment wardrobe saves instructions so you never need to check the label again
- Sorting helper solves the "can I wash these together?" question that causes most laundry mistakes
- Broad appeal: everyone does laundry, and everyone has ruined at least one garment
- Low price ($1.99/mo) for a tool that saves $500+ in clothing damage per year
