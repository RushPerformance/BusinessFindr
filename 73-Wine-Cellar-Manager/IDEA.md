> Track wine collection inventory, optimal drinking windows, tasting notes, and food pairings for casual collectors and enthusiasts.

## The Problem
The average wine collector owns 50-200 bottles, and serious collectors can hold 1,000+. Without proper tracking, collectors open wines too early (before peak maturity) or too late (past their prime), forget what they have (buying duplicates), and miss optimal food pairing opportunities. A bottle of 2015 Barolo that should be opened in 2025-2030 gets opened in 2021 — wasting years of aging and hundreds of dollars in unrealized value. Wine storage conditions (temperature, humidity) also affect quality but aren't monitored. Professional cellar management tools cost $200-500/year and are designed for commercial operations.

## The Solution
1. Barcode/label scanning: photograph a bottle to auto-populate wine details (varietal, vintage, region, producer)
2. Cellar inventory: organized by location, rack position, region, or varietal
3. Drinking window alerts: "Your 2018 Brunello is entering its optimal window (2026-2032) — perfect to open now"
4. Tasting notes journal: rate wines with standard descriptors (nose, palate, finish) and personal notes
5. Food pairing suggestions: "This Sancerre pairs beautifully with goat cheese, shellfish, or roasted chicken"
6. Purchase history and valuation: track what you paid vs. current market value
7. Wishlist and shopping alerts: "A 2019 Châteauneuf-du-Pape you wanted is on sale nearby"
8. Dinner party mode: "You're serving lamb — here are 5 bottles from your cellar that pair perfectly"

## Existing Alternatives
- **Vivino**: Wine rating and discovery platform, minimal cellar management features
- **CellarTracker**: The gold standard for serious collectors, but dated UI and steep learning curve
- **Delectable**: Wine scanning with expert ratings, acquired and neglected
- **Wine-Searcher**: Price comparison and retailer search, not a cellar tool
- **Spreadsheets/notes**: Common among casual collectors who don't want complex software

## Why This Wins
- Drinking window alerts prevent the most costly mistake collectors make (opening too early/late)
- Modern, mobile-first UI vs. CellarTracker's desktop-era design appeals to casual collectors
- $4.99/mo is accessible vs. CellarTracker's premium tier or professional tools ($200+/year)
- Dinner party mode creates a delightful, practical use case beyond inventory management
- Label scanning with auto-population removes tedious manual data entry
- Social potential: share tasting notes and cellar highlights with friends
