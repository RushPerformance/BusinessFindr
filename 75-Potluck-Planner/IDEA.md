# Potluck Planner

> Coordinates who's bringing what to potlucks and group meals, prevents duplicates, handles dietary restrictions, and sends reminders so nobody shows up empty-handed.

## The Problem
Every potluck has the same problems: 5 people bring dessert and nobody brings a main dish. Someone with a peanut allergy doesn't find out until they arrive. The host texts everyone individually, loses track of who confirmed, and has no idea if there's enough food. Group texts become chaotic and people miss updates. Americans attend an average of 6-10 potluck-style events per year (workplace lunches, family holidays, block parties, church suppers, school events). The host spends 2-3 hours coordinating over text/email for each event, and the result is still unbalanced.

## The Solution
1. Event creation: set date, time, location, and food categories needed (appetizers, mains, sides, desserts, drinks)
2. Sign-up board: guests claim what they're bringing from needed categories — preventing duplicates
3. Dietary restriction management: guests mark allergies and preferences; system flags conflicts
4. Ingredient labeling: contributors note allergens in their dish ("contains nuts, dairy-free")
5. Guest headcount with RSVP tracking and automatic reminders for non-responders
6. Quantity calculator: "For 24 guests, you need approximately: 3 mains, 4 sides, 2 appetizers, 2 desserts"
7. Serving gap alerts: "Nobody has signed up to bring a main dish yet — send a nudge?"
8. Post-event recipe sharing: guests can share recipes for dishes that were hits

## Existing Alternatives
- **SignUpGenius**: General sign-up sheets, works but not food-specific, no dietary management
- **PerfectPotluck**: Dedicated potluck tool, but dated web-only design, limited features
- **Evite**: Event invitations with basic potluck features, but sign-up tracking is clunky
- **Group texts**: The most common method, and the most chaotic
- **Shared Google Docs**: Functional but require manual setup and have no reminders

## Why This Wins
- Dietary restriction management is the underserved killer feature — no competitor handles this well
- Category-based sign-up prevents the "5 desserts, no main dish" problem automatically
- Quantity calculator takes the guesswork out of "is this enough food?"
- Dead simple UX: create event in 2 minutes, share link, guests sign up without downloading an app
- Low price ($1.99/mo) for event organizers who plan multiple gatherings per year
- Web-accessible for guests (no app download required) removes friction for attendees
