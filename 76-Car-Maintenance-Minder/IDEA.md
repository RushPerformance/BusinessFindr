## The Problem
There are 280 million registered vehicles in the US, and the average car is now 12.5 years old (S&P Global Mobility). The $300B US auto repair market thrives partly because drivers miss basic maintenance — 1 in 3 cars on the road has a neglected maintenance issue (Car Care Council). A skipped oil change can lead to a $4,000-8,000 engine replacement. A missed brake pad swap ($150-300) can destroy rotors, turning it into a $600-1,200 repair. Most people rely on memory, windshield sticker reminders, or dealer mailers — none of which track holistic vehicle health across all systems. The problem multiplies for multi-car households.

## The Solution
1. Vehicle profile setup with year, make, model, and current mileage
2. Manufacturer-recommended maintenance schedule auto-populated for your specific vehicle
3. Mileage-based and time-based reminders for oil changes, tire rotations, brake inspections, fluid flushes, filter replacements, and belt checks
4. Service history log with date, mileage, cost, mechanic name, and receipt photo
5. Mechanic finder with ratings, specialties, and price ranges for common services in your area
6. Cost tracking dashboard showing total maintenance spend per vehicle per year
7. Resale value protector: exportable service history PDF for when you sell the car

## Existing Alternatives
- **Drivvo**: Fuel and maintenance tracker, but cluttered interface and weak reminder system
- **Carfax Car Care**: Service history tracking, but heavily tied to Carfax reports and dealer upselling
- **Simply Auto**: Comprehensive but overly complex for casual car owners
- **Dealer service departments**: Send reminders but only for their own services, not holistic vehicle health
- **Windshield stickers / memory**: The most common "system," and it fails constantly

## Why This Wins
- Mileage-based reminders are more accurate than time-based — people drive different amounts
- Full service history in one place increases resale value (documented maintenance adds 10-15% to sale price)
- Multi-vehicle support for families with 2-4 cars is a gap in most competitors
- Mechanic finder adds utility beyond just tracking — it helps you act on reminders
- Simple, focused UX vs. competitors that try to be fuel trackers, trip loggers, and insurance managers simultaneously
