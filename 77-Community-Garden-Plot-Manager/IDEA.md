## The Problem
There are over 29,000 community gardens in the US (American Community Gardening Association), most managed with spreadsheets, paper sign-up sheets, and group text messages. Garden organizers spend 5-10 hours per week on administrative tasks: assigning plots, managing waitlists (some gardens have 2-3 year waitlists), coordinating watering schedules, resolving disputes over plot boundaries, and tracking who has paid dues. The $890M community garden market is growing 10-15% annually as urban farming interest surges. 35% of US households now participate in some form of food gardening (National Gardening Association), but community gardens lose 20-30% of participants each season due to organizational chaos.

## The Solution
1. Visual plot map with drag-and-drop assignment, showing occupied, available, and waitlisted plots
2. Waitlist management with automatic notifications when plots become available
3. Watering schedule coordinator: assigns watering duties by plot or rotation with reminders
4. Plant spacing calculator based on plot dimensions and chosen crops
5. Harvest log where members record yields (weight, variety, date) for community reporting
6. Dues and fee tracking with payment reminders and receipt generation
7. Community bulletin board for sharing tips, surplus produce, and garden events
8. Seasonal planning calendar with planting and harvest windows by zone

## Existing Alternatives
- **Google Sheets + email**: The default, but no visual plot maps, no automated reminders, and no harvest tracking
- **Manage My Plot**: Basic plot tracking but limited features and outdated interface
- **OurGarden**: Community garden management but focused on large municipal programs
- **Facebook Groups**: Common for communication but terrible for plot management and scheduling
- **Paper sign-up sheets**: Still used by most gardens, easily lost, hard to share

## Why This Wins
- Visual plot map is the killer feature — organizers can see the entire garden at a glance
- Watering schedule automation solves the biggest source of conflict in community gardens
- Harvest logging provides data for grant applications (many gardens depend on grants)
- Per-garden pricing aligns with how community gardens budget — it's a garden expense, not individual
- Reduces organizer burnout by automating the 5-10 hours/week of admin work
