## The Problem
An estimated 24% of US adults have tried intermittent fasting (International Food Information Council, 2023), making it one of the most popular dietary approaches. The $4.8B health and diet app market continues to grow, yet most fasting practitioners struggle with consistency. The biggest pain points: losing track of fasting windows (especially with shifting schedules), not knowing what to eat when breaking a fast (eating the wrong foods causes digestive distress for 40% of fasters), and lacking visibility into long-term progress. Generic health apps treat fasting as a secondary feature. Most people use the phone clock or set alarms — a system that breaks down when schedules shift.

## The Solution
1. Visual fasting timer with circular countdown showing fasting vs. eating windows (16:8, 18:6, 20:4, OMAD, 5:2)
2. Flexible scheduling: adjust today's eating window without losing your streak
3. Fast-breaking meal suggestions based on fast duration and dietary preferences (gentle on the stomach after long fasts)
4. Progress dashboard: streaks, total fasting hours, weight trend (optional), and energy level logging
5. Smart notifications at window open/close with customizable lead times
6. Weekly/monthly reports showing fasting consistency and patterns
7. Social accountability: optional fasting buddy system for motivation

## Why This Wins
- Clean, focused timer UX — does one thing well vs. competitors that bolt on meal plans, coaching, and content
- Fast-breaking meal suggestions address a real gap (what to eat matters as much as when)
- Flexible schedule adjustments preserve streaks instead of penalizing real-life variability
- Lower price point ($3.99/mo vs. $5-15/mo for competitors) captures price-sensitive health consumers
- No guilt-tripping or weight-loss pressure — positioned as a lifestyle tool, not a diet
