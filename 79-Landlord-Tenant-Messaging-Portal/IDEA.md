## The Problem
There are 48.2 million rental units in the US, and landlord-tenant disputes are among the most common civil court cases — over 3.6 million eviction filings per year (Eviction Lab, Princeton). In court, the outcome often depends on documentation: who said what, when, and whether the other party received the message. Most landlord-tenant communication happens via text messages, phone calls, and verbal conversations — none of which create reliable legal records. 62% of small landlords (1-4 units) have no formal communication system (National Apartment Association). The $22B property management software market serves large operators, leaving 10.6 million small landlords using personal phones.

## The Solution
1. Dedicated messaging portal with automatic timestamps and read receipts on every message
2. Maintenance request system with photo attachments, priority levels, and resolution tracking
3. Legal notice templates: rent increase notices, lease violation warnings, move-out instructions — pre-formatted to local requirements
4. Document sharing: leases, inspection reports, and receipts stored in a shared, immutable timeline
5. Export-ready communication log: PDF export of entire message history for court, mediation, or insurance claims
6. Rent payment reminders with confirmation receipts (not payment processing — just documentation)
7. Move-in/move-out inspection checklists with timestamped photos

## Existing Alternatives
- **AppFolio / Buildium**: Full property management suites, but designed for large operators (50+ units), expensive ($1-3/unit/mo minimum with onboarding fees)
- **TenantCloud**: Free tier available, but communication is a secondary feature to accounting
- **Text messages / phone calls**: The default, but create no verifiable legal record
- **Email**: Better than texting but easily disputed in court ("I never received that email")
- **Certified mail**: Legally strong but slow, expensive ($7-9 per letter), and impractical for routine communication

## Why This Wins
- Legal documentation is the core value prop — not property management, not accounting, just provable communication
- Read receipts solve the "I never got that notice" problem that dominates landlord-tenant court cases
- Per-unit pricing works for small landlords who only need it for 1-4 properties
- Both parties benefit: landlords get documentation, tenants get a formal channel with proof of requests
- Export-ready PDFs are immediately useful in court, mediation, or insurance situations
