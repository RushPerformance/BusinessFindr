## The Problem
Americans spend an average of 17 hours per year searching for parking (INRIX, 2023), and a significant chunk of that is spent wandering parking garages trying to remember which floor and section they parked on. The $4.2B parking market includes 44,000+ parking structures in the US. A survey by NerdWallet found that 63% of drivers have spent more than 10 minutes searching for their car in a parking garage at least once. The problem is especially acute at airports, hospitals, shopping malls, and event venues where garages have 5-10 levels. Beyond the frustration, paid parking meters and garages result in $2.3B in parking tickets annually — many because people lost track of time.

## The Solution
1. One-tap "I parked here" button that saves GPS location, floor/level, and zone/section
2. Photo marker: snap a picture of nearby landmarks (pillar numbers, signs, painted zones)
3. Parking timer with notifications for metered spots and garage time limits
4. Walking navigation back to your car using saved location and floor
5. Parking history log showing where you parked previously (useful for regular garages)
6. Meter top-up reminders with "time remaining" countdown widget

## Existing Alternatives
- **Apple Maps / Google Maps**: "Save parking" feature exists but doesn't handle floors/levels and has no timer
- **SpotHero / ParkMobile**: Focus on finding and paying for parking, not remembering where you parked
- **Taking a photo**: Common workaround but photos pile up and you have to scroll to find the right one
- **Dropping a pin in Maps**: Only works for outdoor lots, useless in multi-level garages
- **Memory**: The most common approach and the most unreliable

## Why This Wins
- Solves a specific, universal frustration that everyone has experienced
- One-tap simplicity is the key — must be faster than taking a photo
- Floor/level support is the differentiator that maps apps lack entirely
- Parking timer prevents tickets, adding real financial value to a free app
- Tiny app with viral potential — people share it when they see someone wandering a garage
