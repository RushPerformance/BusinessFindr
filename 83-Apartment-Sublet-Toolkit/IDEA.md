## The Problem
An estimated 11% of renters have sublet their apartment at some point (Apartment List), and the need spikes during job relocations, study abroad programs, extended travel, and financial hardship. The $4.5B US subletting and short-term rental market is largely unstructured. Subletting wrong can lead to eviction, lease termination, or liability for damages — yet most subletters have no idea what their lease actually allows. 44 states allow subletting with landlord consent, but the process (getting permission, drafting a sublet agreement, pricing correctly, finding a subtenant) is confusing and legally risky. Renters typically Google around, copy a template from a random website, and hope for the best.

## The Solution
1. Lease analyzer: upload your lease to identify subletting clauses, restrictions, and required notice periods
2. Landlord permission letter generator: customizable template requesting subletting approval
3. Sublet agreement builder: legally structured sublet contract covering rent, duration, damage liability, and rules
4. Pricing calculator: suggests sublet price based on market rents, your lease rate, season, and furnishing
5. Listing creator: generates a subletting listing with photos, description, and terms for Craigslist, Facebook, and other platforms
6. Subtenant screening checklist: what to verify before handing over your keys
7. Move-in/move-out documentation templates with photo checklists

## Existing Alternatives
- **LegalZoom**: Generic sublease templates ($39-79), not customized to your lease or local laws
- **Rocket Lawyer**: Legal document builder but subletting is a small niche within their platform
- **Google "free sublet agreement"**: Low-quality templates that miss critical clauses and vary wildly in quality
- **Landlord's property management company**: Sometimes provides subletting forms but often discourages subletting
- **Real estate attorney**: Gold standard but costs $200-500 for a single sublet agreement

## Why This Wins
- End-to-end subletting workflow in one tool — from lease review to listing to legal agreement
- Pricing calculator prevents leaving money on the table (or pricing too high and finding no subtenant)
- Much cheaper than a lawyer ($14.99 vs. $200-500) while more reliable than free Google templates
- One-time pricing matches the use case — you only sublet occasionally, not monthly
- Subtenant screening checklist protects the original tenant from nightmare subletters
