## The Problem
Noise is the #1 complaint among apartment dwellers — 67% of renters report being disturbed by neighbor noise (National Apartment Association). The $1.2B acoustic products market serves mostly commercial and studio clients, leaving residential renters to figure out soundproofing on their own. Renters face unique constraints: they can't modify walls, install permanent fixtures, or make structural changes. Most "soundproofing" advice online is generic, often wrong (egg cartons don't work), and doesn't distinguish between renter-friendly and owner-only solutions. A renter dealing with bass from downstairs needs completely different solutions than someone hearing conversations through a shared wall.

## The Solution
1. Noise assessment quiz: identify the type of noise (impact, airborne, bass), source direction (above, below, shared wall), and severity
2. Room-by-room recommendations tailored to your specific noise problem and budget ($50, $200, $500+ tiers)
3. Renter vs. owner filter: only show solutions you can actually implement without violating your lease
4. Product recommendations with affiliate links to Amazon, Home Depot, and specialty acoustic retailers
5. DIY installation guides with step-by-step instructions and video references
6. Before/after noise reduction estimates: "This solution typically reduces perceived noise by 40-60%"
7. Noise documentation tool: record noise levels (dB meter) for landlord complaints or legal action

## Existing Alternatives
- **Google / YouTube**: Massive amounts of conflicting advice, hard to filter for renter-specific solutions
- **Reddit r/soundproofing**: Community advice but inconsistent quality and no personalization
- **Acoustic consultants**: Professional assessments cost $200-500, overkill for apartment noise
- **Soundproofing product companies**: Sell products but don't diagnose problems or guide purchases
- **White noise machines**: A band-aid, not a solution — masks noise without reducing it
