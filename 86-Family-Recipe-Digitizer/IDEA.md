## The Problem
An estimated 89% of Americans have family recipes passed down through generations, many on handwritten recipe cards, in grandmother's handwriting, or scrawled in cookbook margins (Food Network survey). These physical recipes are irreplaceable — and they're being lost. Water damage, moves, estate cleanings, and simple deterioration destroy family recipe collections every day. The $6.3B meal planning and recipe app market is dominated by discovery platforms (finding new recipes), not preservation tools. Digitizing a recipe manually is tedious: retyping ingredients, figuring out abbreviations ("t" vs. "T" for teaspoon vs. tablespoon), and interpreting handwriting. Most families have 20-50 treasured recipes with no digital backup.

## The Solution
1. Camera scan with OCR: photograph a handwritten recipe card and auto-extract ingredients and instructions
2. Handwriting recognition tuned for recipe terminology (tsp, tbsp, c., oz., pinch, dash)
3. Serving size adjuster: scale any recipe from 4 servings to 12 (or down to 2) with automatic ingredient recalculation
4. Measurement converter: toggle between US/metric, volume/weight, and common substitutions
5. Original photo preservation: keep the scanned image of the handwritten card alongside the digital version
6. Family cookbook builder: organize recipes by category, contributor, or occasion and export as a printable PDF
7. Sharing: send individual recipes or entire collections to family members
8. Cooking mode: step-by-step instructions with large text and voice-read-aloud for hands-free cooking

## Existing Alternatives
- **Paprika Recipe Manager**: Excellent recipe organizer but designed for web clipping, not handwritten card scanning
- **CookBook**: Recipe storage app but no OCR scanning capability
- **Google Lens / Apple Live Text**: Can extract text from photos but doesn't understand recipe structure or terminology
- **Typing recipes into Notes/Word**: The manual approach — works but takes 10-15 minutes per recipe
- **Professional digitization services**: Exist for photos but not specifically for recipe cards

## Why This Wins
- Handwriting OCR tuned for recipes is the killer feature — general OCR misreads recipe abbreviations constantly
- Emotional value is enormous: preserving grandmother's handwriting alongside the digital version
- Serving size adjustment solves a real cooking pain point that handwritten recipes never addressed
- Family cookbook PDF export creates a tangible, giftable product from digital data
- Lifetime pricing option aligns with the preservation use case — digitize once, keep forever
