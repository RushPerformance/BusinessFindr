## The Problem
Over 36 million Americans park on the street daily in cities where parking permits, street cleaning schedules, and alternate side parking rules create a complex web of regulations. The $4.2B parking market includes an estimated $2.3 billion in parking tickets issued annually in the US. In New York City alone, drivers pay $545 million per year in parking fines. Street cleaning violations ($65-115 per ticket), expired permit fines ($50-200), and alternate side parking violations cost urban drivers an average of $200-400 per year. Most cities have different street cleaning days for different sides of the street, permit renewal deadlines that vary by zone, and seasonal parking bans — all tracked via memory, paper permits, and hard-to-read street signs.

## The Solution
1. Permit renewal tracker: enter your permits (residential, visitor, overnight) with expiration dates and get reminders 30, 14, and 7 days before renewal
2. Street cleaning calendar: input your block's cleaning schedule or auto-detect from city data with move-your-car reminders
3. Alternate side parking rules: daily notifications telling you which side of the street is restricted
4. Suspension alerts: many cities suspend alternate side rules for holidays and snow — get notified when rules are lifted
5. Multi-vehicle support for households with more than one street-parked car
6. Ticket cost calculator: see how much you've saved by avoiding tickets this year

## Existing Alternatives
- **NYC 311 / city apps**: Some cities have apps with street cleaning data but they're buggy, slow, and don't send proactive reminders
- **SpotAngels**: Crowdsourced parking data with some street cleaning info, but unreliable and cluttered with ads
- **Phone calendar reminders**: Manual setup, breaks when schedules change, and doesn't handle suspensions
- **Street signs**: The "official" system, but signs are confusing, contradictory, and easy to misread
- **Neighbor word-of-mouth**: Common but unreliable — "I think they clean this side on Thursdays?"

## Why This Wins
- Proactive reminders are the core value — preventing even one $65-115 ticket pays for a full year of the app
- Suspension alerts are a unique feature that no competitor handles well
- Clear ROI: $1.99/mo prevents $200-400/year in tickets — the app pays for itself immediately
- Hyper-local data makes it defensible — once you have accurate schedules for a neighborhood, users stay
- Multi-vehicle support addresses the household use case that calendar reminders can't handle
