> Manages book selection voting, reading schedules, discussion questions, and meeting coordination for book clubs.

## The Problem
An estimated 5 million Americans participate in book clubs (Pew Research Center), and the number has grown steadily since the pandemic. Yet book club management is almost entirely manual — group text chains, Doodle polls, Google Docs for book suggestions, and email threads for discussion questions. The $1.2B book discovery and reading community market lacks a purpose-built tool for book clubs. The biggest pain points: choosing the next book (endless suggestions with no structured voting), keeping everyone on pace (no shared reading schedule), and generating meaningful discussion (most clubs devolve into "I liked it" or "I didn't"). Book club organizers spend 1-2 hours per meeting on logistics that could be automated.

## The Solution
1. Book nomination and voting: members suggest books, add cover art and descriptions, and vote with ranked-choice or simple majority
2. Reading schedule: break the book into weekly reading targets with chapter milestones and progress tracking
3. Discussion question generator: AI-assisted discussion questions based on the current book, plus member-submitted questions
4. Meeting scheduler: polls for date/time, location (in-person or virtual), and RSVP tracking
5. Book history: archive of every book the club has read, with member ratings and discussion notes
6. Reading pace tracker: see who's keeping up (opt-in, no shaming) to avoid spoilers
7. Theme nights and genre rotation management
8. Library availability checker: see if the selected book is available at local libraries

## Existing Alternatives
- **Goodreads**: Book tracking and social features but no book club management tools
- **Bookclubs.com**: The most direct competitor, but focused on virtual meeting hosting, not full club management
- **GroupMe / WhatsApp**: Common for club communication but no voting, scheduling, or book-specific features
- **Doodle + Google Docs + email**: The Frankenstein approach most clubs use — fragmented and tedious
- **Fable**: Social reading app with some club features, but pivoting toward publishing partnerships

## Why This Wins
- Ranked-choice book voting solves the single biggest book club friction point
- Discussion question generator elevates the quality of meetings beyond "did you like it?"
- Reading pace tracker with spoiler protection addresses a real social dynamic in clubs
- Free tier for basic features ensures the whole club can join, with premium for organizers
- Library availability checker saves money and removes the "I can't afford this book" barrier
