## The Problem
The US secondhand market is worth $227 billion (ThredUp, 2023) and growing 3x faster than the overall retail market. 52% of consumers shopped secondhand in the past year. But thrift store shopping is fundamentally inefficient — inventory is random, unpredictable, and unindexed. Shoppers make repeated trips hoping to find specific items (a mid-century dresser, a Le Creuset pot, size 10 men's leather boots) and leave empty-handed most of the time. Goodwill alone processes 80 million donated items per year across 3,300 stores, but there's no way to know what's on the shelf without physically visiting. Resellers spend 15-20 hours per week browsing stores because there's no inventory visibility.

## The Solution
1. Wish list builder: describe items you're looking for with category, brand, size, color, and price range
2. Crowdsourced inventory updates: users photograph and tag interesting finds at local thrift stores
3. Match alerts: push notifications when a crowdsourced post matches your wish list
4. Thrift store map: nearby stores with hours, specialty focus, pricing reputation, and best days to shop
5. Find-it-first alerts: premium feature showing matches within minutes of being posted
6. Price check: compare thrift store prices to retail and online secondhand marketplaces
7. Contribution rewards: earn credits by posting inventory updates that match other users' wish lists
8. "Just dropped" feed: newest crowdsourced finds in your area, filterable by category

## Existing Alternatives
- **ThredUp / Poshmark / Mercari**: Online secondhand marketplaces, but don't cover in-store thrift inventory
- **Goodwill's website / shopgoodwill.com**: Online auction for select items, but vast majority of inventory is in-store only
- **Facebook Marketplace**: Peer-to-peer, not thrift store inventory
- **Thrift store social media**: Some stores post on Instagram but inconsistently and not searchable
- **Physically visiting stores**: The current default — time-consuming and hit-or-miss

## Why This Wins
- Crowdsourced inventory solves the fundamental problem of thrift store opacity
- Wish list matching turns passive browsing into targeted shopping
- Contribution reward system creates a flywheel: more posters = more matches = more subscribers
- Reseller market is a built-in power user segment willing to pay for early alerts
- Recession-proof positioning: the worse the economy, the more people thrift shop
