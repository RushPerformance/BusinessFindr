## The Problem
The US power tool market is worth $28 billion, with 85 million households owning at least one power tool (Statista). Power tools require regular maintenance — blade sharpening and replacement, oil changes (chain saws), brush replacement (motors), belt replacement, filter cleaning — but most owners neglect maintenance until tools fail or become dangerous. A dull table saw blade increases kickback risk by 300% (Woodworking Safety Council). A chainsaw with old bar oil can seize, requiring a $200-500 repair. Tool owners typically have 5-15 power tools across different brands, each with different maintenance intervals and requirements. Owner's manuals get lost, and maintenance schedules are buried in 50-page PDFs.

## The Solution
1. Tool inventory: add tools with brand, model, purchase date, and serial number
2. Manufacturer-specific maintenance schedules auto-populated from a database of common tools
3. Usage-based tracking: log hours of use to trigger maintenance reminders (blade change every 20 hours, oil every 10 hours)
4. Maintenance log: record completed maintenance with date, parts used, and cost
5. Replacement part finder: direct links to blades, filters, belts, and oils for your specific model
6. Safety alerts: notifications for critical maintenance items that affect safety (blade sharpness, guard condition)
7. Tool warranty tracker: know when your warranty expires and what's covered
8. Maintenance cost dashboard: total spend per tool per year to inform repair-vs-replace decisions

## Existing Alternatives
- **Owner's manuals**: Comprehensive but get lost, and nobody reads 50-page PDFs
- **Spreadsheets**: Flexible but require manual research on maintenance intervals for each tool
- **Tool manufacturer apps**: Some brands (DeWalt, Milwaukee) have apps but only for their own tools
- **YouTube maintenance videos**: Great for how-to but don't track your specific tools or send reminders
- **Memory**: The default approach — "When did I last change that blade?"

## Why This Wins
- Usage-based tracking is more accurate than time-based — a weekend hobbyist and a daily contractor have different needs
- Multi-brand support in one app vs. manufacturer apps that only cover their own tools
- Safety alerts differentiate this from simple tracking — preventing injuries is a powerful motivator
- Replacement part finder generates affiliate revenue and saves users time searching
- Woodworking and DIY communities are passionate and share tools enthusiastically
