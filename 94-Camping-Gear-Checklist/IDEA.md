## The Problem
The US camping market is worth $23 billion, with 57 million Americans going camping at least once a year (KOA, 2023). The #1 frustration among campers is forgetting essential gear — 78% of campers report having forgotten at least one critical item on a trip (REI survey). Forgetting a tent stake means a collapsing shelter. Forgetting a water filter on a backcountry trip means a dangerous situation. The problem compounds with group trips where coordination is needed ("I thought YOU were bringing the stove"). Static checklists from Google don't account for trip type (car camping vs. backpacking), weather conditions, altitude, group size, or the fact that you might already own some gear but not other items.

## The Solution
1. Trip profile setup: trip type (car camping, backpacking, glamping), duration, group size, location, and expected weather
2. Personalized packing list generated based on trip parameters, with items categorized by priority (essential, recommended, optional)
3. Weather integration: pulls forecast data and adds/removes items accordingly (rain gear, cold weather layers, sun protection)
4. Group coordination: shared lists where members claim items they're bringing to avoid duplicates
5. Gear inventory: track what you own with purchase date, condition, and weight
6. Pack weight calculator: total weight estimate for backpacking trips with suggestions to cut weight
7. "Don't forget" alerts the day before departure with unchecked essential items
8. Post-trip review: note damaged/consumed items that need replacing before the next trip

## Existing Alternatives
- **REI camping checklists**: Free PDFs but static, not personalized, and no gear tracking
- **PackPoint**: Travel packing app but designed for air travel, not camping-specific
- **AllTrails**: Trail finding and hiking but no packing list or gear management
- **Pinterest checklists**: Popular but generic, no weather integration or group coordination
- **Paper lists / Notes app**: The manual approach — prone to error and no personalization

## Why This Wins
- Weather-integrated, trip-specific lists are genuinely more useful than static checklists
- Group coordination prevents the "I thought you were bringing it" disaster
- Gear inventory with condition tracking helps plan purchases and replacements
- Pack weight calculator is a high-value feature for backpackers trying to reduce load
- Simple one-time purchase model matches the occasional-use pattern of most campers
