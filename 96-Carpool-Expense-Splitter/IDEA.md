## The Problem
An estimated 9.4% of US commuters carpool (US Census Bureau), representing roughly 14 million workers. Carpooling saves each participant $2,000-6,000 per year in gas, tolls, parking, and vehicle wear (AAA). But splitting costs fairly is a persistent source of friction. Drivers absorb gas, tolls, parking, and vehicle depreciation while passengers benefit without a clear system for contribution. Current splits are usually informal — "just give me $20 a week" — which leads to resentment when gas prices change, routes vary, or one person drives more often. 35% of carpools dissolve within 6 months due to perceived unfairness in cost sharing (Commute.org survey).

## The Solution
1. Carpool group setup with members, regular route, and vehicle details (MPG, fuel type)
2. Automatic fair-split calculator based on actual gas prices, distance driven, tolls, and parking costs
3. Driver rotation scheduler with calendar integration and swap requests
4. Trip logging: record each carpool trip with driver, passengers, route, and associated costs
5. Settlement tracker: running balance of who owes whom with periodic settlement reminders
6. Gas price integration: automatically adjusts cost splits when gas prices change
7. Monthly summary: total savings vs. driving solo, cost per person, and fairness report
8. Venmo/PayPal settlement links for easy payment

## Existing Alternatives
- **Splitwise**: General expense splitting but not designed for recurring carpool-specific calculations
- **Waze Carpool**: Focused on matching strangers, not managing costs within existing carpool groups
- **Scoop / Commute with Enterprise**: Corporate carpool matching, not expense management
- **Venmo / Cash App**: Payment tools but don't calculate fair splits or track rotation
- **Spreadsheets**: Can track costs but tedious to maintain and no gas price integration

## Why This Wins
- Automatic fair-split calculation removes the awkwardness of negotiating contributions
- Gas price integration ensures splits stay fair as fuel costs fluctuate
- Driver rotation scheduler prevents the "I always drive on Fridays" resentment
- "Savings vs. solo driving" report reinforces the value of carpooling and reduces dropout
- Solves the #1 reason carpools fall apart — perceived unfairness in cost sharing
