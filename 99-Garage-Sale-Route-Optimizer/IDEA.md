# Garage Sale Route Optimizer

> Maps nearby garage sales from online listings and creates an optimized driving route with start times and category filters.

## The Problem
The US secondhand market is worth $227 billion (ThredUp), and garage/yard sales remain one of the largest segments — an estimated 165,000 garage sales happen every week during peak season (YardSaleSearch). Dedicated garage sale shoppers drive to 5-10 sales per Saturday morning, but planning routes is chaotic. Sales are listed across Craigslist, Facebook Marketplace, EstateSales.net, and community bulletin boards — no single source aggregates them. Shoppers waste 30-60 minutes each Saturday morning driving inefficient routes, arriving after the best items are gone, or visiting sales that don't have what they want. The difference between arriving at 7:00 AM vs. 8:30 AM at a popular sale can mean missing the best finds entirely.

## The Solution
1. Aggregated garage sale map: pulls listings from Craigslist, Facebook Marketplace, EstateSales.net, and local sources into one map
2. Route optimizer: creates the most efficient driving route through selected sales based on start times and proximity
3. Category filters: show only sales listing furniture, electronics, baby items, tools, clothing, etc.
4. Start time sorting: prioritize sales by opening time to hit early-bird deals first
5. Favorites and alerts: save searches for specific items and get notified when new sales list matching categories
6. Community ratings: users rate sales after visiting (quality, pricing, worth the trip)
7. Estimated time at each stop with total route duration and return-home time
8. Historical hot zones: neighborhoods with the most frequent and highest-rated sales

## Existing Alternatives
- **Yard Sale Treasure Map**: The closest competitor, but outdated interface and limited listing sources
- **EstateSales.net**: Estate sale focused, not garage sales, and no route optimization
- **Craigslist garage sale section**: Listings but no map view, no route planning, no category filtering
- **Facebook Marketplace**: Individual item listings, not organized as garage sale events
- **Google Maps manual route planning**: Can plan routes but requires manually entering each address

## Why This Wins
- Route optimization is the key differentiator — saving 30-60 minutes of driving each Saturday is tangible value
- Multi-source aggregation solves the biggest frustration (checking 3-4 websites every Friday night)
- Start time sorting ensures you hit the best sales first when the best items are available
- Community ratings prevent wasted trips to low-quality sales
- Seasonal pricing ($1.99/mo) matches the use pattern — most users only need it April through October
